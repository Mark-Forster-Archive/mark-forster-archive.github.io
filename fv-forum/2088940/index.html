Two-list variant

  • I find this works really well for me. It keeps my FV list spritely, relevant, and under 20 items: I follow the rules of FV, with the following difference:

    When I finish a task, I consider whether I can delete it, put it away, or should get back to it soon. If my answer is to put it away (because I mustn't forget it, but there's no call in the near future to address it), I move the task to list 2.

    One of the tasks on my main list is to review list 2. When I review it, I grab any tasks that need doing soon and bring them back to the FV list. I delete any tasks that aren't needed. I merge any duplicates.

    The only other thing I do sometimes is group things into projects tracked elsewhere. There are no rules about such.

  • Hi Cricket -- I'm not sure whom you are asking, but here is what I do. I scan the Evernote notes from time to time to review what's in there. I usually do this when I am sitting and waiting for something - waiting in the car to pick up my son after baseball practice, or waiting for my next meeting at work to start, or something like that. If I notice something I really need to do that I have forgotten about, then I might copy it from Evernote and email it to myself. Or maybe I'll just type into the note, "do this!" (causing the note to float to the top of my Evernote list).

    But usually I'll handle things like that in the regular course of things. I have my volunteer work on my mind pretty often, and in the evenings when everyone is in bed I'll put "volunteer work" on SMEMA and look through all my papers and emails and meeting notes and just deal with anything that needs to be done. Or if some particular task is on my mind then that will go on SMEMA. During working hours, I'll be thinking about my work projects and handle them in the same way.

    With DIT, I would just write everything in DIT. If I had meeting notes to review, I'd write "review meeting notes" and I'd get to it eventually, if not the next day.

    I felt more methodical when using DIT, but feel more engaged using SMEMA.
