Cons: "Schedule everything."

  • This is intended to be a list and discussion of all the drawbacks to scheduling blocks of time (self-appointments) for all of your tasks instead of working from a list at your discretion.
    16 June 2015 at 1:06 | Michael B.
  • The primary con to scheduling everything down to the minutia is that you will be unable to stick to it and will end up spending inordinate amounts of time repeating the scheduling process. It is, however, very effective to schedule a small number of high-impact activities a week.
    16 June 2015 at 1:13 | Austin
  • Two, you lack the flexibility to deal with people dropping by your desk or your manager giving you a short-tern assignment.

    Three, you are stifled from getting into the flow working on something that grabs your attention.

    Four, you cannot respond quickly to daily changes in priorities.

    For certain work environments, these drawbacks might be acceptable. For engineers that work in teams in a complex factory environment with many short-term crises, they are not acceptable.
