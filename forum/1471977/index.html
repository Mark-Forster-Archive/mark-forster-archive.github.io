Name: Super vs. Auto

  • While reading about SuperFocus (at the OmniFocus forum, http://forums.omnigroup.com/showthread.php?t=20836), it struck me that "SuperFocus" was the natural name for the next level of "AutoFocus," but that "AutoFocus" actually is a more descriptive title.

    It seems the great strength of the systems is the automatic nature of task evaluation as you go through the list.

    Perhaps a thought to consider from a marketing standpoint. Maybe Super Autofocus?
    25 April 2011 at 14:55 | DS
  • From a marketing standpoint, Mark would need to consider other product names that are already out there, and how to distinguish his system.

    Use of the term "Focus" will remind some of the FranklinCovey program: << The company markets a variety of book and audio products based on Covey's 7 Habits material, as well as numerous titles by other FranklinCovey consultants. The company also offers two main training courses: one based on _The Seven Habits of Highly Effective People_; and another based on the original FranklinQuest time management program, now titled Focus. >> (from http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FranklinCovey)

    "AutoFocus" is descriptive, but I think it would cause confusion in the marketplace, as it is a feature built into camera lenses, as well as a class of algorithms for optimizing parameters to sharpen all kinds of imagery (e.g. radar).

    I like "SuperFocus" the best. It is descriptive, as well as competitive. Imagine a stationery store with a display of the FC Focus planners and 7 Habits books, next to a display of the MF SuperFocus notebooks and associated guides. Why would you choose Focus when you could buy SuperFocus instead? :-)
