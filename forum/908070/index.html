Stop tweaking and start doing

  • This post is an instruction to myself. I have this worry that things will get lost and disorganized, and so I'm studying the system and how to make sure this doesn't happen. This is taking too long, so here's my solution:

    Add to my list the following tasks:
    1. Tweak
    2. Scan areas I think I might be forgetting something.

    Now following the little and often process, I can forget about getting it perfect, and be confident it will get work. If it doesn't work, the Tweak task is there to fix it. Now with any luck eventually Tweak will get ignored because I can't think of a specific problem to fix. Tweak will migrate to the backlog and get dismissed because practically speaking there won't be any more problem.

    Thoughts?

  • Yes, organizing is overhead.

    But there is still that difficult matter about whether or not you can trust your system. If you can't rely on your system (or you DON'T KNOW whether you can rely on your system), there is always something in the back of your mind, nagging you.

    In my experience, that's what drives the tweaking. You keep finding edge cases that the system can't seem to handle. So you create a tweak to address that edge case.

    Pretty soon you lose track of the tweaks and have a monster system that requires 24-hr care and feeding.

    Another approach (which I learned from doing AF and from watching Mark), is to follow the given system for a time, until you learn to trust it. During this initial trial phase, you can use a safety net, like using reminders OUTSIDE the system that address all your edge-case worries. The idea of the safety net is that it is temporary and doesn't change the mechanics of the actual system.

    E.g., you have a deadline coming. A really important one. If you miss, it will be a significant loss of revenue or whatever. So, you put it into AF and work it as normal. But also put a series of reminders on your calendar or Outlook or whatever -- "Deadline coming! Everything OK?"

    The reminder gives me plenty of warning, in case AF somehow didn't deliver on time. It gives me time to recover.

    But Autofocus ***DOES*** deliver... So eventually I totally forgot about the safety net reminders and rarely even think to use them.
