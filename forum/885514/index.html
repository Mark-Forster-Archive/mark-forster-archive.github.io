  • Hi Andy,

    Until Mark sees this ...

    I'd cross out the completed call. No matter the exact outcome of a call that is not "finished" I'd treat it as any other "worked on but not finished task" ... cross it out and add it to the end of the open list, reworded to specify the next action. For example:

    - Joe returned call?
    - Joe returned voice mail left on xx/xx?
    - Follow up call to Joe. Third try.

    Or something that make sense to you.

    What you don't want to do is leave tasks open on the "closed list" once they have been acted upon in SOME way. To do that leaves you in an indeterminate state on the closed list. You count on completing and dismissing that closed list so you can draw that line and them move on to make the remaining open list the new closed list. If you leave items open that you have worked on, it will undermine that process.

  • Hi Andy,

    Similar to those above, here's what I use for these cases, taken from your text.

    Firstly, write Call X about Y on AF list, separate lines for each call to be done

    1. I get to speak to the person I want (great!!)
    > Cross out task
    > no further action unless anything comes out of the call

    2. I leave a voice-mail
    > Cross out task
    If it is not time critical when I get a reply by then
    > Add "W - call from X" (where W is wait for)
    > otherwise add "W - call from X" to calendar at appropriate day/time

    3. I speak to his/her PA who promises me s/he will call back.
    As 2 above, just adding a note on the original task to say talked to Z
