Reduced number of active pages

  • Mark used to write that he spent most of his time at the end of the list. At the time I read that, I thought it was odd, because the way I use the system it forced me to cycle continuously through all of the active pages.

    But over the last weeks I've noticed that I've been spending most of my time at the end of the list too. This is happening because, generally, I have very few active pages. At the moment, I have one active page in addition to the last page. When there are only 3 or 4 active pages, it's easy to spend most of one's time near the end of the list, because EVERYTHING is near the end of the list.

    I've been asking myself why I have so few active pages. Perhaps it's because I've had to travel one week in January, one week in February, and I will be leaving for another business trip this Sunday. Maybe my subconscious is telling me not to overcommit during a period in which my discretionary time will be minimal. Or maybe it has nothing to do with travel; it's just the way I've adapted to AF.

    I think that AF accomplishes one of the DIT principles in a way that is superior to DIT. With AF, if I take on stuff at a more rapid rate than I do stuff, it becomes very obvious very quickly. I like very much Mark's suggestion that I make at least one cycle through all the active pages each day. If I can't make that cycle, I get very anxious. And that will make me think twice, or thrice, or more, before I add another item to the end of my list.
