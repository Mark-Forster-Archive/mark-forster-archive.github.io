Coming home

  • I have just come back to visit this site after an absence of a few months. And look what's happened while I was away... smoke and mirrors!

    For me, reading Do It Tomorrow, and doing my (limited) best to apply what I learned, was a significant step forward in my journey.

    But now, this Auto Focus bizzo, which I have started implementing for, oh, let's see now, about 30 hours, feels like another breakthrough.

    But here's the thing... I have always been a pretty intuitive person, and trusted my intuition what's more. And a lot of what is involved with the practice of the Auto Focus approach, seems a lot like what I normally do anyway, even within the DIT framework... scan through the 'to do' list, and do what hits me.

    What's different, is the simple but powerful, and in some ways quite subtle, addition of a structure around that process, in such a way that the structure is not constricting.

    Plus a further clarification of separating out tasks from appointments.

    So I am looking forward to pursuing this. Thank you to all those who have written recently to this forum, as I have already learned from you.

    And a special thank you to Mark for being such a personable fellow. :-)

    By the way, Mark, off on a tangent I would be interested in exploring one day whether the AF approach is more applicable to certain personality types.

    Regards
    Steve
