    There are two instances where you can have previous pages. First, if you look at the list and pick out more than one item, then as long as one of your items is on the first page then the rest can be on any page at all. Second, if you can look at the list multiple times in a single "visit", then you can look at the whole list and not apply the dismissal rules until the end of your "visit". (So, I can work my entire list throughout the day and only apply the dismissal rules at the end of the day. If, by the end of the day, I haven't worked anything on the first page, only then do I dismiss them.)

    I now have three approaches to choose from. I can choose to continue dealing with my list the way I have been... only with a clearer understanding of what's going on.   • jFenter, the system totally is not concerned whether you work it non-stop or take a break and come back. It doesn't matter if the day ended and a new day began.

    "Visiting a page" starts when you turn a page, and ends when you turn another page. If in between those two moments you did anything in the first column, nothing gets dismissed.

    In my experience dismissing a page is quite rare; usually only after most of the page is finished, after having gone around the entire list several times.

  • jFenter needs to realize that the idea is not to "visit the list" but rather "visit the page." Put a paperclip or post-it note or flag or something on it, to remember which is the current page. When you come back to the list, continue working from the same current page. When you have taken some action on at least one task in Column 1, and have everything crossed off in Column 2, you may choose to go on to the next page. This is the "visit" we are talking about. When you turn to the next page (and mark it as the new current page), if you don't find anything you want to do in Column 1, the page must be dismissed.

    The rare circumstance where there is only one active (non-dismissed) page is the same situation as when starting a new notebook, writing a new list. I don't think there is any point to using Column 2 on a 1-page list for unfinished tasks. Just rewrite it at the end of the list (in Column 1). It's not urgent, so it doesn't belong in Column 2 of this current page. And there is no "Next page" yet, until the current Column 1 is filled.
