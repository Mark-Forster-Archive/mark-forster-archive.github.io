Spending less time on minor things

  • I have a strong tendency to dump and tend to chunk down what are major projects on the list. I re-read some of Mark's very good advice to Mel in an earlier post where he told her that with the amount of discretionary time she had, she had too many active pages. I have the same issue as I found that my list was getting overwhelmed by housecleaning items. I do want a reasonably clean house, but it's not what I want written on my headstone ie. "she had a clean house".

    I was experiencing resistance to dismissing those items, but in reality many of them could have been dismissed and weren't really very necessary. I was definitely operating in *push* mode on them, seeing them as some sort of personal challenge that I wanted to overcome. So this morning, I went through, weeded out 95% of them and pulled the others onto one little list that I will do today in about 4 hours (vs. the 17 hours and 10 days I thought they would take pre-dismissing. I'm only moving them onto one list because some are time-dependent (have to wait until stores open).

    As I was going through dismissing however, I realized that a large number of the tasks I was dismissing were specific cleaning items (eg. clean out microwave). Now my microwave isn't that dirty, but my kitchen floor is as I just had the plumber in to install my new dishwasher. So I had a realization that instead of breaking down these housecleaning type of tasks like I had been doing, I should be making this item MORE general. So instead of having 15 items on the list relating to cleaning the kitchen, I can have only 1 - "clean kitchen" - whatever is needing to be cleaned the most can then be attended to vs. having "clean kitchen table" come up on the list when in fact, the counters are messier than the table. Then I can use common sense and do the one that's most pressing. I don't need a project plan for this as I have very clear visual evidence of the problem. :-)

    Doing this will also have the benefit of cutting the length of my list by 50%, keep my focus on doing basic maintenance towards a clean house and free up my time and my list for more important things.

    I'm not sure how far to go with this - do I break it down by room - or just write "clean house" on the list? Or is that too far?

    I already do this with my decluttering project and that's going well. All I have written on the list is "declutter" - which to me means getting at least one box and preferably my whole car trunk full of stuff to give away - or listing a big item on freecycle.

    I'm going to consider doing something similar with my home reno as well and move the individual items off to a separate project list because I'm not as sure of some of the steps as I am with something basic like housecleaning. If I have less pages, it will surely come up on the weekends like it ought to.

    Has anyone else tried reducing their granularity/chunking down to achieve less pages ?

  • Hi Jacqueline

    I did exactly this - but only after trying every other combination - here's my "clean kitchen" journey .......

    Stage 1 - Individual Items
    Several individual items found their way onto my list as part of the organic process. Levels of resistance and speed of action on those items made me aware of certain "feelings" related to specific task (e.g. my dishwasher story).

    Stage 2 - The Quest for Balance!
    I assumed that my new revelations would cause my kitchen to be instantly transformed - well actually that I would "just do" the relevant items :-) That didn't happen to the extent hoped for with the result that "clear kitchen" became a necessary task, leading to ....

    Stage 3 - The Dumping (Panic) Stage
    In an attempt to force the "clear/clean kitchen" tasks to jump out I dumped every single related task I could think of into AF, all in one go, and waited to reach that page .......

    Stage 4 - Action - "Not Fun"
    When I reached the page I ignored the resistance and "just did it". It was not fun. It was not AF, and I most certainly would not recommend doing it again!

    Stage 5 - Rethink
    It is obvious when you think about it, that any "project" to clear or clean a room can be treated as a recurring task, regardless of whether some of the individual tasks are periodic (eg defrost freezer) or daily (dishwasher). I therefore created a quite pretty MindMap, with all of the tasks associated with the kitchen, complete with little pictures and colour (using MindManager). Any time I need my kitchen to be addressed I can just print off my MindMap - which looks fun - and do whatever tasks are necessary. In actual fact, since creating the MindMap I haven't had any problem with the kitchen - it is staying clear, and everything is just being done as I go along. It's as though creating the MindMap put the original AF fun back into things!

    I now have tasks to create similar MindMaps for the other rooms but have already found that they are starting to clear with just the addition of the few organic tasks at present ......
