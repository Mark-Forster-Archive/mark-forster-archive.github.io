altering the DWM dating algorithm

  • I've been trying some "tweaks" to DWM.

    First, I very rarely get through my whole list, since I have hundreds of tasks on it. So, my default behavior is now to look only at what's on the list for the next 7 days. I can still look beyond that, cycling through all the pages up to Today+30, but I normally don't.

    But I CAN cycle through the first seven days pretty easily -- I get through it several times a day, as DWM is supposed to work.

    I always start my day on Today+6 (which was yesterday's main re-entry point, at Today+7). This is usually where I find my "most active" projects, and also the recurring items that need daily attention. It is the best place for me to start, to make sure I see these active items.

    I also have been putting more thought into resetting the expiration date on unfinished and recurring tasks.

    For example, there are some tasks I may want to do more than once per day -- for these, I re-enter them at Today+7, as per the DWM rules.

    But there are other recurring items I don't want to see more than once per day -- so if I work on them, I re-enter them at Today+8, so they don't appear again till tomorrow (since I don't normally look past Today+7).

    Also, there are some recurring items that I don't want to think about for at least a month, so I set the expiration date to Today+60 or something like that. This effectively takes the place of my paper-based Tickler system.

    For non-recurring tasks, such as project work, I may want to stop working for now, but possibly pick up again later that day. So it gets re-entered at Today+7. But sometimes I may say "that's enough for today" and re-enter at Today+8, so it doesn't show up again till tomorrow. At other times I might say "that's enough for this *month*", so I re-enter at Today+30, or even Today+45 or something like that.


    I don't even like calling these "tweaks" since they are fully in the spirit of DWM -- simply adjusting the dating algorithms to fit my particular needs. It doesn't seem to affect the "dismissal" effect at all -- that still works great.

    Anyway, there had been speculation on the forum whether it would be "OK" to tweak the entry and re-entry dates. I am finding that it's not only "OK", but makes the system work much better -- for me. It helps me adjust my total workload -- that is really DWM's greatest strength -- enabling me to gauge how much is currently on my plate and how well I am keeping up with it. These "adjustments" give me more flexibility to control what is on my plate.
