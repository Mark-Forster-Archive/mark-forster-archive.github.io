Autofocus and anxiety

  • I notice that my anxiety level goes up when I'm using Autofocus (as opposed to DIT or just an ordinary To Do list). And I realize it's because my list is such a hodge-podge of different types of items (utterly mundane things like taking out the trash; high stakes activities like editing a story or writing a letter of recommendation; the need to get my cholesterol checked bumps up against an e-mail that I must write to a colleague; my girlfriend's birthday present is juxtaposed with a student screenplay that I have to read. Just going down the list I feel a sense of chaos -- rather than a sense of order.
    What I liked about DIT was that I could lay out my day and just deal with it. I had a greater sense of control (or at least a greater illusion of control). Has anyone experienced a similar reaction?

  • Here's a thought...

    At the beginning of the day (or the evening before—whatever works), work through AF as usual, but without actually DOING anything. Instead of DOING, just SCHEDULE.

    If Task 1 stands out, then think how much time you want to work on it, and block out that much time on the calendar.

    If Task 2 stands out, do the same.

    If some tasks are "odds and ends" kinds of tasks that take 10 minutes each, then block out an "Odds and Ends" hour (or two), and put those tasks into that bucket.

    Keep going until you are satisfied with your "map" of the day. Maybe leave some open time for flexibility and to work the list as normal.

    This might give you the structure you need, but still be guided by the rational/intuitive approach of AF. (And still have AF as a catch-all in box, and still have AF available to be worked as "normal".)
