Not sure AF is for me in the long run...

  • Hi Folks,

    Well, I have tried to make AF work for some time now. My implementation has been in OneNote with time-sensitive tasks in Outlook 2007. But I am finding that perhaps this system -- while admittedly brilliant -- just does not fit my personality and work style. Here is why. I have so many projects with deadlines that a good portion of my tasks end up being better tracked in Outlook 2007 in a linear sequence manner rather than in long lists in AF. I have used project-tracking software for my many projects with the tasks created and moved into Outlook. But what I am finding is that I have two systems -- my time-sensitive tasks in Outlook and then my AF lists, which I try to engage in my "free" time. Everyone always discusses the low overhead of AF -- and if you can incorporate your entire business portfolio of tasks in AF -- this may be the case. But I am finding that I have to manage two almost separate task systems, and to me -- that is unnecessary overhead.

    I will admit freely here that my personality type is one of structure and linear scheduling of tasks. When I am asked if I can do a particular task or project, I like being able to look at my schedule and linear scheduling of my tasks -- and being able to state that I cannot this week or next because of my commitments. When I have tried to have everything in AF, I have no sense of really what is on my plate because I am looking at long lists -- master lists if you will. I really do miss DIT, which really fit my personality and workstyle better.

    Maybe I am the only one that feels that way. It is amazing -- I almost feel guilty that I am an outlier. There are so many here that have embraced AF and it seems to be almost a life-altering event. I wish it was that way for me, and I have really tried over the last several weeks.

    So...I think I am going to go back to either GTD or DIT as I am more comfortable with either of those. I wish everyone here the best.

    -David
