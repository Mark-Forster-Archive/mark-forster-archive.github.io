AF works great with difficult tasks!

  • My digital page consists of 27 tasks each and currently I have 12 pages with 3 pages either completed or dismissed.

    I have been circulating through 3 tasks on a very difficult page 4, eager to dismiss it, rephrase or just do it. I got 2 of them done, leaving a task I was avoiding due to the sheer volume of very mundane actions. (the end result was very important, though).

    So, today, having gathered all the material to begin the task (I guess that was the initial break down of my resistance to it), I ploughed through it in about 3 hours. I finished the boring actions, leaving me with one follow up task that would produce the end result.

    What a feeling of release! The thing is, today was a day I stopped circulating through the AF pages when I hit the task on page 4 and put all my energies into it.

    I have another very important project on my plate (moving office), with multiple sets of actions on my AF list, but the completion of this huge task has motivated me to meet the leftover project tasks with energy and vigour. I breezed through many of the project actions after that.

    AF is indeed amazing, and the more trust I put to it, the more it pays me back in terms of productive time spent in sometimes very unplanned ways.

    I am eager to get some of my goal oriented actions tagged on to my AF lists, as it has proven to be robust yet malleable and structured yet fluid.

    Thanks Mark for all you have done to bring this to us.
