  • I am newer at SF than many here.

    Mark has advised us not to put into SF things that we would do anyway. Are people complying with or violating this suggestion?

    I can think of reasons why it would be good to violate this suggestion. But a good reason to comply is that I like the feeling of release and, yes, freedom that I feel when I occasionally go off the list during my workday.

  • SF etc. is only for your discretionary time. So a valid reason to be off-list, even for an extended period of time, is that you have no discretionary time. You might still want to carry your list around, to add things to it that come to mind. But you don't need to work the list if your time is already committed.

    When you have a lot of discretionary time, it sometimes makes sense to list routine tasks that you would do anyway, as a way of taking stock of all that you are doing. And for some of us, it's easy to get absorbed in a fun and/or important task for hours, and forget to brush our teeth.

  • SF isn't meant to nail you down and take away your freedom. It helps enable your freedom and balance it with your other needs and responsibilities.

    E.g., I doubt very much that Mark took his SF pad along with him on his 9-mile walks.

    SF can help us decide when we feel it is the "right" time to "break away". E.g., enter lots of tasks like these:

    - Take a long break doing nothing in particular
    - Walk around the house and do whatever needs doing
    - Take an hour to putter around in the garage
    - Plan a weekend getaway and leave all your TM stuff at home
    - Go talk to your neighbor
    - Ditch work and go for a long drive
    - Drive up to the Arboretum with the children
    - Go spend some time at the monastery (if you happen to have a wonderful monastery nearby like we do!)

    etc.

    These items can all appear in SuperFocus just like anything else, but I don't think I'd bring my SF notebook along while I was doing those things... Unless I wanted to capture random thoughts or something.
