Happy birthday AF4!

  • One year ago today Mark demonstrated AF4 to this audience. It was immediately a hit.

    When I discovered this method, it changed my work life from semi-chaotic to semi-controlled. In the past year, I've gained experience with using and adapting the system. Many people moved on to DWM but I found AF4 was closer to my ideal.

    After 6 months of using AF4 essentially as written, I started tweaking a bit to get something that better suits my circumstances. Many tweaks just made things worse, It seems that AF4 is something of a local optimum. AF4 works great, but if you change any one rule, it simply works worse.

    I am now congealing to variant I'm extremely pleased with, but that's for another thread. This one is to say AF4 has been a wonderful discovery for me, and say thanks to Mark.
    4 September 2010 at 21:57 | Alan Baljeu
  • Happy birthday indeed! AF4 has definitely changed how I work; it has made me much more efficient, but more importantly has made me more relaxed, knowing that everything I need to get done is in one place, and that the rules, and my intuition, will sort out the important things and the dross.

    My one tweak has been to add a DWM expiry date next to each item, and follow that dismissal rule along with the closed list rule of AF4. These rules together ensure for me that the right things get done in time.

  • Hi Ms,

    I follow the DWM rules as written, every new task is expiry +30, when I make progress and reenter it is expiry +7.

    But I also follow AF4 rules-- using the line to mark off a closed and open list-- if no task gets hit on one pass of the closed list, those tasks are gone, and the open list becomes a closed one.

    And I do kill DWM tasks that outlive their expiry date, but that happens rarely.. More often, I make sure that tasks set to expire that day get hit.

    Hope this helps. I am doing this electronically with Excel so it is easy for me to follow both AF4 and DWM on the same list.
