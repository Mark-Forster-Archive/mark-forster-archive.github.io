  • With the different AF versions, it seemed like someone would post a "tweak" of some kind, or maybe identify a limitation in the current version. That would spark something in Mark's mind, and he'd go off and sequester himself for a couple weeks. He'd then emerge with a new system that elegantly solved whatever limitations were there before, and sometimes you could see the grains of the ideas that other people had proposed, but Mark had taken those grains, and put them into use in such a clean, elegant, intuitive way, that it looked like a whole new approach. Which indeed it was. There are so many "tidbits" of time management wisdom that we all carry around with us. Mark is THE expert at integrating those tidbits into a working coherent system. Plus he always came up with a few new tidbits that no one else had thought of...

    So, here is my guess... I think ping-pong had something to do with it. Maybe the new system is something like this:

    1. You use a daily diary book.

    2. New tasks are added to tomorrow's diary page.

    3. You start the day by looking at today's page. You process it in "AF1" fashion -- read through the list once, and then go back to the top and carefully go down through each item. Do whatever stands out. Work on it as long as you want. Keep cycling through the page till nothing stands out.

    4. Then go back to the earliest diary page that still has open tasks on it. Process them in "AF1" fashion. When nothing stands out, you can either move forward to the next diary page with tasks still on it, or jump forward to "today".

    5. For all old pages, "dismissal rules" are in effect. When you come to the page, if nothing stands out, it all gets "dismissed". Not sure how that would be handled. Maybe whenever you do a dismissal like that, you should add a new task on tomorrow's page to "review dismissed items".

    6. During the day, as new tasks arise, they normally would go on tomorrow's task list. If it genuinely is something that CAN'T be started till some time in the future, then write it on the diary page for the earliest day when you can start on it. If it is something that MUST be done TODAY, then draw a line at the bottom of today's task list, and start adding the new urgent items below that line.


    So, Mark, is my guess anything close to what you've got in the works? :-)

  • But Avrum, AF _does_ help you with your taxes. It tells you to write "Plan to do Taxes" on your list. When AF tells you it's time to plan, follow Barbara's other piece of advice: Pull out a sheet of paper and map out the things that are necessary to complete your taxes.

    Perhaps the first thing in your plan is to gather papers related to your taxes. Add to your plan (A) filling in forms, or (B) hiring someone to fill in forms. Add a few more details to your plan.

    So following AF you planned your work. Rephrase your task to "Work on Taxes". Then following AF and your above plan you actually do the work. If it's onerous, follow Mark's advice to just do a little bit.

    The only way this can fail is if when the system says do a little bit, and you know it needs doing, and you know it's only a small thing, then you decide not to do anything.
