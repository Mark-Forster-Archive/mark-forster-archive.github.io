Support Mechanisms for DWM

  • There has been many mentions of support mechanisms to DWM. A tantalizing glimpse into others systems. Can you describe how you support your DWM list. For example 'ticklers', checklists, and so on? I'm not the best organised, but I know if I go too over the top it could be a mistake. Similarly if I don't cover all the bases then something will slip through the net.
    23 February 2010 at 23:20 | Alison R
  • Alison

    I start each morning by tidying my office if its a tip and then clearing my in-boxes (e-mail and post). By clear I mean deal with it if its easy and quick or just list it in DWM if not. Then I run thru the DWM list for anything where my world will collapse if I don't do it that day - these get highlighted. Then I work on my current initiative if I can be bothered. Then I make a coffee. Then I potter round my DWM list as I fancy.

    Anything where I need a specific date reminder I put in my outlook diary that synchs to my iphone by magic. Jobs the recur or where I need a few reminders before the deadline I put in my tickler system - memotome.com which sends me e-mail reminders. When the reminder arrives I either action or just pop the task in DWM.

    My DWM list is in excel. I use other tabs on the same spreadsheet for my annual goals and have 3/4 others for projects where I need space to job notes to breakdown the tasks or keep track of things.

    For everyone in the UK: simples.

  • I use Google calendar for reminders of important due dates, but also for ticklers.

    If I think of something that I'd like to do, but I know it's not important or urgent enough to work on in the next month, I enter it into Google Calendar a month or two ahead and set an email reminder. When the reminder comes, I can decide whether to add it the task to my DWM +30 page so that I'll start work on it sometime soon, or whether to leave it as a tickler, ie re-add it to Google Calendar another month or so ahead.
