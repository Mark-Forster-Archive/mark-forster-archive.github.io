How many things to do on a page

  • The AF method is to study a page looking for tasks that stand out as ready for doing; and to stay on that page until no more tasks stand out.

    Another approach would be to do, or dismiss, one task per page. That would decrease the time to complete a round of visists to every page. What drawbacks attend that alternative, such that it is not the recommended AF method?

  • The "one task per page" method is actually considered acceptable, especially you know there are urgent things you need to get to further down in the notebook.   • I think adopting the one-task-per-page as a permanent rule (rather than an exception to be followed on occasion, when you feel you need to get through your list faster than usual) would have several negative effects:

    - AF would start to feel like one huge open list, rather than a series of closed lists. You'd lose all the advantages of working through closed lists.

    - Instead of slowly going down the page, and acting on an item if it stands out, you'd most likely get into the habit of scanning through the page and choosing the "highest priority" or "most important" item on the page. It seems to me this involves a completely different kind of evaluation of task readiness, and wouldn't let the AF "standing out" magic to work.

    - It would add more overhead -- after you complete a single task, you would always be required to scan through the whole next page of items, and choose only one item. I personally would develop a huge resistance to that, I think, and probably abandon the system.

    But maybe you are different and these kinds of things wouldn't impact you the same way they impact me.
