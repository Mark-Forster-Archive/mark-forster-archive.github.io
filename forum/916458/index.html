Important question about AF4

  • I think AF4 is brilliant and works well. But I do have some issues that I hope Mark and others can adress. Sometimes in my position -- and I am sure this happens with many others -- I can suddenly have new and very important tasks or projects thrust into my lap that are urgent. I know one should use common sense and just work on these as they come along, but then I am ignoring AF by doing this. I have tried to start my day where I left off on the AF list -- and if it is in the closed section -- I usually can find plenty to do as I go round and round again the list -- all before I come to my open list that has the new, very important, and reasonably urgent items (I don't mean same day necessarily -- a new, complex project that is due in a few days let's say). But it does not feel right to be working on things in the closed list -- they may be important, but not as important perhaps as these new items -- that are on the open list.

    So how does one resolve this kind of issue? It can happen to someone in a high-level administrative position like mine a lot.

    Thanks,
    -David
    18 October 2009 at 5:49 | David Drake
  • David:

    I think we discussed a similar issue a while back. If you have a strong feeling that you should be doing a certain task, then go straight to it.

    What you are doing is in effect saying that there are no tasks which stand out between where you are now and that task. That's perfectly within the spirit of the system.

    Once you have finished working on that task, continue from it - don't go back to where you were.
