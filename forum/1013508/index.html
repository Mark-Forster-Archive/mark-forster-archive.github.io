    Columns: Diary Date, Status, Task (can be expanded into sub-tasks, useful for projects and recurring daily/weekly/routine stuff), Flag, Done (checkbox), Context

  • Paper. AF1... sort of...

    Major changes:
    Pages can be worked in any order.
    Completely different dismissal rules.

    My major goals are to keep on top of urgent tasks, to move big projects forward, and to trust the list. I discovered that for me one of three things is usually the source of feeling overwhelmed:
    1) Languishing tasks.
    To keep the list fresh, dismiss a page once all its open tasks are over a month old.
    2) Too many tasks.
    To keep the list short, dismiss the oldest page if # open pages > "x". For me, critical mass at the moment is 7, so you can see why I didn't even give DWM a chance. My current rule is that when I start writing on the 8th page, the oldest active page is dismissed.
    3) Flipping too many pages.
    To keep the list tight, dismiss a page if its open tasks <=3.

    How these rules tend to play out in the real world is that several times a day I "bounce" to the most current page or two to stay on top of urgent tasks, but spend most of my time closing out the oldest few pages except when something really "calls" me from the middle. In general, my list consists of a few old pages with some lingering tasks, a few half-done middle pages, and one or two pages almost full of current entries. Threat of dismissal encourages little and often.

    1) Go through and remove any pages before the current date that have no open items
    2) Set "Day 7" and "Day 30" from the first day with tasks (which may be before current date)
    3) Go through the lists and update any tasks that wound up getting done even w/out the system, reentering on new "day 7" as needed
    4) Start using system with new "day 7/30"
    5) As soon as the last item on a date before the current date is actioned, I will remove that page and change the "day 7/30" if needed

    This should result in my being able to work through any backlog created by being away from the system without having to re-date pages, thus screwing up my calendar too.

    If I actually have to use this tweak at some point, I can give an update about how it works.
