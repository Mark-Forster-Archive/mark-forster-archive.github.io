Autofocus or DWM... Who are the digital to paper switchers?

  • I love my Mac. I'm a graphic designer, and my macbook travels with me to clients, coffee shops, and, dare I say, to bed. I've purchased The Hit List, "borrowed" Omnifocus & Things. None of them felt right when doing AF. So I'd switch to another piece of software, and then another iPhone app. As well, there seems to be a correlation between increased anxiety (personal or work) and a frantic search for a perfect productivity tool.

    I switched to paper two weeks ago, and the results were immediate:

    a) I UNDERSTOOD Mark's madness better with paper - I'm using DWM
    b) The assembly-line effect of DWM is more apparent with paper
    c) God bless the iPhone, but it takes 2-3 x's longer to input items with the keypad vs writing
    d) The sensation of writing helps me focus on what I'm doing or plan to do.

    Anyone else experience something similar?

  • I'm using a nice (Moleskine) A5 ruled notebook for DWM. I think someone (Mike?) posted somewhere else that he didn't find paper-based DWM practical. I thought I would add a few lines explaining what I'm doing, since I do find it at least as practical as all of the AFs (with the possible exception of the first incarnation):

    - Mark dates at the top of right of each page (I only use facing pages - the other side I use for journaling - from the back)
    - Fold down the corners of the +7 day and the +28 day pages - these are the entry points
    - business card in at my current page as a bookmark (generally I only use this when I'm closing the notebook - otherwise it's open at my desk at the right page). The integral bookmark is used for my journal.
    - Occasionally I snip off the top-left corners of expired pages to make it easier to turn to the first 'current' page.
    - Closed pages get a cross in the top-left, with a circle around the cross if there are no earlier tasks

    I think that's fairly simple, minimal overhead, and still looks aesthetically pleasing.
