Desire For Certainty

  • Part of my procrastination in the past has been a desire for certainty that whichever task I do is the right one, and in the right order, and a visceral feeling of dread reinforced the belief that it was actually possible to be absolutely certain, and I was always in danger of messing it up, so better to just avoid. I had already been practicing choosing to do tasks, even though I don't have definitive knowledge of whether its "perfect"--and AF is a very useful contex for continuing to practice. The dread has always perpetuated the avoidance of doing things, because the assumption is "this feels scary, therefore it must be scary" and it takes a certain amount of courage to go ahead and do something anyway, in spite of the fear. But as I practice, I get more experience with actually getting things done, and surviving the decision making process, and the dread diminishes--and if things go awry, I remind myself that they used to go awry while procrastinating, and at least this way I am actually moving toward what I value in life.

    In the past I would've gotten stuck on the "feeling of release" or items "popping out at me" and second-guessing myself as to whether I really chose the right item, but I'm at a point where I can see that the important thing thing is to actually just choose something, even if I haven't complete certainty, and learning to live with trial and error, which is how all learning works. And I've ignored my own sense of wants and likes and dislikes for so long, that they have become quite invisible, but they are still there, and slowly emerging. It takes courage to do this when perfectionism and procrastination are so ingrained, and I commend all those who are working with AF and struggle with these issues.
