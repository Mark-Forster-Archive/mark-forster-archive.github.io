  • AF2 is working fabulously well for me. I liked AF1, but there were a few problems with it, and all those problems are solved with AF2.

    I am doing it pretty much "by the book," even the part about going back to the end of the list each time. If there is a task near the beginning of the list that is pulling me, sometimes my "going back to the beginning" amounts to a very quick scan, not really reading each item. But I figure that is ok ~ it just means that item is really, really standing out, and I need to do it.

    I have my work & home items on one list, and the work items are marked with a symbol. So when I'm at work, I only look at those items. When I'm at home, I tend to read all the items, because I might see something that triggers me to go to the worksite (Much of my work schedule is discretionary.) I also have my DVR programs on the list, and they are highlighted in pink, so I can easily ignore them if I don't want to see TV-watching options (or seek them out if I am wanting to watch something on TV!)

    I am finding that there is much less resistance to using my AF list ~ throwing everything at it ~ With AF1, I would resist putting things on the list, because it might be a couple of days before that item would come up. This caused me to put the list aside for days at a time. Now I can use AF all the time, even for easily-done, routine tasks that I tend to neglect. (I think this will have a good result, because it puts "stoppers" in amongst items that are more likely to make it to the "on notice" stage. Then I won't have more than a couple of things on notice at a time.)

    Dismissal has been psychologically easier, for some reason, and more in line with what Mark describes it should be. Perhaps this is because I can see it coming, and adjust to the idea gradually. Yesterday, I had three tasks on notice, and I dismissed all of them with no problem. That has never happened before ~ I used to look for a loophole rather than dismiss anything!

    This system came at a good time for me, as my daughter left for 2+ years in the Peace Corps this week, and I would have found it very difficult to focus on work, without this AF2 procedure. I moved 58 things forward yesterday, even in an emotionally compromised state of mind. I feel very productive. Thanks, Mark!
