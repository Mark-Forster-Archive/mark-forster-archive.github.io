AF2 and To Do lists

  • Mark

    I'm asking this in a spirit of genuine interest, having been a keen user of AF since the start and now planning to try out AF2. However in AF2 the closed list concept virtually disappears, which led me to wonder about the journey you have made since DIT. On the front page of this website you still say about To Do lists: "A to do list is the finest known way of ensuring that you never get to the end of your work. The proof? How often have you ended the day with more items on your to do list than you started it with? Me, I finish all my work, just about every day. And I can teach you to do the same.". I wonder what you would say about this now? The process of dismissal keeps the list under control, but nevertheless it is still a list that at the end of each day is likely to have many undone items on it. So how would you put the key advantage of AF2 over the traditional to-do list?
