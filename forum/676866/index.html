From Paper to Electronic to . . . . ?

  • I started off at the beginning of the beta test period, got going quite quickly, adapted AF to my lifestyle (working abroad mostly). I was enthused by my new productivity vs ny inherent procrastination.

    The I tried to go electronic so that my Pocket PC Phone (Windows Mobile) would have a synchronised copy with my desktop/laptop. A brief foray with My Life Organised was abandoned, it reeked of too much overhead a la GTD, so I retreated back to paper.

    My next stop on this never-ending quest to find out what's right for me (and AF has been better than all the others in which I have sought "perfection" - part of my problem, I know!) has been ListPro (laptop + PPC, synchronised) which has been set up so that I can see the list shrinking as I cross items off, group items according to a handful of Status conditions (Completed, Defer to Do Later, Dismissed for Review, DIT Closed List, Reminder, Task Scheduled) etc.

    BUT, I have found I am not screening my AF list as often I I did with the Paper method. It's been great to be able to sit next to my sleeping Mum in hospital and review my AF items on ListPro. It almost seems that once items are solely in electronic format, I shy away.

    Some users have mentioned that paper is the only way, but I'd alike to hear from those who are using both paper and electronic simultaneously, which does seem to make things more drawn out, but maybe is practical for some people.

    After all, what really matters is what works for each of us individually in the long-run.

  • Christine:

    If you try out GIMP, here's a quick tip for making screenshots with it: as you probably already know in Windows you can make screenshots to clipboard by just pressing the print key on the keyboard or alt+print if you want only the active window. Then in GIMP just select "File" -> "Create" -> "From Clipboard". If you want to save only a portion of the image, select that portion with the rectangle selection tool and click "Image" -> "Crop to Selection".

    I recommend saving screenshots as PNG. Just type "filename.png" in the save dialog and GIMP will automatically save it in the right format.

  • Christine:

    PNG and JPEG are very different formats. JPEG is "lossy", which means the JPEG picture loses quality each time it is saved, also JPEG works best with pictures like photographs that have smooth edges and many colors. PNG is "lossless", the image is not changed by the compression. PNG performs best with sharp edges and if there are large areas with the same color, which is often the case with screenshots. This is important if you create graphics for a website, but I don't think it makes a big difference for your current situation.

    There is actually a problem with gimp help under Windows at the moment. You can download and install it from here:

    http://sourceforge.net/project/downloading.php?group_id=121075&filename=gimp-help-2-2.4.0-setup.exe&a=92708011

    The problem is that for many people, the gimp help browser doesn't work in Windows. Then you will still be able to use the offline help, but it will open in your web browser. I'm afraid you'll have to get used to orange. ;-)
