  • Hi Jiru

    Yes my lists do have repeating and partially completed and reentered tasks within them. I treat the statistics on the basis that I will generally be enetering the same "type" of items, therefore a percentage basis is a pretty good guide for me. I think you get a feel for how productive you are (which for me I define as doing what I have scheduled to do i.e. by putting it on my list) because that is what, at any given point in time, is "important" to me.

    If I spend the entire day on list without procrastinating then I feel very productive. With all other system I struggled not to procrastinate. I think for me the key is in knowing that doing the tasks that genuinely need to be done is the bais of productivity. If we are constantly beating ourselves up for doing what we feel is unimportant, which we subconsciously equate to unproductive, then we feel unproductive, even though those tasks may be essential. With AF, seeing that I am achieving a clearup rate of around 90 of tasks is a real encouragement. The relative importance is irrelevant if they genuinely need to be done - and if they don't then the system gets rid of them!

    I think when you refer to your feeling of focus that is all you need - we know that we are being more focused and that we are achieving what we set out to as a result.
