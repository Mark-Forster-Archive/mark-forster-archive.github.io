Differences between SAF and AF4 Revised

  • I'm really curious to see what the differences between SAF and the Revised AF4 systems are from a user's point of view. Since I don't use them myself, I'd like to know what one system does better then the other and which is best suited for what type of personality...

    Any comments are really welcome!
    4 December 2010 at 7:08 | Erik
  • The new system feels much simpler. With SuperFocus you must think whether a task goes here or there.

    With Af4R it's obvious: if a new/old task is done, you must decide if it's recurring. Over time there will be very few recurring tasks in new, because they are already on the appropriate page. So keep a tab on the Unfinished page and it's easy.

    If you think of recurring stuff as maintenance, it seems a bit like your Kanban system in that you work on one type of task for a while then move on.

    Me, I now think in terms of my project system, and in that conText, SF is the basis of my project system, and the new AF4R is just an example of one way to apply it. We will see if it's a good way.

  • Will:

    The two main types of task that go in the SAF column 2 are unfinished tasks and urgent tasks.

    Unfinished tasks now have their own page, so you would be concentrating a period of time simply on getting them done. Urgent tasks can be done at any time according to the standard AF rule of "if it needs doing now, do it now".
