AutoFocus stress test

  • I posted about this earlier, but I think this site ate my post, so here goes again. I signed up for AutoFocus on Jan 7th with the intention of giving it a 2 week trial run without changing anything.

    After I read the directions the first time, I started writing my list and got a page and then a few more items on a list when I got a call from my employer. I got laid off. I immediately added about 10 items to the list from calling to see how long my insurance was good for to updating my resume. I had this weird peace that I could just go ahead & knock out all the job hunting stuff and get back to the rest of my list later.

    For about a week, the job hunting stuff became the #1 priority and then keeping the house clean and then everything else as I could fit it in. I noticed an odd thing happen - I don't think I've been very good about dismissing stuff that should be dismissed but I find myself wanting to finish up the one or two items remaining undone & undismissed on a page just to be able to say the whole page is done!

    I feel like I'm getting much more done than I was before Autofocus. I also mentally have a list of things I need to get done for the day and normally do those first, tackle a few other items after that and then just relax & enjoy the evening with my wife when she gets home from work.

    Some good news: not long after I was laid off I got a call from my old manager that they reworked the budget at work and they are wanting to hire me back. This has allowed me to go back and dismiss the few job hunting items that were not done on my list and also during the couple days off I have I can go and take care of some of the things.
