AF2 electronic problems

  • I curiously started testing AF2, since I was not totally happy with AF1.
    I have made one change, since I prefer electronic notes taking instead of physical: I re-sorted my list, so the newest additions are at the top, and I work my list from top-to-bottom. (I didn't appreciate going from bottom-up - working my way down from the top feels a lot better.)


    However, I'm stuck with making the dismissal of old items work smoothly.
    I have to list also my done items, in order to find what items should be 'on notice' for dismissal (from the bottom up to the latest acted-on). I mark these with a yellow flag (I am still working with MLO / Mylife Organized).

    When I the following day decide to really dismiss these marked items I don't know how to do this efficiently.
    I can mark them 'dismissed', and make them disappear from my current list.
    But imo, I also should remove the very last 'acted-on' items, so I can detect what items should be on-notice the next day. These last one of few lines have no use any more, and the list must not grow forever, I guess?

    (There is no easy way to add a separator exactly where I want it, since I'm sorting on (already past) creation dates.)

    Any one have any ideas on doing this in the electronic AF2 system?

    (I hope you can disregard my sorting principle of the newest at the top instead of AF2's way at the bottom - the problem of dismissal is the same nayway.)

  • Jabbe I use this exact approach in Listomni for the iPhone. I regularly delete completed (not dismissed) tasks to keep my list shorter, leaving only the completed tasks I need to denote uncompleted blocks of tasks. So if I have

    uncompleted a
    uncompleted b
    completed c
    completed d
    dismissed e
    dismissed f
    completed g

    I delete completed c, d and g. Make sense
