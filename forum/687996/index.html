Experimenting with AF (1)

  • A post earlier this week regarding trying to influence the list set me thinking about those tasks that we believe are urgent and which we have concerns that they won't get done. With sporadic discretionery time I am not moving through my pages every day and have the same concerns as everyone else that tasks that need to be done will not be reached in time. Obviously there is the "just do it" rule but I find that I still have resistance to tasks done that way, as if they have somehow cheated me out of doing them "properly"

    My experiment was to take one particular project which was causing me concern and to write every little detail that came to mind into my lists, on the basis that, when I reached the relevant page I could choose from those project items and get my project cleared. It didn't quite happen as hoped. It was a helpful exercise in that it enabled me to get all of the constituent parts of the project into the my lists and clarified my thoughts a lot better. When I came to the page when I could choose from the various tasks however, I found that I had a lot more resistance than I would have hoped to as I felt I had to stay on the page until everything related to the project was completed, rather than moving on at the appropriate time.

    The conclusion I came to was that it would have been better to make that list of tasks as a separate project list, and to create a single task within AF for the project as a whole. I could have added that project time as a recurring task and probably achieved the result in a much less resisted way, particular as I would have been able to use the little and often approach. Of course I would still have been able to "just do it" if there were still any parts that needed completing.

    This is one o the biggest difficulty i have bee confronted to since i use AF (2weeks) after GTD. Honestly i did found the right answer. i worked on Omnifocs in a GTD way. Since 2 years i conscienciously drop all my items in it by project & contexts but on the rush it was indeed difficult to focus on context such as call ... My project was difficult to finish and i was disapointed not to go to the end,

    When i discovered AF i realised that it could replace the GTD context. But the same problem was still there how finishing my projet ?. I realised to that i have be more productive but was i more efficient ?

    So i found some little tip. 1) i write every thing which happen in my mind and do it in the AF time. 2) I block a time working on projects in the natural project for important projects wich needs it make a mind map or a list of my BIGS ROCS concerning the project. 3) report all my tasks on autofocus and if it begins comolicated i only report a general topic.

    During the day i always keep my project list on my desk and a binder with all my project alphabeticaly classed (except for the business wich is at the very beginning)

    When i work imagin i sudently decide to work on i item. It can be a general topic or a specialised item. If its general i just have a look on my project list and may be my mind map and work as far as i can. If it it not finished i just report it at the end of the list. And if it is not concerned by a project or there is no intrest for me to see my project i just do it.

    The result is that today i find more easy to work on paper than on computer.
    I am also more focus on what i do and i have realised tha i could easy report my due date on my agenda with no needs to have it in Omnifocus.
