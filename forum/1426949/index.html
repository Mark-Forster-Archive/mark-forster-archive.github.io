  • +Ad Jesum Per Mariam+

    Hi jens!

    If I understand what you posted, you meet tasks in your SF which grab your attention, which make you say, "hmmm...." but you judge you cannot do right now because of time, energy, or situational constraints, and thus you skip them again and again until you dismiss them, right?

    Hahaha, welcome to the club ;)

    Talking from my experiences, those "hmmm" moments are actually the "standing out" of tasks that is so important in Mark's system. Therefore, to skip those tasks without doing anything will actually harm your SF/AF system in the long run, because you are ignoring your instincts, and doing that repeatedly will eventually lead to you unconsciously not trusting the system.

    I am now actually convinced that my own willful ignorance of those "hmmm" moments is the greatest factor in my problems with following ANY of Mark's systems over that past years.

    Therefore, when we meet "hmmm" tasks in SF, those tasks that somehow stand out but we cannot do now or in the near feature, I suggest that we /somehow/ act on these tasks ASAP. So how do I do this, in my own SF?

    1) NEVER IGNORE THE "HMMM" TASKS! This is most important. To let those "hmmm" lie fallow in your SF list will not only make you unconsciously not trust your SF/AF list, but will also mess up your processing of the list since it will continually call for your attention.

    2) If you do not have the time or energy to even consider it now, cross out the "hmm" task ("hmmm" tasks are invariably found in Column 1) and write it in the Column 2 of the next page. If you want, place a mark beside it (in my case, I put an asterisk, *) to let you know it is a task under consideration. However, it is best to consider "hmmm" tasks ASAP.

    3) When you find the time or energy to consider the "hmm" task, decide right there and then to know why it called out to you.

    a) Is it because you have no plans to do it anymore? Then cross it out forever.
    b) Is it because you have to prepare something before you can do it? This, I found, is a common reason for these "hmmm" tasks to stand out. Therefore, do something to prepare yourself for the time these tasks are ready to be done.

    For a common example in my life: the "hmm" task is "buy vegetables." Well, when this task stood out I do not have time to do it, but I must do it sometime...and yet it is standing out from all the rest right now. So therefore I prepare for it: I prepare a grocery list of all that I need to buy, aside from the vegetables.

    Other examples: "clean car" ---> consolidate all the tasks for maintaining my car to be done in one day, then schedule that day. "Clean house" ---> make sure I have all the materials I will need, and break down the parts of the house that I need to clean for one particular day, then for the succeeding days, if needed.

    c) Is it because you CAN do it, but you DON'T WANT to? This is the MOST COMMON case for me. And I only have one solution for it: DO IT ^___^

    God bless.
