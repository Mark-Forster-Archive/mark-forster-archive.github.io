  • Hi Andreas

    You are correct in that there could have been a reason that you had "chosen" not to do the task but it is important to remember that you are new to the system and it could just as easily have been that you had not yet got used to the scan and review aspect. If that task was on Page 1 and you did not get back to Page 1 until it was too late then that could be the reason. If on the other hand you had reviewed your lists consistently and the item had not stood out then you are correct that AF could be indicating something else. It may not be that your subconscious was pointing out that the relationship was not important but conversely that it is an important relationship to you and "missing" that date could be the trigger to re-establishing contact.

    I really would not recommend the "do one unpleasant task a day" option. Apart from the fact that that is adding an element of grading of tasks, you will be surprised at how many tasks which you would initially have considered to be "unpleasant" reach the point where they "stand out" and the doing becomes almost (and often actually) enjoyable. It is the very way in which AF deals with those "unpleasant" tasks that enables them either to be done easily, reassessed or dismissed - but dismissed consciously in full knowledge of the reasons.

    Stick with the instructions without grading for a while longer and see how you go ...............

    By the way, here are my observations and recommendations so far.
    1. I like its completeness: The system allows you to capture everything in your world in a simple and trustworthy way. Nothing will fall through the cracks - just keep adding it.
    2. It also allows intelligent. The system allows you to make the right decision on each item with the right amount of reflection, intuition and, if I may add, conscience. It's a smart way to work and is obviously much more than a to do list.
    3. It is a miracle worker with regards to overcoming procrastination - it really gets you started like nothing else I have ever seen. I have never been more productive after using this system.
    4. One observation: It really helped me as I began this system to have a clear understanding of what a task actually is. If a task on my list is too big or too vague it I got bogged down in it. A distinction between a goal, a project and a task might help. Goals and projects probably require a different medium, which is fine because you can always transfer the details to the AF list. For me, I think an AF task has to have some physical boundaries with a concrete result -- make a phone call, type up a report, something like that. GTD has some good thoughts on this point.

    I would add only this: Thank you, Mark, for a great contribution to time management. This has been the best system I've used so far.

  • It's funny how we assume that most pleasant tasks aren't important. Pleasant tasks rejuvenate us so we can tackle the less pleasant ones. The biggest advantage of AF for me hasn't been increased productivity (though that aspect is certainly wonderful); it's been that I relax and even procrastinate in a positive way. Where I would once do mindless things that drained my energy and ultimately made me feel miserable (look through a catalog, surf the web forever), I now am reading and researching and enjoying my hobbies. We WILL take time to do restorative things. The question is, will we do quality things that add to our lives or not? We cannot work 24/7. One of the biggest reasons I procrastinated in the past was feeling I didn't have enough free time. Using AF gives me a feeling of luxuriating in recreational activities. I no longer feel guilty when I do them! I recommend letting go of the idea that doing pleasant activities isn't a good thing. It's very good indeed! Eventually, you'll be left with more challenging activities on a page and you'll do them because you've thoroughly enjoyed yourself on the way to them.
