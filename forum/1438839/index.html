Dismissal question

  • When I reach a page with one final item on the Col1 which I am unable to action (because I am not in the right place/context), how do I handle the situation? Mark mentioned that for Col2 items you push the items to the next page. But given that this is Col1 item, does the same hold good or should the page be dismissed? Anyone deal with this situation?   • My hunch is to dismiss it. If it is the last item left, then presumably you have been past it several times and chosen not to do it. Now, here you are again and not even *able* to do it. It ain't getting done!

    At least, not as written.

    Dismissal will focus you on rethinking it and rewriting it, perhaps finding a better way of achieving the same thing, perhaps writing a better task that will be more likely to stand out or that you will be in a position to do more often.

    Or, upon reflection, you may decide that this really was the best task to have on the list, and it just got unlucky. No biggie. Nothing says you can't put it back on the list—after a responsible review—if that is what's best.

  • I regularly face this situation. I keep one notebook with each column of each page separated into home & work tasks, by writing home-related ones from the bottom up, and work-related from the top down (and drawing a heavy horizontal separation line when the column fills). If I visit a page with C1 tasks I can only do at the other location, I just skip to the next page without dismissing. If there are some C2 tasks on this page as well, I either do them (if possible) or transfer them to C2 of the next page.

    If there are some C1 tasks I could do (but choose not to), then the entire page gets dismissed. This means I might be dismissing some tasks that could be done later, when I get to the other location. But that's okay – when reviewing, they can be added back to the list if necessary.

    Hope this helps.
