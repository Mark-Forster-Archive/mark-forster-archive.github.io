"Endless Loop" Effect

  • I'm using the new revised AF rules. Just had an interesting experience.

    I was in Reverse Mode (been in that mode all day!). I came to "Change to Forward Mode", and it felt ready to be done. So I switched.

    Came to first page, where I have some goodies on notice. One of them happened to have been already completed awhile ago (perhaps a duplicate?), so I crossed it off. Nothing else on that page felt ready to be done.

    So I moved to the next active page. But nothing on that page appealed to me, i.e., nothing felt ready to be done. So, I switched back to Reverse Mode.

    Back in Reverse Mode, the first thing that felt ready to be done, was to switch back to Forward Mode. I just felt there must be some things at the beginning that needed to be done. Some of those things are months old (using my old, original AF1 notebook), and were prime for dismissal under the AF1 method.

    But I came to my first active page, where my on-notice items are. But none of them are ready to be done. Probably never will be. So, back to Reverse Mode.

    Again, Forward Mode stood out. Something there was calling me. Perhaps I just need to get used to how the new dismissal/review works. So, back to Forward Mode, and the First Page. Those items were still no more appealing than earlier, so back to Reverse Mode.

    Finally, I had some sense beaten into me, and realized Forward Mode was going nowhere, so it didn't feel ready to be done anymore. And I moved on. And now it occurs to me that I should add "Purge List / go mark a whole bunch of items for review" as a new task, so my list will be cleaner. I was just hoping the system itself would take care of that. :-)

  • I'm somewhat relieved to see that others have actually hit the "infinite loop" problem. When I read the rules I saw that it might happen (because the forward rule allowed for it) and asked about it, but my question got lost in the aether somewhere ;-)

    I addressed that issue in my own system (I think ... Yes, there is still the problem of dismissing things that I really don't want to dismiss, but I took a suggestion given to me by (I can't remember who now ;-( to "rescue" things from dismissal by adding them to the end of the list with a bang (!) in front. That makes so much more sense to me, from a logical perspective and it seems to address the underlying issue:

    - I put it on the list
    - I decided it was not a someday item
    - I did not do it when I had the chance
    - I still think I OUGHT to do it
    - I need to keep it on the list
    - I need to have it in my face continually
    - I need to be reminded of my previous procrastinating on this item

    Dismissing it, rescuing it, and adding it to the end with a bang seems to accomplish all of that. Of course, I've not tested it yet so it does remain to be seen.

  • I have a feeling that Mark already experimented with those different options, and, in the end, landed on the method described in RAF.

    One reason I suspect this is the way the psychology of the switch back to Reverse Mode has been affecting me.

    Sometimes when I'm in Forward Mode, nothing on the page "feels ready to be done". But it's not because the tasks on the page are unworthy of action, but more because I've recently entered some new tasks at the end of the list, and those new (perhaps urgent) tasks are beckoning me to return to Reverse Mode so I can deal with them.

    So, since nothing feels ready to be done in Forward Mode, I follow the rules and switch to Reverse Mode. I then deal with those urgent tasks. Eventually the "Forward Mode" task stands out, and I am back at the tasks I was examining earlier. This time, they feel ready to be done, because none of the urgent tasks are distracting my attention.

    Changing the rule to be "choose: either dismiss, or switch to Reverse Mode" just seems to slow down the system. Maybe it's simply the fact that I now have a choice I must deal with -- and I don't want to break my "flow" and stop long enough to figure out what choice I should make. I'd rather just follow the rules and move on. I'm not sure why. But it just bogs it down.

  • And, let's not forget, there are many ways items might become un-actionable:

    1. You decide you aren't going to do them (as above).
    2. You already *did* them (i.e. away from AF).
    3. Someone else did them.
    4. Circumstances have changed. No need to water the garden if the rabbits ate everything.
    5. An item was one of mutually exclusive options, and was excluded by action on another item (i.e. Go on holiday to Rome; Go on holiday to Istanbul. . . you choose Rome, you delete Istanbul).

    And there are likely others.

    The point being that when an item doesn't *belong* on the AF list for any reason, it can easily (and properly) be taken off. Just deleted, without being up for review, or reviewed.
