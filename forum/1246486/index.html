The Essence of DWM and AutoFocus

  • Mark Forster has created 3 systems (AF1, AF4, DWM) which are essentially the same. They have weird quirks like rewriting tasks rather than leaving them, and mechanical rules rather than planning. Yet they all work very well because of these essential mechanics:

    record everything
    scanning
    little and often
    dismiss
    closed backlog
    rewrite acted items

    Effects:

    Record everything - Nothing is forgotten.
    Scanning
    - you see everything
    - choice is simplified to Now or Not Now
    - "standing out" allows your intuition to take charge.
    Little
    - you can always make progress
    - you can get to everything in a day
    - urgent things are acted on today
    - non-urgent things aren't neglected.
    - once you've satisfied all the urgent things, you may slow down and do Much instead

    Rewrite
    - shows you are working
    - helps ignored tasks stand out
    Often
    - progress accumulates
    - your subconcious solves problems when you leave them
    - you avoid "writer's block" or other stuckness

    Closed Backlog with Dismissing
    -  Proves progress.
    - nothing can be neglected forever

    Simplicity 
    - easy to get into the system 
    - doesn't fall apart either from neglect or overwhelm.

  • My kids have trust issues with their agendas (planners). The school gives them out and some teachers expect parents to sign them every day. It's a great way of communicating quick notes. Without that adult-enforced structure, though, they aren't always used.

    In order for a system to work, you have to trust it in two directions.

    You need to trust that writing it there will be worth it. If you don't look at it regularly, there's no sense writing anything in it.

    You need to trust that reading it will be worth it. If you don't write everything in it, there's no sense reading it regularly.

    Eventually, if you work on both sides, it gets to the point where you write things in it and trust it. Your mind can relax and be creative and productive.
