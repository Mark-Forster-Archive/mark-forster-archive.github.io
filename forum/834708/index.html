Google Tasks with Autofocus 2

  • Autofocus 2 is working out very well, and I am enjoying a level of productivity even higher than what I achieved with AF1. I'm able to follow a 100% straight implementation of the AF2 workflow and get those urgent items properly knocked out when when needed.

    I have completely dumped my email clients (Mail on Mac and Outlook on Windows) in favor of extremely capable and always accessible Gmail.

    http://mail.google.com/mail/help/tasks/

    At the end of last year, Google implemented a light task manager called (strangely enough) Google Tasks. The simplicity of it initially tickled my fancy, however with the hard page end requirements of AF1, I discounted it.

    Now, with AF2, it works like a dream. Adding a task with a link to the referring email is a click or keystroke away. It works on any computer I find myself in front of, and is also accessible from my iPhone.

    Tasks are added at the top, like most task managers. This actually is more intuitive (for me at least) having the most recent item at the top of the list. Blocks that are "on notice" for dismissal get indented as subtasks of the line drawn for that day. At the beginning of the new day, the line with its subtasks gets moved to another list, and a new line is drawn and on notice items get indented.

  • Mel,

    Moving tasks to another list is accomplished by entering a task (the "line) above the block to be put on notice (I use a dash and today's date). Then, I indent each task below the line (remember, this list is upside down in relation to a written AF2 list), making them subtasks of the line task.

    To move them to another list, click on the arrow to the right of the line task, and in the "move to" dialog box, select the other list. Since all the tasks below the line are indented and therefore are considered subtasks of the line task, they move with the line task to the new list.

    I have adopted a (very) minor tweak which I'm very happy with: I never allow one "block" of tasks to have fewer than two tasks in it. That way every day at least 2 items are up for dismissal. So any time two single tasks are separated by a completed action, I just delete the completed action and join the single tasks into one block.

    Regular interspersal of frequent, simple tasks keeps the list moving and virtually guarantees that only 2 tasks (but never less) are up for dismissal every day.

    This is working very well for me.
