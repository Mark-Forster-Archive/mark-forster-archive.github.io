How can I persuade other people to use AF

  • One problem with adopting this system is that suddenly everybody else in your life seems unbearably disorganized and unproductive. Is anybody else feeling this way? What are you doing to get other people using AF to manage their time, and have you had any success?

    Even before using AF, I was amazed how many people would complain of being overwhelmed with tasks and work, but would refuse to write a list or identify manageable actions and do them. It's like a resistance to change, or as if people grow to depend on their backlog of work somehow. I feel bad for people in that situation, and it's also frustrating for me!

    Any advice?

    Dave

  • Dave:

    That is something that I've noticed when giving seminars in organisations where the people attending have been told to be there. There are many people who are very resistant to making any changes in the way they work. It is as if being extremely busy, having a huge work load and always being on the verge of overwhelm is in some way important to their self-image. In fact I found that people were very protective of their work loads.

    That's why I much prefer to give seminars where the people are there because they want to be!

  • Coincidentally, I attended a time management workshop today organized by my own employer. I suppose I was quite resistant to any systems that contradicted Autofocus.

    We looked at the Covey matrix: urgent, non urgent, important, non important. I know that without any system at all, it is extremely tempting to work on urgent unimportant tasks all the time -- you feel busy, you make colleagues happy, but you aren't doing anything that puts you in a risky position if it goes wrong.

    Maybe people fear a system because they know it would move them away from those seductive but pointless tasks.
