  • As Mark says, it is a function of the kind of person you are. If it helps ...

    I'm a very visual person. My internal experience of carrying on a conversation while driving is a little like going blind. My vision switches from external to internal and while I am internally visualizing I can't use my external visual sense properly. I takes a close call or something to snap me out of it. For me, this also applies to talking with my wife sitting next to me.

    We have had numerous debates on this issue (let your imagination run wild here ;-) as she talks on her cell phone for almost all of her 2+ hours/day commute. She is better able to drive than I, but I'm not convinced that she does not also suffer some degradation.

    After thinking about it it seems as if it is a particular kind of conversation that causes me (at least) trouble. It is one in which I must solve a problem. I can chit chat about things with my wife but when she asks the best way to get where we are going, I'm in trouble. My mind constructs internal maps and I start working on the problem and go blind. The trouble is that I'm driving when I go blind. No biggie if we are on a highway cruising, but if I need to make turns or am in stop and go traffic, it can be dangerous. It drives her nuts ;-) And for me, the ONLY time I am on the cell phone is when I am solving a problem. I don't get on the phone for fun ... I HATE it ;-) Like as not it will be my wife calling to ask what's for dinner and that takes thinking ... or my neighbor asking me to pick up some things while I'm at the big box store ... and then I'm memorizing a list.

    It turns out that we are all able to work in all modes of thinking. I am more extremely visual, but I am also somewhat auditory and a little bit kinesthetic. You may say that you are primarily kinesthetic, but you DO visualize to SOME degree and when you are doing that, you are detaching (to some extent) from the external world. It is probable that the auditory and kinesthetics also do, even when NOT visualizing. My reasons for saying that are speculative but they comport with my personal experience.

    As to "hands free" phones, it turns out that holding the phone is only a small part of the distraction and so there is a small movement here in the US to stop ALL cell phone usage while driving. The thought is that the carriers could disconnect the call and refuse to allow new calls (send or receive) when it detects that the phone is in motion (via GPS or cell tower triangulation).

  • Eugene, I found out that the strict Autofocus system didn't work for me when trying to make calls while driving. Nowadays I use a combination of Autofocus and GTD where basically GTD takes care of the project planning part plus provides some necessary contexts (like 'calls', 'errands' etc.), and Autofocus provides the main 'working environment' in the form of a one list, which I work through in AF4 fashion.

    This all is brought together by OmniFocus which allows me to have projects and contexts for the GTD part and a custom perspective for the Autofocus list (only next actions in chronological order like they would be on a written list). So, while sitting in front of my Mac I use the Autofocus list, where the calls are just normal tasks to be dealt with when they stand out, but when I'm out and about (or driving) I have my iPhone with the synced data from the Mac and I can just select the 'calls' context and work from there. Best of both worlds, methinks.
