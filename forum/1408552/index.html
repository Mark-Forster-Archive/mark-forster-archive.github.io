Delete a task anytime

  • In two recent posts, Mark has advised that one can decide simply to delete a task at any time. I had been under the impression that, unless I worded the task carefully, this was not allowed according to strict interpretation of the rules. That is, a task would hang out undone on the list until it was dismissed (highlighted), and then would finally die when I reviewed it and decided not to reenter it. Because of this interpretation, I sometimes prefaced a task description with "Consider ..." or put a question mark (?) at the end. These little hacks allowed me to delete those tasks sooner, without feeling that I had violated the rules. But now Mark has indicated that reconsidering a task on the fly, and deciding not to do it, is a valid action. Sounds good to me!
