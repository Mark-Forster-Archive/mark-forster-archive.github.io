Reviewing dismissed items

  • In AF4, when the Closed list was dismissed, all remaining items were to be highlighted and the next time around you must rewrite or delete everything.

    I see no rules in SF or AF1 that say when or how to deal with dismissed tasks. What's good practice here?
    8 February 2011 at 16:09 | Alan Baljeu
  • The AF1 practice was to keep a recurring item on your list, "review dismissed items". During the review, you delete or re-activate or ignore each dismissed task, at your discretion.
    8 February 2011 at 19:32 | Seraphim
  • Would you do this very often? Would you go through everything at once?
    8 February 2011 at 19:35 | Alan Baljeu
  • It seemed like that task would "stand out" maybe once a week, maybe somewhat less.

    If I was in a leisurely or contemplative mood, I might take my time going through the dismissed tasks, thinking about them, why I had put them on the list, whether each of them was really important, and if so, why it got dismissed. It would turn into a kind of long-term goals clarification session, and could be very fruitful.

    If I was in a hurry, I'd delete some of them that obviously needed deleting, but that's all.

    The longer it had been since the last dismissal review, the more entertaining it would be ("What was I thinking??? How did THAT ever get on my list???"). LOL
