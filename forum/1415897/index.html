The Meaning of Urgent

  • There seems to be quite a bit of confusion about the meaning of the word "urgent" as used in the rules for the use of Column 2.

    There's no mystery about it. I'm using the word in the standard dictionary definition of "requiring immediate action or attention."

    It's essential to understand the difference between urgency and importance. As Covey's Four Quadrants suggest something may be urgent and important, urgent but not important, important but not urgent, or neither urgent nor important.

    Column 2 in SuperFocus is for 1) unfinished tasks and 2) urgent tasks. The urgent tasks may or may not be important, but they gain their place in Column 2 because they are urgent, not because they are important.

    Take the case of doing the dishes after a meal. On the face of it, it's not very urgent nor very important.

    But if you have a policy that you will wash the dishes before you start preparing the next meal, then you have defined the task's urgency as quite high. It's still not important though.

    Imagine you are working for a gangster and it's HIS policy that you will wash the dishes before you start preparing the next meal otherwise you end up in the harbour with your feet in a concrete block. Then the task has become very important. But the degree of urgency is exactly the same (... before you start preparing the next meal).

    Remember: it's urgency,not importance that qualifies a task for Column 2.
