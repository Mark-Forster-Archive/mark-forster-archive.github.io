What do you think about this DWM/AF4 hybrid idea?

  • As I have stated in another thread, I have tried DWM in a paper diary twice now and have decided I did not like it and have gone back to AF4. What I missed was my trusty AF4 list a simple spiral-bound noteboook. I found that I really disliked the adding tasks 30 days or 7 days ahead. Like others, the idea of having to flip through so many blank pages until a task was found was a bit too much overhead for my tastes.

    But what do you think of this idea? I am following the AF4 rules in my notebook, but am thinking of adding the dates as in DWM in the margins. If I have not dismissed a task via AF4 rules, but the date of doom is there, then I will follow the DWM principle and dismiss that task. I can easily scan my lists and see the date in the margin -- this is much faster for me than using a daily diary.

    So, in essence I am following the AF4 system with the add-on of the dates of doom in DWM. What do you think? Is this a silly idea? Mark, have you tried this approach in your previous testing?

  • David,

    Your real problem will be that you will lose the forward looking approach. If you make one long list and put dates by the entries, you won't be able to move a new task that is just entered and shortly thereafter worked on UP to the one week mark to get that effect ... you won't have a place to write it.

    I guess I just don't see the problem some experience with the number of pages. It is not pages but tasks that should strike fear into hearts and those will be the same no matter which system one uses.

    One thing you can do to reduce the quantity of tasks is to "chunk up". Don't write every small thing on the list but rather just put "project names" for some things and have a separate list for the individual tasks. Upgrade computer is sufficient to remind you of what you want to do or you can have a list of things you want to do. "Shopping" might be enough with the items on a shopping list.

    Also, don't put the more "trivial" stuff on the list. You know you are going to eat dinner so don't clutter the list with it. You will know to wash dishes when the sink is too full. Lots of things send their own messages. I need to take out the trash tomorrow morning. I have forgotten to do it once or twice but the consequences are nil. I don't write it on my list because I so seldom forget it. (It has been 30 years on this schedule ;-)

    Mostly, don't look at the list as a whole ... just look at the next task and put one foot in front of the other ;-)
