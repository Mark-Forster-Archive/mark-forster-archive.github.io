  • Sometimes when I get very tired I procrastinate getting out of bed for a while. In that time of "twilight consciousness" ;-) my mind races along some very odd tracks ;-) ;-)

    This morning I was tormented by this game idea. I've just recently been using the Eternity time logging application. I asked myself why I was doing that. Well, I find that time can get away from me if I don't measure where it is going. What does the time log do for me? It tells me how I've used my time. What does that do for me? It allows me to change how I use my time ... IOW, I try to get better each day. What is the weakness in the software? It does not present the information in a way that is motivating. IOW, you just get a table of percentages.

    How could it be better?

    - Present the information graphically to give motivational feedback

    - Allow me to set a target and tell me how close I've gotten. (Ex. Your score today is X which puts you ahead of yesterday. Congrats.)

    - Give interim results (Ex. Every time I change my time usage it could tell me that my day is x% finished and that at this rate I'll score Y points which would put me behind (or ahead) of yesterday by Z points and my best day was Q points.)

    - Allow prioritization of categories of time usage so that I could get more points for better time usage and be penalized for wasting time. (Ex. "A" priority time usage gets 10 points, "B" gets 3 points, "C" gets 1 point, overhead things (eating or sleeping, for example) get 0 points, and time wasting gets -5 points. Very problematic time usage can get a bigger negative point value.)

    A point to consider is WHAT do you want to train with this game. If you want to train yourself to achieve better focus, the game could reward with points for each 10 minutes you work on the task without changing to something else. If you wanted to train yourself to get the most important tasks done, you could prioritize the tasks themselves and work on a task basis. If simple time usage is where you want to focus, then generalized time buckets (as in Eternity) would be good.

    The game could also allow you to SPEND your points on rewards! Reach 50 points and the program looks up a 50 point reward from your reward list and tells you that you can take a break and read a book for an hour ... 100 points and you go to a movie.

    The game could also be more "controlling". IOW, instead of relying on me to change my time usage (which I often forget to do) it could pop up a reminder at fixed (or random) intervals to ask what I'm doing. It could even tell me what I SHOULD be doing, much like a calendar reminder program but based not only on appointments but on the value of tasks to be done.

    Advanced features could include motivational quotations, music, cutsie pie pictures. If your score gets TOO low, maybe the phone could deliver a shock? LOL ;-)

    If I had even the slightest urge to write software, this is the kind of program I'd go after ... and it would be an iPhone app for sure!!!
