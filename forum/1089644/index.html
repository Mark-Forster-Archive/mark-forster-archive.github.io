  • Linda,

    The only way I've been able to manage projects IS by doing weekly reviews. For me, that is the whole point of doing weekly reviews. Keep your projects together in separate folders (paper or electronic). Look at them at least weekly. Pick tasks you will do for each for that week. Assign a time block when you will do them, then do them. Go back to the folder for additional tasks as needed during the week. Review and reevaluate again next week. Lather, rinse, repeat.

  • One thing that works for me -- I learned it from Neil Fiore's great book "The Now Habit" -- is to set a limit on how much time I will spend on an unpleasant task over the course of a single day.
    For example -- today -- i have a ton of reading to do for a course teach at a university. It is a beautiful spring day and I have NO desire to do this reading (which is one reason I'm on this forum at 10:07 a.m.). However, I am going to say to myself the following: "You will do SPEND NO MORE THAN FIVE HOURS reading today." That's the limit. I will time myself -- and make sure I don't go beyond that limit And that leaves me with nine or ten hours to do other things. I usually only use this technique at times when I have a huge amount of work and a great deal of resistance about doing it.
    It's usually effective for me and others may find it useful as well.
