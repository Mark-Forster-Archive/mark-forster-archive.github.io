  • As you know I recommend a separate list for each location. So at work, your list would include private or domestic items which you need to do at your workplace. Similarly your home list would include work items which you need to do at home. But I wouldn't include a home item on the work list just because I *could* do it at my workplace, only if I need to do it there.

    In Jordan's example of exercise, the answer is easy. You just put the item on both lists, and re-enter it on a list whenever you do it at that location. If you've done your exercise at work, your brain is presumably capable of remembering when you get home that you have already exercised!
