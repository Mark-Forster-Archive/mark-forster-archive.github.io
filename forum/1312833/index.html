SuperFocus gives you control

  • I haven't seen a lot of excitement yet about SuperFocus. Probably because it's new, and probably also because everyone already has a good enough system. So no need to get excited, right?

    Wrong. Having looked at SF for a couple weeks, I'm seeing something here that was missing in previous systems: Control.

    First though let me suggest one slight tweak to the rules. The rules say new tasks get entered at the end and worked-on tasks to the current page (aka here). But you can finish a Part of a task and reenter it last page as the next part if you want. Or you can take a new item, decide to do it now, and enter it here. So to simplify:
    - write anything here if you want to do it now/soon.
    - write last page if you don't
    Or to oversimplify:
    - write here or last page, as seems right.

    So obviously SAF is just AF1 with an added dimension of Control.
    You can do AF1 (aim to do a little of everything) by always writing on the last page.
    Or you can do Triple task by always Rewriting on the current page, choosing only the last 3 items.
    Or mix the two.
    But SAF also lets you pull in tasks from elsewhere, so you can control your current workload and continue Focusing.
    Or you can skip to the next page, work on a new variety of things, while pulling along your major project to get it done.

    Bottom line: Mark is right: this has all the benefits of AutoFocus, plus elements of Control and Focusing. The key to Super Focus is to use this control for your advantage. You are the master and SuperFocus is your tool to get done whatever you want.

    I have tons more I want to write, but this is enough for now.
