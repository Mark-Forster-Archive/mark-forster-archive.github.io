  • Gretchen, Rainier, Sarah: to each his own!

    When I started using AF back in January, I too was worried about the distracting potential of highlighting. But in fact, I find it works well for me, my eye is now able to seek out UN-hiighlighted items easily, despite the use of three colours:

    Green: for completed, tackled (then re-entered at the end again) or items entered into Outlook with a reminder (task or time slot).
    Yellow: for items entered but which are physically impossible to do at my current location. I work mainly overseas, so some home office items cannot be done till i get home. Tonight I leave Egypt after a month's assignment (hey, just thought, the Ancient Egyptiians used a lot of blue and yellow highlighters!), so will trawl through the yellow items that I have to do at my home office, marking them again in green when done/addressed.
    Pink: for dismissed items, in case I ever want to revisit them.

    As I say, my eye seeks out the unhighlighted items with ease. When entering items, I do as many others do (and after trying electronic a couple of times), I put a short dash at the beginning, "target" items by putting a vertical line through the dash ie the dash becomes a cross, and strike through the entire line when done, then highlight.

    So despite three colours, this works for me, which for any of us, is the important thing. Boxes at the beginning wouldn't, tried that as well.

    Champing at the bit for AF2, or 6!
