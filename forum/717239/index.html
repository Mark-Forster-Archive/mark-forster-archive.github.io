Least urgent things first in AF - what's your experience?

  • In DIT, Mark recommends to start tasks with far away deadlines early, to avoid them becoming urgent and stressful (if I remember correctly, it's been a while since I read the book). It strikes me that AF is very well suited to this approach. You can just inject those tasks and get them rolling.

    So I listed a series of actions I need to progress by a certain target date, some as far as the end of the year, in AF to see what would happen. And see, I already completed some tasks due by end of December in February. Somehow I even feel less resistance to these tasks, because the far-away deadline somehow makes them less threatening. So I'm afraid I may risk that they get done way in advance, while more urgent tasks become overdue...

    Do other users of the system do the same? What's your experience?
    6 April 2009 at 21:19 | Marc (from Brussels)
  • Hi Marc

    It sounds as if AF is working well for you. There is a tendency to think that "urgent" tasks don't get done because of panic or lack of time or that we have procrastinated on other items. If a task does not get done "in time" it is possible that we just have too much to do, but it is also possible that there is a resistance. It is highly unlikely that tasks will be accidentally missed in AF as there are safeguards in place. If there is a resistance to doing the item then AF will highlight it and enable you to assess the reason.

    I have virtually completed some tasks due next year but am resisting others - I have identified the reason for that resistance and am trying (not terribly happily I have to say!) to work around those reasons. Sometimes we will find that we have to make decisions which we do not want to make ...... :-(
