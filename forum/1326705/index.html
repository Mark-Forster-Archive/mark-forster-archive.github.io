  • As far as I'm aware, you start in the morning where you left off the day before. So, if that is a few pages in, then start there.

    As for dismissing... Again, as far as I'm aware, when you are on a page and there is no task that feels ready to be done, you *mark* them as 'to be dismissed'. Then when you get to that page *again* later in the day (for example), you decide which to add to your list again (preferably you rephrase them or split them up so that they will be easier to tackle), and which to cross off permanently.

  • BryanR:

    In AF1, after a break you start again where you left off. If the break has been long enough for you to feel you can't remember what is on the list, then it's recommended that you read through the entire list quickly before starting again. This isn't usually necessary if the break is only overnight.

    Dismissal takes place every time you don't do any tasks on a page during a visit to it. So in the example you give you would dismiss the five tasks immediately, not on the next visit. However please note that tasks are never dismissed from the last page (that is the one that is still open).

    Dismissed tasks should be highlighted and reviewed regularly.

  • In regards to dismissal, I figured out a great key to this system that I missed in simply reading the rules. "Work on that item for as long as you feel like doing so."

    Dismissal forced me to look at an item and then decide if there was one simple thing I could do to keep it from being dismissed even if it meant I only worked on the task for less than a minute. It caused me to see how tasks could be broken down into smaller and smaller chunks.

    For example, I have a very large, personal photo project that involved reviewing several hundred photos but with no deadline to it. It was about to be dismissed because I had overlooked it several times and procrastinated on it. Instead, I realized that I could spend a few moments, build the folder structure for the project, and then be done. Moving it forward and saving it from dismissal. I just had to remember, "little and often." When I rewrote it, I broke my next step down as "Review 20 photos."

    Finally, if it wasn't something I could break down into a small task, then it had gone through its chances and was highlighted as dismissed.
