  • Mark Forster called his method “Autofocus” because it is designed to make your attention naturally “focus itself” — automatically — on the right task at the right moment, without forcing a decision or overthinking priorities.

    Here’s what that means in context:

    1. Automatic focusing of attention

    Instead of consciously deciding what’s most important, you scan through your list and wait for your intuition to “lock onto” an item that feels ready to be done.

    This mirrors how a camera’s autofocus works: you move through possible focal points until the image “snaps” into focus on the right one.

    In the same way, you move through your task list until your mind “snaps” into focus on a task that feels right to do now.

    2. Balanced use of rational and intuitive processes

    Forster often said the system gets your rational mind and intuitive mind working together.

    The rational side provides structure — the big list, the page-by-page sequence.

    The intuitive side decides, in the moment, what to actually do.
    He wanted a system that didn’t rely on heavy planning or constant reprioritizing, but still ensured progress on everything that mattered.

    3. Progress through natural cycles (closed lists)

    Each page in Autofocus becomes a kind of field of attention that keeps narrowing until it’s finished. The list structure “focuses” your energy automatically over time — like adjusting a lens from a wide view to a sharp close-up.

    In short:

    Mark Forster named it “Autofocus” because the method automatically brings your attention into focus on the right task at the right time, blending intuitive feeling with rational structure — just as a camera automatically brings an image into clear focus.

  • I found a post by Mark Forster where he definitely says that word "autofocus" comes from photography (So ChatGPT got it right after all)

    http://markforster.squarespace.com/forum/post/931306#post934075

    "To expand on what I said on another thread yesterday:

    The name Autofocus comes from photography. Most modern cameras don't just have autofocus, they also automatically adjust the aperture, shutter speed, ISO and colour balance, and sometimes a lot more as well. They make it much easier for you to do your job - which is to point the camera in the right direction.

    It's the same with AFs 1-4. They are tools which will make it much easier for you to do your work, but you still have to point yourself in the right direction.
