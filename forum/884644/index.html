AF4: does the order matter in the active list?

  • Hello,

    I was wondering if the order of tasks in the active list had any importance. I have an electronic implementation where re-entering the task at the end is more work that leaving it where it is, and I'd like to know if it will break things not to add (repeating) tasks at the end.

    When you are doing the one pass through the active list, do you often find yourself doing a repeating task several times (as it's added at the end thus is available again during the active pass)?

  • For me, I find it very important to cross off the task and add it at the end of the Open List. I do take action on many items more than once on each pass through the Open List.

    Email, for example: While working the Open List, "email" gets done several times.

    That is, check email, then cross it off and add it to the end. Then work a few more items on the Open List (and some of them also get crossed off and added to the end again.) Finally I come to the newly-written email task. I take action on it again, cross it off, and add it to the end.

    If I could only do it once each time I come to my Open List, I think I'd fall way behind on email.

    Tasks that are associated with the "pressing work of the day" typically get similar treatment. Work for 20 or 30 minutes, then cross it off and add to the end. Work on the next thing for 10 minutes, then cross it off and add to the end. Etc.

    Maybe this is because I've got one or two big projects, mixed together with a dozen medium sized ones and who knows how many small projects and one-off tasks. I need to keep pushing forward on all the large and medium projects every day, or else they will get too far behind. The timed little-and-often approach works great for me here, and the cross-it-off-and-add-to-the-end is essential to making sure everything keeps moving forward in a timely manner.
