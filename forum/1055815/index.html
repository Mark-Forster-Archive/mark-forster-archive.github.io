  • Seems to me that "not getting thru" your list in one week is a problem. If you are following DWM, that means one of two things is happening:
    1) You are doing your tasks in order from day 1.
    2) Tasks are falling off as you are stuck in the middle of weeks 2-4 for several days, and not getting back to the start of your list.

    If DWM is going to work for you, you need to be able to pass through your list faster. If you ever get bogged down for a day, it's time to switch to overdrive and get through that list in an hour: Remind yourself what's urgent; do what's urgent and quick. Then don't let yourself get stuck again if you have urgent tasks ahead. (i.e. let this knowledge influence your "standing out" and "little and often" to keep you going.)

    If that doesn't work, you need a shorter list with only important things.

  • Since you are worried about missing your package, I assume you aren't yet comfortable with how you use and react to DWM. So I would propose two things:

    1. Put it on your calendar for Friday or Saturday (the latest day that you could get it based on the office is open and your other hard commitments). This is your fail safe mechanism since you aren't confident in DWM.

    2. Put "Collect Parcel [by Apr 3]" on your DWM list for @Sat + 1M. Brackets with a due date was recommended for noting hard deadlines. It makes it a bit obvious when going through your list. For me, I use square brackets since it makes them look completely different than any of my sloppy writing.

    On a day when you think you might have time and desire or if your deadline is getting close, you can add the Next Pass marker if you want that prompt as well.

    Good luck. Let us know if you get your package via DWM or Calendar/Diary.
