Bad idea or not ?

  • Hi,

    I do not try to tweak AF system but just make it work smoothly

    I have some tasks that are weekly or twice a month tasks. I have 3 options:
    1- put them at the end of the open list
    2- put them in some reminder system with alarm that will tell me to put it back to AF list
    3- ( Bad or good idea). Put the task on next empty page.

    Explanation of option 3:
    We all know how many pages we do / day or week ( Lets say I do 4 pages / week) so if I want get some task be done in 1 week I can put it 2 pages away so I get it in the list half a week away. I hope you follow my idea.

    So the question is : Option 3 is good or bad ?

  • For that kind of items, I like better the reminder system. I use outlook , but I have experienced a trick that also works : write the reminder with action and due date on a post-it note, a few pages away from the end of open list.
    When you reach the post-it, it's easy to copy the action at the appropriate place if the time has come. If it's not time yet (let's say your did 8 pages this week instead of 4 usually), you just move the post-it note a few pages away.

  • I use a tickler file (43 folders style) for anything which is in the future, which I define as over 1 week forward. I put a note or copy of a letter, birthday list - whatever- in the relevant date or a week before if e.g. I need to send a birthday card or a cheque to make a payment. I check the appropriate day file every morning first thing or the night before if I am feeling particularly efficient, and then if I do not action it that day it either goes onto my AF list or is refiled for another date.

    I have a recurring entry for "check tickler file" on my AF list.

    Works a treat and requires no technology, and it has the advantage of keeping my AF list clear of longer term things.
