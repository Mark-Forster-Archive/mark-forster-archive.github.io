Standing FOR attention

  • Just a post to follow up on my thread last year on standing instead of sitting at one's desk or workstation.

    I've been standing at my home office desk when working during the last three months and have found:
    - decline in back pain and discomfort
    - incline in ability to concentrate
    - declining waist statistics, not enough, but better than no improvement.
    - better posture
    - ability to move around during phone calls

    In truth I only sit down for any length of time in the evenings in the lounge. I have a kitchen barstool in the space in the centre underneath the desktop, but only use this occasionally to put one of my feet on the horizontal cross pieces near the bottom of the stool legs.

    I really urge anyone with back/posture problems (other than major ones) to try working standing at one's desk. My conversion only cost me two Really Useful Storage boxes (front-end opening flap so I can use the space inside), each placed on one of the two two-drawer filing cabinets and then the worktop placed on top of the boxes.

    Now my next problem will be to get a wooden folding "platform" to place on top of my desk at my next assignment office in Damascus next month; the work surface will be smaller than a standard desk top, but big enough for my notebook, external keyboard, external monitor and any files I need next to my computer.

    There are lots of web references such as this one http://37signals.com/svn/posts/1001-standing-versus-sitting, go to this site and RIL it if you're too busy right now.

    I am at the 1-year mark for standing at the desk. On April 1, I removed the wobbly bricks I had placed under my desk for more stable cinder blocks. I still have those cinder blocks.

    My neck problem ended long ago. I've since been reading a lot suggesting that neck and back problems are often of psychosomatic origin. My current view is that standing at the desk is a good idea, regardless of its effects on neck or back pain. Roger suggests, and I agree, that there is an increase in ability to concentrate. And I would add that there is an overall increase in effectiveness.

    A side benefit is that visitors to my office will now often stand beside me at the desk. It seems somewhat awkward to have me standing and them sitting below me. When they are standing, they tend to be more likely to get to the heart of whatever it is they want to say.
