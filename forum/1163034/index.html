In fact, looking back, my previous attempts at time management have generally been writing lists which I use for a few days then abandon and find several years later!

    Does this sound familiar to any other disorganised souls out there? Is the best response to declare a backlog to keep the list as small as possible to keep 'list resistance' at bay.

  • This is a form of procrastination, so you probably may benefit from the procrastination thread. The biggest point there is to ask yourself why. Different answers merit different remedies.

    The second biggest point there is to overcome that resistance. So you don't want to look at the list? Do it anyway. Just because. What happens when you do this? Does the resistance go away or is something bothering you even as you work the list?

    Regrading keeping the list small, I say absolutely no question. Get everything you don't plan to work on soon off that list. Declaring a backlog, I might do that once, but after that try to keep your list from growing out of control..

  • Nuje: returning to your earlier posts and Leon's response, like Jupiter I favour the handwritten list in whatever system on uses.

    My current adaptation of DWM also uses a Daily Closed List which I set up at the beginning of the day and cull from the main DWM list. Using a separate Moleskine pocket book, I enter tasks which MUST be done today, then SHOULD be done, and then others from the DWM list.

    Work tasks are entered from the top of the right-hand page (dated manually eg today is 12/07/10), personal/other from the bottom up, then ruled off with a line below the last work task and another one above the last personal task.

    This creates a space into which goes any additional tasks I do during the day. The left-hand page opposite is used for sundry stuff during the day, eg cash expenses, broadband usage (no unlimited ADSL for me yet here in Damascus), quick contact notes, password for a new web site (later inked out and transferred into eWallet), errand items eg 9kg of drinking water to be hauled up the 76 steps to my apartment, not pleasant in the current run of 44C.

    I have to guard against putting too many items in the Daily Closed List,so I'm considering the 1,2,3 approach mentioned elsewhere, then adding others later via DWM scans etc during the day or just doing them and crossing off the main DWM list.

    One other adjustment to my "system" is that my DWM notebook is also a Moleskine pocket notebook. This has an elastic band around it so that the DWM list and the Daily Closed List notebooks are always together with me.

    In the DWM book, I do not have separate pages for each date 28 or 7 days hence. I write that date on the right-hand edge of the left-hand page, then enter the tasks themselves on the right-hand page, using more than one line for a task if necessary, not often.

    Re-entered tasks are written on the left-hand page against the relevant date so that they stand out.
