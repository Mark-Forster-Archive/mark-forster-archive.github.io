Missed days with DWM - a different approach

  • I've been thinking about the "missed days" problem some more.

    In my own situation, my "missed days" have occurred mainly because of chronic headaches. At first I was thinking I should use Mark's method for just pushing all the dates back whenever I miss a day, so my DWM book would be reset to the point before I got sick.

    But as I was thinking about this, I realized that I actually do need to account for my sick time differently. It's part of my life, something I need to live with (till I find a resolution for it, at least). So, if I can't accomplish my work and something "tumbles over Niagara Falls" because I missed a couple days due to illness, my current thinking is that I should just let it fall.

    This will motivate me even further to make sure I stay on top of my list and don't take on more than I can really handle. If I find that stuff is getting missed because I'm sick, then it's another sign I have too many commitments, more than I can handle right now.

    This got me thinking even more broadly, in terms of vacations and other planned time off-list. Instead of resetting the dates, maybe it's actually better to make sure you clear your list! You know, clear the decks to make sure you are really ready to take a vacation.

    You always have the option of looking at the items that expired during your absence, and deciding what really needs to be done with them.

    This approach also has the advantage that you never need to rewrite the calendar dates. This allows you to use the same book for appointments and for DWM, as some of you are doing.

    Anyway, I am going to try this alternative approach and see how it goes.

    Let me know what you think!

  • Alan - Yes, exactly! But just because I got sick, or just because I decided to take a few days off, doesn't mean my workload has become any smaller.

    While I've been away, the rest of the world has continued along its way, filling my inbox with more paper and more emails. And my deadlines have not been pushed back -- they really have moved closer. Re-dating the pages masks this and makes it seem like everything is just the way I left it. But really, it's not (unless all your deadlines are self-imposed and you are the only one who puts stuff in your inbox!). :-)

    By refusing to re-date my pages, I can more effectively manage my workload ***while taking into account*** the fact that I might need to miss days unexpectedly from time to time.

    If you rarely have unplanned missed days, it's probably not an issue and re-dating may be effective. But for me, I need to EXPECT unplanned missed days and STILL be able to handle the workload.

    Re-dating the pages gives me an excuse for continuing to be over-committed when my real life circumstances are really telling me to manage those commitments better.
