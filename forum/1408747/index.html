Dismissal when sick - new insight from Mark

  • In another thread, Tijl wrote:
    << I notice that you have quite a few dismissed tasks awaiting review. Can you explain that? >>

    and Mark Forster replied:
    << Yes. Because the range of activities I can do at the moment is quite limited I am having to dismiss a lot of tasks which I wouldn't normally dismiss. That's nothing to worry about - they can all be reinstated when I'm well again. >>

    I'm not sure why, but this simple statement opened new vistas in my mind about how to use dismissal. I had always envisioned "review dismissed items" as a task that might happen once a week or a few times a month at most.

    Since I get frequent migraines, I've struggled with working my list when I'm not feeling completely up to speed. When I'm feeling bad, I would cherry-pick the list, looking for some easy thing to do, so I don't totally waste my day.

    But this gives me a new insight. I can still work the list totally as normal, following all the rules, most likely doing a lot of dismissing, and making sure to enter a new task "Review dismissed tasks when you're feeling better" or something like that.
