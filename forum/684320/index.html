  • Mitch:

    I think many former GTDers seem to have trouble letting go of some of the concepts in GTD when coming to AF. They are two very different systems and work on very different principles. I would recommend you to let go of GTD completely and try pure AF at least initially. When you have experienced how the system is supposed to work, then you can include some personal "extras" if you still want to.
    4 March 2009 at 17:49 | Mark Forster
  • Mitch,

    I, too, have come from GTD, and I must say, I do not miss my contexts at all. That is, I do not miss them now that I have used AF as intended for a while. I did miss them intellectually, which is somewhat different.

    So, as Mark has already said, just give it a go, maybe for a week, and stick to nothing but the original instructions. Then you will have some experience which will give you a much better frame of mind to ponder changing some aspects.
