  • 7. If you go to a page and no item stands out for you on your first pass through it, then all the outstanding items on that page are dismissed without re-entering them. (N.B.   • Bogdan

    "If you go to a page and no item stands out..." Mostly this would mean if you go forward to a page but in the case of the first page, it means when you go back to the first page. Mark has coverd both cases by simply saying , "If you go to a page..." So it seems your understanding of this part is correct.

    "Dismiss": This simply means you effectively remove them from the list. It is a firm rule not to re-enter these items (at least for some time to come). The rephrasing only applies if, after some time, there are fresh impulses to return to them and give them another go and put them back on the list.

    "Entered without evaluation". This means that when an item was first entered it was simply because it "occurred to you". You did not censor the idea. You did not commit yourself to doing anything about it. You did not know exactly where it might lead. It was just put down because you felt there might be something in it for you.

    "The system found them wanting". This means exactly the opposite of what you appear to think it means. It means the item lacked any drawing power for you. After repeated passes you still do not feel prompted to do anything about them. The system has finally assurred you that you can safely conclude that they have no relevance for your life at this point.

    An example: "Give $1000 to the Red Cross". If you were to find that you are not moved to act on this after 10 or more passes during a week of you life, get rid of the item. You are not ready to do it after all.
