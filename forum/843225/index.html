  • I'm no nutritionist by any means, but it strikes me that thinking in simplified labels of carb, protein and fat foods is not a great starting point for basing a diet on. The value of proteins, fats and carbs and food consumption in general depends so much on the context.

    With context I mean, the quality of the food (i.e. not all fats are the same), how you are eating it, when you are eating it, how much of it you are eating and in what combinations and last but not least, how are you feeling when eating and how well your digestive system functions (which is related to your general health and psychological well being).

    With nutrition I think in terms of maintaining optimum energy levels throughout the day. It helps to think in terms of how your digestive system best functions.

    questions like the following in my view, are better starting points to addressing nutrition than attempting to find the perfect combination of nutrients according to some book or person.

    are you eating while multitasking, standing up or sitting down, are you chewing it enough (since a huge part of the digestive process takes place in the mouth), when are you consuming? Just before bed? In what combinations are you eating foods? What's the quality of the food, is it processed or is it closer to its natural state that are easy to digest? Are you combining starches with fruits and meats? After you've eaten, do you do something to aid digestion, like walking? Are you eating things that take a long time to digest? Are you eating certains foods to turn a bad feeling in a good one? Are you eating just before going to bed when you're digestive system doesn't work at it's best? Are you eating portions larger than you can literally stomach? etc etc

    Or to put it into two questions, before eating, ask how much your body has to work to digest what you are about to eat depending on your personal context and what nutritional value is it going to give you in return in relation to what you need depending on your personal context. I find those better questions than making choices based on the carb, protein and fat values.
