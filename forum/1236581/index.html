Trouble shooting

  • I have a theory that when something goes wrong, in fact at least two things are wrong:
    1) The source of the problem.
    2) The thing that failed to avert the problem.

    Last Saturday I was on course for a productive day at home. I was going through my list, getting many things done, until I got to one task: Do some office work. Now this task I was avoiding, but I picked it because I felt there were some important things I ought to tackle. So I turned on my computer to check my work list.

    I never made it. Instead I mentally checked out. I checked email, surfed the web, and watched DVDs. Out of nowhere, my productive day was zapped.

    1) Why did this happen? The immediate trigger here was the perceived difficulty of the task. I picked up my computer with the notion that I intended to tackle some troubling bit of office work, only I didn't know what work that would be, how long it might take, or whether I could make useful progress at this time. That is a tall order for one task.

    Instead I should have made it a smaller task: Just *study* my office list. Don't *do* anything. In the process of studying the list, I could select something to do, or not. And if I select one, I only write that on my AF list.

    2) Now why didn't I cope well with this trigger? I *should* have been able to recognize this giant task and either put it off, or rise up to the challenge. That didn't happen, and I think Erik's video blog explained why, very elegantly. I needed balance, and a variety of tasks to keep various kinds of energy high. I had lots of energy, but in one respect it was lacking.

    I needed to take a break to recharge. And I needed perspective, to realize that I could freely take that break. This week I'm working on improving my perspective, to cope better in the future.

  • Hi Alan,

    You're not alone, describes what happens to me as well.

    One technique for me, IF I can stop myself wandering off at a tangent to pleasurable things in time, is to use the Salami Technique which has already had several mentions on this forum in the past.

    My version is to start off with a very small slice - 10 mins - and increase subsequent slices by 5 minutes each phases. Six phases results in 10 + 15 + 20 + 25 + 30 +35 = 135 minutes of work and usually ending with a good feeling, and a reward like spending time on this forum.

    Another reason for things going wrong: sh*t happens and it usually rolls downhill.

    A luta continua.
