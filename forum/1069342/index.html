Does anyone have any advice on whether to dismiss or re-enter items? Do many people transfer dismissed items to another list? or leave in your notebook as a reminder for later?

  • Erik,

    I'm assuming that you are not talking about partially completed tasks ... those are immediately reentered at the end of the list (last page).

    If you do a true "dismissal" it happens because there are no tasks on the page that you wish to do. You highlight those and do NOT reenter immediately. You wait a while and then review the dismissed pages to see if there are any highlighted tasks that you now feel ready to do ... THEN you add them to the end of your active list. But only do so once you understand WHY they need to be done and WHY they did not get done before.

  • JD and Alan have the right of it in my mind.

    To me, the dismissal aspect of AF is one of its most powerful.

    It is what helps keep you honest, humble, and realistic about what truly needs your help.

    Sometimes, it is reasonable to highlight and give some thought to an entry that has been dismissed. Perhaps the entry wasn't quite what needed your help, perhaps it was something related. Let your gut be your guide, not your head, nor your heart.

    Dismiss, dismiss, dismiss. I found that following the dismissal rules ruthlessly, that I dismiss less and less. Is this because I am more "productive"? I doubt it. It just I have a more realistic view of what stands out to be entered initially on the list.

    If something is dismissed, that doesn't mean that "task" will leave your attention forever? It might caught your attention again in the future and find its way back onto the list.

    My advice is to stick with the practice long enough and give it as honest a try as possible and see what happens you try to give up "forcing" yourself to do what you think needs to be done and let things stand out on their own.

    Good luck!
