“I’ll do it later because…”

  • See Mark's latest blog entry.

    <<If you’ve got some tasks which are “sticking”, have a look at what you’re saying to yourself when you pass by them. “I’ll do it later because…”

    Then tackle the “because”!">>

    Well isn't this the (dynamic) rub? What if the "because" is: "because I never really enjoyed doing this task/project but now I'm stuck doing it because...". David Byrne captured the spirit of this idea with these phrases:

    And you may ask yourself
    Where is that large automobile?
    And you may tell yourself
    This is not my beautiful house!
    And you may tell yourself
    This is not my beautiful wife!

    Then the real work begins. Perhaps the first item should be: Shred the list.
