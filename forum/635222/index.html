Emotional Upset

  • Hi all,
    I am having the strangest reaction...and am wondering if it is due to AF. I have been doing AF since the first day and find myself doing things I have put off for years. Like, this afternoon I got my ipod speakers working and all set up in my kitchen and I have had these speakers (a xmas present) for something like 3 years!

    I am cleaning out cabinets and drawers and desks---things that have been hanging me up AND repelling me for years..though I really wanted to do them. I am printing out boarding passes for an upcoming trip and packing up the dog's travel bag. All these strange things, way ahead of time, but on my list and mind none the less.

    I think that rather than my usual running away or avoiding or trying to discipline myself, I am really getting stuff done that has bothered me and also that I have all but given up that I would ever be able to do them and I am so surprised by how easy it is and also how important they are to me.

    Now along with all of this good stuff, I find myself crying and feeling lonely, and vulnerable as well. All new feelings. I am wondering if I have found a way to unwedge a rock, and now blocked emotions are flowing? That's what it feels like. Cooking dinner tonight and listening to my ipod on my "new" Xmas speakers brought me to tears and to another era in my life as well. Very tender things. Seeing my armoire with my sweaters culled, de-cat furred, and all folded neatly brings strong emotion as well. Knowing that this whole place will come together and that I will be able to work at my work is incredible to me.

    Does anyone else relate to this? Any similar feelings? Trish

  • Hi Bev

    Thank you for the nice comments. It's funny isn't it how so much of the self-help stuff out there may identify the issues we have surrounding procrastination, perfectionism, attention deficit, stress etc., but leave us thinking, "yes it makes sense but I can't do it!" I think "self-help" is one of the biggest mis-nomers around because the last thing a lot of these books etc do is teach us how to really help ourselves. Knowing the answers isn't enough. Knowing how to help others find the answers is great but leaves us believing that we're the only ones none of this stuff works for - ergo I am a failure.

    And then ............ along comes AF - an innocuous little idea, cunningly disguised in an understated notepad, complete with Bic Biro (Cristal of course Mark :-), and bam! like the proverbial ton of bricks we're hit with an almost addict like desire to "do" - and to do the stuff we've been running from - and it's easy! All of a sudden we know how to "help ourselves" - well maybe not all of a sudden - takes a few days :-) and all of those self-help books can be consigned to recycling, or sold on e-Bay with the TM T-Shirts.

    I think self-help books are great in identifying why but often that's where they leave you. AF identifies why and then says "ok, so what are you going to do about it?" And that's where the choice is made - to go back to where we were or say "hey, so that's my problem - ok, time to let it go, right another item crossed off - next page........"
