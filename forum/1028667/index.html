  • I learned a simple trick for reducing things like books, CDs, DVDs, office files, socks, shirts, tennis-balls and general paper work.

    Keep the items in a 'stack' (some sort of ordered arrangement). Each time I use something it gets moved to the top (or end) of the stack. Over time the stack self-sorts so all the active items are near the top and anything on the bottom has been unused (or less-used) over that period.

    So you can safely dispose of the bottom X% of the stack, knowing it is the least-used portion of the inventory. For me X% is usually 50% - but you can adjust that to suit the circumstances.

    After a few cycles through this process - the stack is small and contains only relevent often-used items.

    This has decluttered my life in many ways.
