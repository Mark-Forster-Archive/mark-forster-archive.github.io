SF - So many pages how I take a head in my work

  • I indeed love SF. Since I tried every method of MF I think it's the best method I ever tried for DOING things. Except one thing. I stay sometime quiet a long time on a page and it blocks me often half a day. When I do my dayly review I often notice I have done some tasks I could really avoid and they took me sometime quiet a long time.

    So, as I red a very interesting page of MF the STOP DOING LIST relayed by Ryan Freckleton thanks to him ! http://www.markforster.net/blog/2007/4/14/action-versus-activity.html

    In MF thread, the idea is to understand the "difference between action and activity" and to avoid " to fall into the activity trap if some principle are revealed.

    I found this topic very interesting and it gave me many ideas about SF.

    I made myself a new rule which is incredible efficient.

    As you know the rule forbit to go to the next page until all tasks in C2 are not achieved" So If I don't feel ready to achieve this C2 column for any reason (most of the time energy available) I simply reformulated each tasks, put it on the C2 column (on the next page as unfinished) and dismiss all the C1 column of my actual page. AND, Then, I turned the page.

    I noticed I erased a lot of tasks in C1 of my active page. Reported a lot of them on my last page on C1, put some real must do tasks on the C2 column of the next page which hes a bit increased, but not too much.

    Of course it went much faster and I could stay on the flow.

    Doing this rule, I dont strictly obey to the rules I guess. But it s so good to finish my work and advance and it force me to put at last many of the dismissed task in a "stop doing list" and let me concentrate on the core of my job.

    I wondered if some of you has the same experience. Mark, what do you think about it ?

    Thanks to all.

  • Wait a minute, Jupiter, why do you dismiss all the C1's (if the page is not full)? It sounds to me like you have perfectly legitimate C1's, and their only shortcoming is that they are getting in your way because you currently have a disproportionate number of urgent tasks on other pages.

    And then, with some of them, you're going a step further and designating them for the "stop doing" list (??). To me, the purpose of the stop-doing list is to weed out the "busy work", or the work that is taking up too much of your time because it is not that important in your grand scheme of things. In other words, the candidates for stop-doing are the types that you <<generally>> do that cause you to waste time and only cause "activity".

    Can't you just continue with your C2's the way you are doing (hopefully, gaining some control in the near future) without messing with your 'normal' C1's so much?
