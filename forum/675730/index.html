  • Hi Emma

    The offer is genuinely there if you ever want to bounce ideas around so do feel free at any time. You are right about 1001 tasks - there's so much to think about, from statutory requirements, to marketing, to producing your "product". Whilst I find that working from home lends itself perfectly to a one list AF approach I would suspect that your intuition regarding a separate New Business list is actually spot on. Although my own project management system has always included project specific task lists, since starting AF those project lists have been "converted" to mini AF lists, which then are handled either as part of a project time block or by means of the individual items being included within AF.

    Using DIT, that would lend itself well to the Current Initiative concept, although as I recall the use of the Current Initiative approach itself would primarily be to get the project up and running as opposed to completing everything. My feeling is that by producing a specific AF task list for "New Business" that will enable you to get all of your thoughts in the one place, and to use the intuitive creativity of AF to move it forward, perhaps within Current Initiative style time blocks. Personally I have gone away from the Current Initiative concept of "the first thing every day" as for me that was too structured but that is me - others on the firum definitely do well with that structure. I am more likely to put "New Business" as a task and then action the specific "New Business" AF list when the task stood out.

    I find Mindmapping is a good tool for creative thinking (and much more fun than spreadsheets!) I do wish you all the best with your new venture. One piece of advice - do remember to have fun with your business - if you get too bogged down in all of the detail it can strangle creativity but from my experience so far AF is a great tool for guarding against that!
