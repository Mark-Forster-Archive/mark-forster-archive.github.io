  • Hi Norman

    Your comment makes perfect sense and as you can probably tell I would defend "pure AF" to the nth degree. However sometimes it is just a fact a fact of life that people do actually need to be in "urgent and important mode" for a period of time.

    I love doing pure AF but the fact however remains that there are times in my life when there is a genuine need to stay in urgent and important mode. That is not by choice but by circumstance. I could just say that those periods are times when I have no discretionery time and therefore leave AF to one side but I prefer to try to use AF as a tool in those times as well.

    I strongly believe that those times should never become the norm because there is no doubt that living in such a way has a massive impact on lifestyle, stress levels, and ultimately health. I would imagine that most people goes through such periods at some time and for me, AF is one of the things that is enabling me to keep, if not always on track, at least to keep the track in sight.
