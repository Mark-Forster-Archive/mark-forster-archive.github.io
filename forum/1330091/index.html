  • Hi Erik! Your questions are reasonable, and your purported answers are reasonable too. Things might work very well doing as you propose. However, the intention for the system was like this:

    If you work on a recurring item, rewrite it on the bottom of the recurring page.
    If you work on but don't finish a an item, whether from the open, closed, or unfinished list, re-enter it in the unfinished list at the bottom.
    Whatever you had in your list is what you rewrite.

    For Unfinished, Mark's intention was NOT to have a list of projects that generate New tasks. The intent was to have modest projects that you work on directly.

    E.g. "Plan huge vacation": Normally you wouldn't leave that on the list. It's too big. "Plan normal vacation" would be a better size because there's less planning. But if you are aiming for a big vacation, "Choose a destination (planning vacation)" is better sized.

    Regardless of which you write, work on exactly what you wrote. As you work on that item, re-enter it in Unfinished until you are finished whatever you called for. (If later you decide it's too big, downsize it so you can get it done.) Then when you finish, you might enter a next step as a new item.
