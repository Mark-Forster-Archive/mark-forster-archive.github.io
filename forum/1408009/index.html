clogged up column2 creates resistance to the system?

  • Mark Forster wrote elsewhere:
    <<<My recommendation is that one aims to do at least one pass through the list a day.>>>

    In response, Alan Baljeu noted that if you are naturally disposed to go more slowly, this tends to bog down the system and FORCES you to continue going slowly, whereas if you are moving along pretty quickly, the system stays lean and SUPPORTS you in moving along pretty quickly.

    I certainly had that experience with Autofocus. But Superfocus mitigates the slow movement somewhat. If I move slowly, then column2 tends to fill up. This forces me to action or dismiss everything in column1, and action everything in column2. This prevents you from clogging things up on too large a scale -- the blockage gets cleared out pretty quickly.

    Thus, SF is somewhat self-regulating, much more so than Autofocus, and that's a good thing.

    However, I have found SF's "you must action column2" approach creates some resistance if I run into that problem too often. And that leads to gaming the system. Instead of writing unfinished tasks in column2, I may be tempted to say, "well, it's done for NOW, so I'll put it in column1 on the last page", when I would have been more naturally inclined to put it in column2 on the next page. Likewise with urgent tasks, when I'd be more naturally inclined to write them down in column2, I am tempted to "wing it" and just try to get it done without writing it down -- which leads to some urgent tasks never getting done because they tend to come in batches and I can't keep them all in my head, and also leads to distractions because once I'm away from my list I tend to stay away.

    Also, if I am already struggling, maybe not feeling well, maybe having a too-busy day with too many meetings or whatever, and I see my column2 getting filled up -- and the next page's column2 ALSO filling up -- just looking at all those tasks that I *MUST* action can create resistance. It's easier just to close the book and go read the forums for awhile.

    For me personally, following the "standing out" rules and strictly following all the processing rules, I do NOT necessarily get through my list at least once a day. It seems this "get through the list at least once a day" is another "rule" that needs to be incorporated somehow.

    None of these things have been a HUGE issue, but they have caused me a bit of trouble here and there.

  • I think there's a misuse here.
    If it's to go in C2, it's either urgent or unfinished, but with respect to these nuances:

    urgent: it should be worked on in the next hour because later than that would be bad. Further, you are now able to work on it.

    Unfinished: you define for each task what part needs to be accomplished before you consider yourself done-for-now. That definition is what you write in C2. Until you reach that point, you do some and reenter in the next column 2. If you *can* no longer work the item at present, you are,de facto, done-for-now. To C1 it goes.

    Now if something was stuck and set aside, and if (eg) an email comes to unstuck it, and it's urgent, use the urgency rule, and the trigger of the email to reinstate it.

    It's important to keep the above terms narrowly defined to keep the flow.

  • Here's an interesting thing I've learn about my column 2 / column 1 usage. YMMV.

    I do a large majority of my work for clients much of which arrives as "urgent" to the client. And that work involves usually 30minutes to 2 hours of net effort spread over 1-2 days. When I start doing my part the tasks moves from column 1 to column 2 per the rules.

    However at the end of most of my tasks is communicate with the client to close out the issue, train the client; or get more info from the client to clear a roadblock etc.

    Often times the client isn't available or is slow to respond. When I hit those instances I now ask myself if the task is still urgent; or is it just active. If it's not urgent but active I will now return it to column 1 as it really is a different task at that point. This assures me I will follow up again on the later passes through the list but it prevents my column 2 from choking with issues that I can't move forward because they are in a wait for customer state.
