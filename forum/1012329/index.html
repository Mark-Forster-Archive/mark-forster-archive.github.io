DWM after 10 days

  • I was going to hold off on doing this post for a while yet but I just realized that today is my AFiversary, so what the heck.

    DWM is growing on me. It was definitely NOT love at first sight like AF4 was (http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/900207). Like many people, in the first day I had concerns about how to work the system, toyed with putting items due in less than a week or a month on their hard due dates (SorryMarkSorrysorrysorry). Predictably, I came 'round to doing it the "right way" - just like with every other tweak I've ever tried of one of Mark's systems. (Actually, that's not quite true... I did consistently not dates of line-drawing, dismissal, and # of items reviewed in AF4.) It's definitely NOT been love at first sight the way AF4 was, and I have some concerns about what will happen with weekends when I often have an hour, tops, to work on my combined work/home list. For the moment, though, I'm three days "ahead" (that is to say, no open tasks before 2/14 with only 2 expiring on 2/15 and 2/16 being completely done) and moving forward on a wide variety of things all at once, which I like.

    As I mentioned elsewhere (http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/1012130 ), initially the "going backwards to re-enter a task" element took a little while to get used to. This had nothing to do with the dates element, but just with the physical act of turning pages BACKWARDS to re-enter something. I've mostly gotten over it at this point, and just feel for the paperclip that i know to be at the top of the page and run with it.

    I don't know if I'll stay with DWM long-term, but I'll give it a go for at least a month and then see how I'm feeling about it.

    My poor AF notebook that I've been using since the beginning is on its last legs - even the duck tape that's been holding it together is failing, and there are only five empty pages left. I think I'm going to celebrate my AF-iversary by starting a new book. This one has had a good run.

    "Marty H:

    << The one about something with a due date less than 30 days out seems easy: Add it on the last day it can be done. Include the due date in brackets which is what I do now in AF4 like this: [17 Feb].     Because:

    1) It was one of the things I tried out when I was developing the system, and it didn't work at all well. Mainly I think because it destroyed the closed list aspect of the system, and therefore much of its effect.

  • A more detailed explanation: Only the next 7 days are really the issue. Following Mark's rule, everything between now and 5 days hence are tasks you've been ignoring. Tasks on the sixth day are largely those you worked on recently. The closed list effect is to show you what you've been avoiding. Writing tasks on the due date masks that.

    (just something I'm figuring out today)

    Don’t forget that the DWM due date is not the start date. If you have an item due Feb 25, what’s the difference between writing it on the Feb 25 page vs. the March 5 page? You still have to work on it before Feb 25 (unless it’s something that can ONLY be done on Feb 25, in which case, it probably should be a calendar appointment instead). When you work on it today, you’ll cross it off the March 5 page and continue it on the Feb 19 page (BEFORE the Feb 25 due date BTW :) )

    I’ve been putting tasks on my lists (home & work) according to Mark’s +30 and +7 (ok, I use +28 at work instead, but that’s the only time tweak I’ve done) for items that have a hard due date of anywhere from now+15minutes (definitely before the +30 date :) ) to a date after the DWM expire date. I’ve met all the hard dates in the 2 weeks. At work, the list is on Excel and I have the hard date in parentheses and in colored text as part of the task description. At home, the list is on paper and there too I write the date as part of the task.
