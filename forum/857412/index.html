AF2 and the Calendar

  • I'm trying to really simplify things and the one elusive issue I have is what to do with repeating tasks that are date-specific. For example, I want a reminder to backup my website every Friday. I don't really need to make an appointment on my calendar for it because it's pretty much a 5 minute job to click through the web page and start the backup. But if I don't have a reminder somewhere, it will be a month or so before I remember to do that - eeek!

    On the flip side, putting it on my AF list doesn't work out so well because I am cycling through my entire list in about 3 days (AF2 helped me to get caught up to where my earliest tasks are only about five days behind). So the backups task actually goes on the chopping block for dismissal about 2 days before it is needed.

    I thought about using another app that does repeating tasks like Toodledo or RTM, but the thought of yet another tool to keep track of is not appealing. I like the simplification of my calendar and my notebook.

    I am thinking maybe I should put it as an untimed event on my calendar, but not let it repeat...just move it forward to the following week when it's complete. That would reduce clutter on my calendar (Google Calendar) but still leave the reminder. I'm just afraid if I actually implemented this that I'd have a lot of "all-day" events scattered through out my calendar that make it cluttered anyway.

    Anyone have any thoughts on this?

    And while I'm thinking out loud...I've been experimenting with blocking off time on my calendar for specific projects. I've been tagging tasks in my work notebook with a 3-letter client code in all caps so if I have a client file open, I can quickly scan the list for their code and find their tasks. This works well in theory, but scheduling the project doesn't seem intuitive to me.

    Yesterday, for example, I had planned to work on project ABC for 3 hours in the afternoon but before that time block rolled around, I ran across something that needed attention on project XYZ so I just went ahead and worked on XYZ until I came to a stopping point and then I worked on ABC for 2 hours or so. So I feel like I did the right thing by altering my schedule to accommodate XYZ, but the scheduling part seemed like a waste since I had to change it anyway. I guess what I like about the schedule is that it gives me an idea of what I need to focus on and it keeps me from overbooking (all my projects are based on estimates that I provide the client so I know about how much time I need to block off per project). It also helps for billing purposes to see the time blocks on my calendar for each client and it is helpful for estimating when I will complete a project.

    Anyone have any thoughts on this?

  • Hi Gretchen

    Low tech. does it for me.

    I just use the tickler file. An A6 card (or an A4 sheet) for every task that recurs less often that every 3-4 days. (AF deals directly with more regularly recurring tasks.)

    This way I can just reinsert the card every 5, 10, 15, 20 days, monthly, bi-monthly, yearly etc. (This is especially useful if I want to do something on about the same day in every month). I can reword the task whenever I want. I can include instructions on the card as to how to do the task. I can throw away the card whenever the task is no longer required. I can include addresses, phone numbers, personal details. Some of my cards contain everything that is relevant to a small project.

    "Empty Tickler" is a part of my daily routine. Usually more than half of the items are done on the spot and the rest are entered into AF.

    I rarely use my calendar for any tasks that are not time specific.

    I have never found a higher tech. solution that serves me nearly as well as my tickler.
