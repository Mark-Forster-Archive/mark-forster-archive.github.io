  • Hi Gordon. Entering a task 1 month away from today doesn't mean you don't touch it again till 1 month away. Every day between now and 1 month from now, you should be cycling through the DWM list and, as Moises says, the task is then re-entered at 1 week away if you don't complete it all. So if you cycle through the entire list every day, you will have had about 30 opportunities to take action on a task before its 1-month deadline arrives; or if you only cycle through the entire list once a week you will still have have 4 opportunities to take action. If a particular task has had no action taken on it by the time you reach the day the task is due to expire, you have to take some action that day (even a tiny bit) and re-enter it at the day 1 week from now, or it gets dismissed. That's how DWM is supposed to work.

    If you have fast-paced work, then it's even more important to cycle quickly through the list, so that you're constantly reaching the newer items at the end. Because you don't need to action any item on your list until it "stands out", in theory you can quickly skim through from the oldest item to the newest.

    It may be that one of the other Autofocus versions would suit you better and you could try each in turn. Best of luck with your efforts.

  • Gordon, it sounds like you think you are supposed to focus on tasks with today's date. This was a common confusion early in DWM, and completely false. The dates are the Last Possible date to START doing a little bit of work. The system does not track due dates or date completed.

    You should every day look at all tasks on your list, and every day do ANY that feel ready. This includes that one where info came in today for a task dated next week. With DWM the very idea is to work ahead.
    12 July 2010 at 13:13 | Alan Baljeu
  • The dates have very little to do with anything. They are there to help you place three bookmarks or tabs: one for today, one for a week from now, and one for a month from now. Then the rules take over. Add things at a month. Re-add things at a week. Expire or dismiss things today.

    Work through the entire list. Hopefully you get through it at least once a day. If your workflow is lost of small tasks, you probably go through it more than once a day. You will likely spend most of your time either completing things at the end of the list in one shot or things at or before the one week point using a little and often approach.

    For me, things that are not completed soon after they are entered are either left until they are dismissed or are acted upon right away and worked on using little and often until they are completed. I don't have a lot of things that get their first action 2-3 weeks after they are added to the list.

    Hope this helps.
