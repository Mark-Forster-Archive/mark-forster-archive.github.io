  • Ralph,

    If you come back to a page and don't find ANYTHING on that page that you want to do, then ALL items on that page are "dismissed". You highlight them and mark the page as "inactive" and don't go back to it any more.

    So your page may have most items crossed off because they were competed (or started and moved to another page for further work). You may have hit that page many times, but each previous time you did at least ONE item on the page. Eventually, you MUST cross off all items or decide you are not going to do the last few so you are forced to dismiss them. If you have 30 items on a page, you might visit that page 30 times (at most) to get all 30 done. But you really can't visit it more than that. If you have 29 items done and on the 30th time through you decide not to do the last, it is dismissed!

    Of course, you can do multiple items per page review, but you get the idea. The number of items on the page is the maximum number of times you really should visit that page if you do only one item each time.

    You may decide at some later date to go back and review your closed pages and "reactivate" some of those dismissed items. To do that you just add them to the end of your list and they re-enter the system and are done or possibly dismissed again because they wound up not getting acted on.

    Dismissing items is crucial to the working of AF. If you don't do it, you'll never close pages and they will grow until they get big and mean and attack you in your sleep! ;-)
