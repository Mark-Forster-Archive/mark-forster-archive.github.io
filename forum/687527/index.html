The Biggest Surprise

  • Yesterday, I came across the shelf where all the books on time management etc. are that I have collected over more than 20 years. And to my own surprise, a thought popped up in my head like: "Now I can dispose of them all".

    The next moment, I said to myself: "Wait, what's going on here?" I used to believe that time management - all this methods and theories and so on - had become some kind of a hobby, even a passion to me. I always liked to experiment with different concepts and methods.

    But this seems to fade away. I look at these books and feel I am no longer interested in the subject. Thinking of it, I realized I haven't had one single look on all the "life hacker self organization super productivity" websites I used to visit, not since I started with AF.

    So, time management wasn't really a passion. It was a quest, a search. And once you've found what you were looking for, it's over. And this is how I feel in the moment. It's amazing!
