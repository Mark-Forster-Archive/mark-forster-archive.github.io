  • This is what I have decided to do - sort of prompted by one of my big customers who I do a lot of work for and who has asked for a weekly list of outstanding tasks:

    1. Create a list for either each customer or project. So I will have a list for customer X which I can share with him and a list for project Y, and so on for all customers and projects.

    2. Have an AF list which contains ad hoc tasks, and a list of both customers and projects.

    3. Work the AF list, and when I get to customer X or project Y, check that list and pick something to work on.

    The benefit to me of this is that the AF list will be kept much shorter and this I will find easier to manage. My projects/customers tend to have some longevity to them so don't get dismissed as often as other people's tasks, but this will work OK.

    I have a very attractive filofax and have now got a 10 part index to put into it, and have a daily diary in the front. 10 sections is plenty for AF + customers/projects. I use outlook diary on a daily basis (things often get moved around so this is convenient and I can also sync with my iPhone). I will put AF and customer/projects into individual sections at the back of this filofax. This means I can check my AF list at the start of every day and bring 3-4 tasks into my daily diary page to do, then when I have done them I can cross them off and go get some more.

    The outcome is that I will have a list for each project customer, I have control over AF, I end up with a list of my activity to give satisfaction at the end of the day, and it all sits in one place - my filofax (I have an A5 one so it has plenty of room to do all this).

    Wish me luck! I am aboutbhalf way through setting this up - I keep having to some work in between setting it all up!
