  • Your intuition is lying to you. :-) The line comes after page 2 because this is an open list, not a closed (page-oriented) list like AF1.

    From introduction to AF2 blog comments - Mark's post:

    "Unactioned block" simply means the oldest group of contiguous tasks which haven't been deleted or dismissed. If you look at your existing AF list, what is the oldest task that is still awaiting action? Is it immediately followed by another task which is awaiting action? If it's not, then it's an "unactioned block" of one task. If it's immediately followed by other tasks that are awaiting action then they form an "unactioned block" of more than one task.

    I think the instructions can be misinterpreted because he says this in the AF2 instructions (capitalized letters mine):

    Dismissing Tasks
    At the beginning of each day, go to your oldest active PAGE, and draw a line after the first block of unactioned tasks (i.e. the oldest tasks that are still awaiting action).

    If you were doing an electronic version of AF2, you wouldn't have any page breaks. So you would have to use p. 2 as part of the contiguous group of tasks.

    Who knows, I'm probably wrong. I often am. :-)

  • Andreas:

    <<Since the dismissal rule for AF1 is not used at all in RAF, I would say no.>>

    You are correct that **dismissal** in AF1 is not used in AF, but the mechanics of the **dismissal rule** are.

    Now, instead of the closed list of a page being dismissed when unactioned, the closed list of a page is the trigger to return to the end of the list and restart in Reverse Mode.

    Say, for example, you have a 50 page list, and Page 1 has three unactioned items at the bottom and Page 2 has five unactioned items at the top. As you work the end of your list in Reverse Mode you realize that "Switch to Forward Mode" feels ready to be done (good thing too, because page 1 and 2 still have unactioned items!). When you switch to FM you scan page 1, then you go through it item by item. If none of these actions feel ready to be done then you return to RM at the end of your list.

    Now, if the line drawn that morning is at the bottom of page 1, after the three contiguous items, then all is well and dandy. Those items will be put on review tomorrow, and the five items at the top of page 2 will have a line drawn under them, ripe for review. Hopefully they, or some, will be picked off when you switch to FM tomorrow.

    But, if the line was drawn under the *EIGHT* continuous (and not contiguous) items spanning page 1 and page 2, then the next morning all eight items would be put on review.

    Unless you worked backwards from page 50 to page 2 you wouldn't have had an opportunity to action those items.

    It seems to me that if the line is drawn after truly contiguous items, meaning the blocks don't span pages, the integrity of the system is better preserved. Otherwise the "switch to FM" rule loses its utility whenever a block spans two pages and the first portion isn't ready to be done.

    Just my take.
