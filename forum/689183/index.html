Is this discussion forum reaching its limit?

  • On the first page of this forum, we have something like 50 threads all posted to in the past 5 days. Unless you're checking in every day and glancing at each new thread, it's impossible to keep up. I'm fascinated by Autofocus and its users, would like to get more involved in the community. Sadly I don't have time to keep track of so many uncategorised discussions.

    Is the time right to move this discussion to a forum with categories? It might be useful to have separate areas to discuss for example: digital, paper, the "philosophy" of autofocus, autofocus at home, autofocus at work, beginner's questions, advanced hacks and tips, and so on. Having these areas would make it easier to keep track of discussions -- and I think would also spark a wider variety of interesting discussions too.

    It would also mean that newcomers to the system and site could find information they were looking for more easily. Currently I'm not sure this forum is the right place to send somebody who's just thinking about Autofocus.

    I'd be happy to do some of the technical work setting up a different forum, if you (and particularly Mark) think it would be helpful. For a very easy and powerful solution, a Ning group might work well.
