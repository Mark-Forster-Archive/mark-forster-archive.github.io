The Redundancy of Recurring Tasks

  • I've hesitated to jump in to AF because of recurring tasks; figured I'd ask for some guidance instead of just bailing on the idea.

    I have a large number of fairly routine tasks to do on a daily or weekly or monthly schedule, and I've got them tidily set up in an online task tracker. Mark has said to just add these tasks to the list and process along with everything else, but I find myself really balking at the idea of rewriting 15 or 20 or more tasks every day. It seems like a hugely redundant effort, and means I'd have to maintain two systems (online tasks plus the AF list) instead of just one.

    Is there an effective way to manage this without rewriting a ton of tasks, and without maintaining two systems? I understand that AF could be a great way to work through all the routine tasks as well as emergent interesting ones; I'm just balking at having to constantly rewrite them.

    Thanks,
    Beth

  • Good suggestion Margherita, there are often routine tasks that we would not want to add ad infinitum, such as "walk the dog" "eat lunch" etc. However, Mark suggests initially adding these recurrings tasks into the system to allow the intuitive nature of AF to work with those items as it may identify the fact that some of them are unnecessary (not "walk the dog" or "eat lunch" of course :-) ), but what I found was it enabled to see that some of my routine tasks could be managed in a better way. I initially put a few items like "do recurring tasks" on my list but found that they depressed me and became "ought to's" in my mind. By listing them individually and allowing the system to select when I did them, I found that rather than resent them, I actually enjoyed doing most of them and also that my mind was not whingeing because it didn't want to do them but was excitedly looking at other possibilities and coming up with new ideas .....

    I think you may well find that those routines will just slip away, either by being done naturally as "part of life" or as being found to be unnecessary. If there are routine items that you need to keep track of in another list then I would suggest "review recurring list" - for me the slight rephrasing from "do" to "review" seems to reduce resistance. hope that helps ....

  • Thanks for the replies. I've thought it over some more and realized a few things:

    -I was very attached to my old system, and seeing AF as flawed because it didn't work that way, rather than trying it out. It's a more radical shift in perspective than I was expecting, I think.

    -If I just rewrite each recurring task again as I complete it, that takes about 3 seconds and isn't the oppressive burden I had in mind; I was envisioning starting every day rewriting everything on my online task list. But I don't actually have to do that.

    -And if I rewrite recurring tasks as I complete them, the system maintains itself; I don't need to maintain all my daily routine tasks in the online system--so I can just maintain one system instead of two. I'll just need to check the online system every so often for less-frequent tasks (weekly or monthly or annual) and it should be pretty slick.

    So now I'm getting my autofocus list together to give it an honest try.
