Why AF4 is the best one yet.

  • For me, AF4 is the best version yet.

    RAF didn't work all that well for me for a couple of reasons. I suffered from languishing middle pages, probably not as badly as folks with longer lists, but I still suffered from them. More than that, though, the dismissal procedure simply didn't work for me. I think that if my working circumstances were completely predictable it would be ok, but for the bulk of the time that I was using RAF, I was doing the at-home-mom routine (which is not my norm) and my discretionary time for the sort of tasks that I put on my AF list varied from a couple of hours a day to a couple of minutes a day. Having a time-based dismissal procedure at that time, honestly, meant that I abandoned dismissal altogether because I would have ended up dismissing truly important things that my childcare situation had simply prevented me from acting on!

    But AF4. AF4 is like a dream. I love the combination of closed and open lists. My previous concerns about how intuition would work with the open list have receded as I have used the system more. (At the time that I had those concerns, I hadn't yet triggered a review of the closed list.) I am getting a good mix of urgent and less-urgent, work and personal tasks done every day, with remarkably little resistance. Unlike some others, I like the short-term review of items rather than letting tasks sit there highlighted for weeks or months. (In previous versions, I always had "enter reminder into calendar at a more appropriate time" as one of my options during review - basically turning my calendar into a tickler file.) Now I either delete things entirely, rephrase and re-enter, or continue use my calendar as a tickler file.

    As I mentioned elsewhere, I haven't been tracking how often I draw a new line, but it's roughly 2x/week. (I think I'll start noting how long they stay as closed lists just for curiosity's sake.) I have a mix of closed lists that have been completed and ones that have been reviewed - when I have reviewed there have been as few as 6 and as many as 17 un-actioned tasks on the list at the time of review. The numbers of reviewed items have been going down as I have worked with AF4 longer but we'll see what happens long-term.

    In short, well-done Mark, and thanks!
