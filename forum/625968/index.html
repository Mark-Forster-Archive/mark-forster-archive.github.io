Proctrastination busting

  • Here is one of handful of examples of where AF has allowed me to complete tasks that I have previously procrastinated over. I am posting this as a testimonial and also to see if others have found the same effect.

    For 18 months I have procrastinated over backing up my photos. As soon as I thought about how to do this my mind would fill up with questions of technology, hardware, software, time, cost etc and instead I have lived with the risk of losing some very important memories of my 3 young children.

    One of the first items on my AF list was "Back-up photos". Well, tonight on day 6 I have now completed this task. I have used a back-up feature that was already part of my photo software and CD-RWs I already had in my possession. In total the task took me just 1 hour and cost me nothing- and I was able to do other things on my laptop whilst the CDs were burning in the background.

    I will let the pychologists explain how we can allow our minds to play such tricks that such an important task that required such minimum effort can fester undone.

    And I thank Mark for giving us a system that seemingly allows this effect to be overcome with pen, paper and a very small amount of brain power.

    Time will tell whether this effect is lasting. If it is then I am fortunate to be involved so early in something that could be revolutionary to the way we approach our work and play.
