  • Hi Renfrew. I'm using DWM but I know that a lot of people are finding AF4 best for them.

    There's been a huge amount of discussion on this forum on how to handle projects, and perhaps others can direct you to that. AF4 and all the other Autofocus versions are not specifially project management systems, but they can help you to manage your time while you are working on your projects. It's not impossible to handle alll your projects by putting the individual tasks directly into AF4 or any of the other Autofocus systems, and I think some people manage to do this, but I think it's easier to keep your projects in whichever medium suits your way of working, whether that's a manual system or an online system, and just put something like "Work on Project X" as your entry on AF4. When you come to that item and feel moved to action it, then go to your project and work on it for whatever length of time feels right, then re-enter it on the list.

    Some people set aside a block of time every day to work on one or more projects, and only work their Autofocus list when they've completed that time block.

    Best wishes for your projects, and keep us posted on what you decide.

  • same as Jupiter and Margaret:

    in addition:
    I put project reference and actions in a paper based foldersystem (hanging files), when the support material is exclusive paper and in a computer based foldersystem when the support material is digital.
    In this files I collect supportmaterial and a sheet of paper or an textfile (plaintext) with possible next steps - let's say an actionlist. It's important to decide between paper and digital for hosting the actionlist. Two places leads to less productivity and more confusion.
    The files (paper) or folders (digital) got a tags. I name the hanging file for a Project 'P1_projectname' and the digital folder 'C1_projectname'.
    On my AF list I write e.g. 'P1: do task' or 'C1: do task'. Thus I see at a glance where I find the support material and the actionlist.
    As I work on my AF list and the entry stands out I do the task and refer to the Folder named 'P1_projectname' for more possible tasks which I could enter to my AF list, for reviewing and unclutter supportmaterial and for brainstorming the next tasks or whatever.

    I've got some success with the following.

    Discretionary time (of my choice): Use AF in the moleskin
    Time oriented (responsibility for producing a result by date x): Use Things ((http://culturedcode.com/things/) or similar app which supports contexts/tagging.


    All projects with tasks with deliverable dates go into Things (e.g Client X > create logo mock-up) , all else goes into my AF moleskin (e.g get haircut, send in coupon)


    I know you won't have specific knowledge of the Things app so this is a generic way I think that can be applied to all digital project apps.

    Though I failed in the past using software, I applied the AF task selection approach to the program.

    1. In 'view all tasks mode' use the AF task selection approach
    2. Selected those tasks and move them to the 'focus' section of the application. (Things 'Today view')
    3. Those not done get unmarked and start again at 1.

    One of the benefits of projects with tonnes of child tasks and dependencies is that you can categorise them. I think in the past this was for priority and the reason I failed. Now I mark every task with one of the following tags from each line (no due dates)

    - Milestone (if i only get this done today the day is worth it, Task (bonus to get done)
    - Easy, Effort, Challenge
    - 1day, 3hr, 1hr, 20m, 15m, 5m

    For Project titles only (with due dates), I add
    - 1, 2, 3, 4 etc (these represent the urgency of the deadline, 1 being the soonest)

    What these tags do is allow me in the app to filter down the overwhelming list into something more AF like on paper. Again I mentioned this because I procrastinate and one of the reasons was multi-tasking and computers by there nature encourage it, one little email here, a web site there and the days over for me on journey procrastinate.

    So....in the Things app I simply filter all my projects and tasks by entering.

    1, Milestone

    and then can sort these out by time based on the block of time available. This works because I might have a day with only a few hours available versus a whole day.

    Once the milestones are done I then can go

    1, Tasks

  • For most multi-step projects, the actions become apparent as I start to work on them. For the complex ones, buying a house, for instance, I need a separate project plan.

    Your system is likely different from mine, but my notes and personal Autofocus list reside in a moleskine notebook: Notes at the front, and the list at the back. Any bigger goals that I need to flesh out or have multiple moving parts are given a page in the front section as needed. My Autofocus list item will usually be the name of the project and the page number of my notes on the topic [Buy a new house (page x).] When I come to that list item, I know that my project planning and notes are with me in the same notebook. If I find that a project is getting passed over, I'll cut down some of the resistance by rephrasing the project as an action, still referencing the page number [Call realtor re: house tour (page x)].

    I've found this approach simple and effective. Now if I can somehow integrate a paper calendar (I'm currently reliant on Google), I'll have really found what works for me.

  • @Ryan

    I don't process the tasks in my notebook strictly according to AF or DWM rules.

    The right hand 'must do' page for each day is like a calendar, DIT list, and daily focus list. It's for stuff I really need to get done today

    The left hand 'may do' page for each day is like a quasi AF/DITW (do it this week) list :-) I can work on it ahead of time like DWM or in the past like AF, but I'm pretty flexible in how I choose.

    As an example my PC got a virus yesterday, so on the 'must do' (right hand) page for today was a task to restore the backup. I also decided to try a different antivirus so put this task on the 'may do' (left hand) page for this Thursday as it's not so urgent and can wait a few days.

    As it happened I changed antivirus this evening anyway so ticked it off early. But if I'd left it I'd get a timely reminder in a few days time.

    Something else I find useful is when a page is 'closed out' with no tasks remaining, I fold or clip the corner. Then it's easy to see which pages are still open with tasks to do, but there's some incentive to get those pages closed out and their corners folded.
