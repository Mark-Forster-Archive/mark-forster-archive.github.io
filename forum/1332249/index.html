AF4R after one dismissal

  • When Mark laid out AF4R I had been itching for a change anyway - I'd even gone back to AF1 for about an hour. I've been a very dedicated DWM user since it came out - for my lifestyle, the timed dismissal works MUCH better than the no-action dismissal. That said, I've now been using AF4R through one dismissal and I'm happy enough with it that I'm going to keep using it for a while. One think I've had to do is a slight rethinking of dismissal - at one point yesterday I had only strictly at-home things left on my "old" list so I just ignored it until I was actually at home. As it turned out they all got dismissed anyway, but I didn't do so until I had considered and rejected them in a context where that was meaningful. I'm also preserving a bit of a DWM escape hatch... I'm still giving new things a due date of a month from now and unfinished/recurring things 1w due dates.

    So anyway...with that first dismissal I got rid of 3 things, and my current list stands at 28 old, 3 new, 8 recurring, and 17 unfinished.

  • Sarah and RogerJ:

    One useful application for a single-list system like AF and its derivatives is that one can use it to take notes in meetings. What you do is to write every worthwhile point as a task.

    Examples:

    "J's Division made £400,000 profit this quarter"
    "Read hand-out about safety implementation"
    "Self to run Summer Ball this year"
    "New Government regulations may affect market"
    "Target for next quarter £350,000 profit"
    "Nominations for Safety Officer to J by Monday"

    Appropriate action can then be taken, such as entering in records, reading, diarising, considering implications, etc.

    You can also note your thoughts as tasks during the meeting and subsequently do something about them:

    "Bill: four branches in Nevada?"
    "No biscuits again!"
    "WEDDING ANNIVERSARY!!!!"
    "Pick up dry cleaning"
    "Annex A and B transposed - Why?"
    "Kanban??"
