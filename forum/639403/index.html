  • The beauty about AF is that you only have to start a task to cross it off. If you haven't finished it, you just write it again at the end of the list.

    So you just need to do something small and usually when faced with the choice of doing something, anything on the project and dismissing it, suddenly doing something small doesn't seem so bad. It could even be look at the project and think of one small thing to do to write at the end of AF.

    You'll find that most times, the resistance to looking at as task is even bigger than doing it so what AF does is breaks down the initial resistance and often the work flows from that. Even if it is still painful, at least if you're chipping away at it, eventually it will get to the point where it becomes easy to finish which is far more progress than just ignoring it.

    Yesterday, I was coming to the end of one of my pages and I had a task I'd been procrastinating on for a while and could easily have procrastinated on for another week or more. I forced myself just pull out the files and do 1 thing. I ended up spending 2 hours on it and now my resistance to going back to it (it's a several day task) is much smaller if none at all. As I hadn't finished, it went back on the end of my AF list. I can't wait to get to it again when it turns up - THAT's progress!

  • Amanda

    Have you tried rephrasing any of those tasks that you don't want to dismiss? It is fine to rephrase a task, enter it onto the last page and cross it out from the page it was on. I have found if I have a task on my list such as "Do xyz" and I find myself completely resisting it I often rephrase it to "think about xyz" or in the most desperate case you could try "think about why I'm resisting xyz".

    It may seem a very vague thing to write but I find that is the beauty of Autofocus. It seems to let you mull over things like that in the back of your mind while working on other things and when you return to the list and see "think about xyz" often something will have shifted and you can see a way over, round or through a task. Or at the very least realised why you're resisting it and perhaps come up with a partial solution for the resistance.

    Try it and see?

    Good luck

  • Interesting thread, again.

    As I work abroad on my work assignments, my contexts/places are pretty well-defined, I have a Defer To Later tag because I cannot do them whilst on assignment even if I wanted to.

    Example: Internet access is so slow and fragile here in southern Zambia, iI am not going into my company's online bank and credit card accounts to monitor activities. So I've deferred them and set a reminder in Outlook to bring them back to the attention of my AF system. These particular items, along with several others, will reappear on Friday evening next week when I have a six hour stopover with free wi-fi access in Johannesburg on my way home.

    So I check these items off now and don't worry about them because my own AF hybrid is tracking them.

    My procrastination trap is those on which I cannot make a conscious decision to Defer, so they sit there until they get Dismissed or Done.

    In case others are wondering about these contexts I use, I have restricted them to a few:
    @Assignment/Client
    @Errand (I may ditch this and treat it another way, eg write [ERRAND] next to the task in AF so it stands out when I have time and place for errands, a form of batching if you like.)
    @Home
    @Home Office (what used to be a spare bedroom, most personal tasks are under the previous one)
    @Travel/Airport/Lodge
