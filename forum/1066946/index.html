Old Envelopes as a productivity aid

  • I've been trying an adaptation of 'pure' AF for a while and it seems to be bearing fruit for me... it also happens to be more environmentally friendly than methods I've used in the past.

    I keep a full list a la AF in a normal notebook and process it according to normal AF rules.

    The only divergence from AF is that every day I take an old envelope - from junk mail, letters, bills, whatever - from the pile that I keep in a drawer and write a list of the things that I need to do on that day. I try to keep the list short - just the stuff that I really want / need to get done that day, and one or two additional entries - Process AF list and Drain Email. If I have time-bound things to do like meetings or customer visits, I sketch a little timeline somewhere on the envelope.

    Then, in the standard AF way, I do the things that feel right. If that means doing things in bursts, that works too. If I feel the need to do something else, then I have the escape of the 'process AF' entry.

    If additional work shows its head, then I write it (per AF) onto the end of my AF list. Or if its 'today urgent', I add it to the envelope.

    So far, it seems to be working well. Most days I have the satisfaction of ending the day with a completely crossed-out list, telling my subconscious that its OK to finish the day's work and not secretly worry that I should be doing 'one more thing'.

    I think that the reason it works is precisely because I'm using the backs of scrappy old reused envelopes that don't feel like they are part of a 'system' or a 'process', so I'm not tempted to get sucked into thinking about the means rather than the end.

    Also, having a list that is clearly limited to the things that it is feasible to finish in a day removed the feeling of Overwhelm. Ironically, it puts a nice envelope around the day's work :-)
