  • Crown,

    Try to approach this with a fresh mind. Don't try to make AF look like GtD. If it did look like it, then it would BE it. ;-)

    AF is a way of organizing tasks so that they get done in a timely manner. It is not heavy in planning and scheduling. It focuses on presenting tasks to you frequently so that you can decide when the time is right for doing it.

    Since it ignores scheduling and does not speak to calendars, agendas, tickers, projects, filing systems, the proper file folders and labelers, those things are outside of AF proper. But that does not mean you can't do any of those things in your overall system.

  • Worked AF4 style all day. Man, this systems rocks! It really works!

    I put non-active-agendas simpliy on the calendar and active ones on the A4-list and dealth with them straight away!

    Someday/Maybe and planning I just stored on the computer in mindmaps and so forth and I felt like it had nothing to do with the action part.

    Important things automatically got more actions to it, and I worked little & often at bigger projects I never do something about before the deadline.

    While GTD is good at organizing, I think it is somewhat outdated as nowadays one can do anything on the computer (context has lost it's meaning), and the problem is procrastination and no sense of accomplishment (as projects can go on practically forever). AF4 let's you do some here, some there without thinking much about it, and you get this sense of moving projects/tasks forward all day long.

    I use MyLifeOrganized as a simple outliner and mark tasks for review or point out the current task (so I know where to begin when I come to my computer again) with simple flags.

    Feel that this system is sort of strange and magical at the same time. Anyhow; as self-employed plus a student is just works. I focus on the list and work the progress and that's it. Little and often, never cramping before the deadline!

  • Ties, and AdreasE,

    The one thing about Someday/Maybe lists, though, that seem to work better outside of AF is their ability to "hold" really, really, long-term possibilities. And I use the word "possibility" with strong emphasis.

    For instance, putting "buy yacht" on an AF list seems a little odd. Assuming one isn't a millionaire, that is. Certainly most of us won't "feel ready" to buy the yacht when we get to it on our list, and it will end up highlighted. But now we have "buy yacht" highlights interspersed with other highlights such as "create budget" and "learn Spanish". It seems, to me that highlight AF items DO generate a useful list. . . but maybe more of a NOW?/LATER? list, rather than a someday/maybe list.

    A separate someday/maybe list for those really big longshot items (climb Everest!) seems to be helpful to me. It doesn't clutter up the AF list with things I don't really intend on doing now, or might never intend to do. Constantly passing over those items while I am working through AF irks me a bit - I don't know why.

    Thoughts?
