  • I've been meaning to do a blog post on contexts, which I think just may be the most misunderstood part of GTD. There's a tendency to over-elaborate and obsess over just the right context for each task, which then becomes one more way for people to tinker with their systems instead of doing their actual work. Then they turn around and decide that contexts are useless and declare that they're not going to use them. A better approach is to assign contexts as and when they present themselves -- precisely when it becomes clear that you can't do a task at your present location or with the resources available at that moment, but will be able to later under different circumstances. Or that it just makes sense after you've done one sort of task (say an e-mail or a phone call) to go ahead and get the others like it out of the way while you've got the tools at hand. Every time you make a grocery list you're using a context-based action list, and the benefit of not having "buy milk" and "buy coffee" and "buy bread" scattered randomly among all your other to-dos should be obvious even to people who claim they don't use contexts. Make them work for you, which is exactly what Donna was doing when she started a separate Autofocus list for her basement.

  • Bob: That is a very good idea. Keep it all in one book.

    I have my main AF list in the front of the book, but it will have a task stating:"Work on reporting May 2010 (1)". The (1) refers to a reference at the back of the book where the project is listed with all its sub-tasks. The project steps will be in a AF format.

    I do not think this deviates from Mark Foster's idea of getting the right/left brain together, because from my main AF list I will decide that I am ready for project: "Reporting May 2010 (1)" and I can then decide in the sub-AF list what task I am specifically in the mood of doing.

    I do sometimes include project related steps/tasks in my main AF list, but I make sure that all projects are listed in my main AF list.

    Note: The using of the word "project" above relates to an outcome involving more than one step. "Phone John" is not a project and will only be on main AF list, but if my project is "World Domination" and phoning John is a necessary step in this project, then it will be included in the project list at the back.
