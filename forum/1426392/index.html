SF: Hacks to use the list

  • In a previous threat, Mark wrote:

    <<I use my list all the time - literally from the time I get up to the time I go to bed. I've found it to be incredibly strong and resilient in all circumstances. It worked perfectly for me when I had children and grandchild around, when I was testing out the system, when I was ill over the last week, and is still working perfectly now>>

    In theory, I would like to use SF, hell any system, as religiously as Mark. However I resist, I'm not interested in analyzing why I do, but I do. However, without a holistic approach, it's too easy to drift from using SF. So I'm going to try and do the following:

    I will time (via a stopwatch) the amount of time I do things while sticking to SF rules. Each night, I'll make a note of how long I worked within an SF environment. By the end of the week, I should have a good idea of how much time I spent working from my lists, and how well my projects are doing. I'm hoping this will serve as a catalyst to keep working from my lists.

    Any other ideas (besides: "Just do it" and/or "Use SF all day and night)?
