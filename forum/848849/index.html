How to bookmark

  • I would like to be able to switch quickly between the first uncompleted page, the task I am currently working on, and the final page. Oh, and similarly with the end of the book, where I keep a separate list for planning meetings. Post-its are a little big. Paper clips - well, there are two sides to those! Dots (Mark's way) - require finding the page the dot is on in the first place.

    So what are the rest of you using for this?
    29 July 2009 at 13:43 | Laurence
  • How about Post-it tabs? Something like these:

    http://www.3m.com/us/office/postit/products/prod_ft_arr.html

    or these:

    http://www.3m.com/us/office/postit/products/prod_ft_ss.html

    I don't use them for this purpose, but I have used them, or similar sicky, re-usable tabs, when I wanted to mark a page in a notebook temporarily.

  • Try those smaller plastic kind of post it flags (the "sign here" kind). They have a lot more staying power than regular post-it notes and come in ~ 1/4" - 1/2".

  • I use these durable ones -- the one-inch kind:

    http://www.3m.com/us/office/postit/products/prod_ft_dur.html

    I like them better than paper stickies or the cheaper plastic stickies, because they can take a lot more unsticking and resticking before losing their stickiness, and are just generally more durable.

    With the new AF, I have three different tabs for my last active page. They are all the same color, but I wrote on them "Forward", "Reverse", and "Review". I put the tab on my last page that corresponds to the mode I am in. I keep the other two tabs stuck inside the back cover of my notebook.

    I use a different colored tab to mark the page that I'm currently acting on.

    Works great!
