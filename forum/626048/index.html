I'm more productive, but needing help...

  • After my first week of trialing Autofocus, I am happy to find that I am more productive, mainly because I am less affected by distractions. But I can't yet trust the system to process items in a timely fashion.

    When I read through the discussion forum, I see that my experience of Autofocus is massively different to a lot of people. Other people have talked about going through their list multiple times per day, but I have yet to complete a pass through the whole list. I currently have three pages of tasks, with 30 tasks per page. I have completed nearly all of the tasks on the first page. I am working through items on the second page, and recording new tasks on the third page. I find that I'm not moving past the second page because every uncompleted item on the page seems to 'stand out' to me.

    I'm happy to spend the necessary time to do the tasks (more so than before I started using Autofocus), but I don't yet fully trust the system because I know there are more urgent items sitting out of sight on the last page and there is no trigger in the system to do anything about them in the near future.

    I need help with:
    a) How do I know to move on to the next page when uncompleted items on my current page still 'stand out' to me?
    b) When do I review the uncompleted (but important) tasks on my fist page, considering that I have not yet worked my way through to the end of the list?

    I would appreciate any comments that could help me.

  • Are the items standing out because you consider them urgent and/or important or because they make you feel you want to do them? If you are doing items that stand out from an urgent/important perspective you may be missing the intuitive side of AF and trying to work it from a rational perspective.

    Just do the item/s that you "feel" like doing and move on. You also do not need to complete the item you are working on - just work on it for as long as you feel is right at the time. If the item is not completed cross it off and readd it to the bottom of the list.
