Lots of interruptions, little free time

  • I get the feeling Seraphim's situation is characterized by my title here, and not entirely uncommon. In principle there's no reason life or a job can't succeed in the above context, but SF would get unbalanced without a great deal of care.

    One way to rebalance the system is to save SF usage for the free times, so you get smooth operation then until the next interruption. The interruptions will slow progress in SF, but the system itself should remain in balance.

    The other plan is to push hard to get interruption-free time. I have a book or two on that theme. If something does intrude your "focus time", note the issue and push it to the end of the list, or a separate list. Maybe add one urgent item "look at the other list".

    What do you think?

    Don, I agree with you. I tend to think of SF by these abbreviated rules:
    1. write new stuff last page, column 1.
    2. anything you work on, if not done:
    a) continue soon. reenter next page column 2
    b), do more later. last page column 1.
    3. each page, work at least 1 column 1, and all of column 2.
    4. if something comes up
    a) do it now
    b) or enter this page (alternative: next page) column 2.

    So it's all fairly mechanical with a simple choice at each point. Do or do not; now or later.

    The full rules for SF say when and how to make these choices. I take them to be suggestions rather than dictums. I take charge myself.
