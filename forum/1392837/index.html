Ideas for Mark to Stress Test SFv3

  • We all want to make sure that the new SFv3 lives up to its promise, so let's all give Mark some additional ideas for stress-testing the system before its release.

    For example, some of us (i.e., myself) have an issue with being attracted to every new idea that comes along (cleverly disguised as an overwhelming number of tasks). Therefore, I would suggest that Mark use a Random Task Generator (such as a spouse, child, in-law, former colleague, local non-profit organization, etc.) to add 10 to 50 additional semi-relevant tasks to his list every day. Will the Focus still be Super with the additional task load? Will the system continue to produce the Results That Matter?

    Another idea: Work ahead on your Tickler file. Mark uses Outlook reminders for this -- so just look ahead in your Task list for reminders scheduled to appear in the next week or two, and enter them a bit early into your List. See how it handles the temporal dissonance.

    Another idea: Periodically "lose" your list by running it through the laundry; locking it up in a safe-deposit box for a day or two; pulling out a few pages at random to start a campfire; giving it to small child for half an hour, together with some markers or crayons; etc.

    Another idea: Go on an unexpected trip for 2-3 days and leave your List at home. Or simulate a knock-down illness by entering items in your list with your off-hand (e.g., left hand for right-handers) using a dull pencil.


    Any other ideas?
