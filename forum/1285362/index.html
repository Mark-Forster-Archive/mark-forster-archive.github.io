    Yes, that is one reason recurring tasks should not be written back as "re-entered tasks" but rather as "new tasks."

    <<Daily habits however should be reentered at the week mark.>>

    Now here is a funny thing about this new way of doing DWM that I just noticed now. In the original version of DWM, re-entered items are written several pages away from where the new items are written; there are two entry points.

    In this new way of DWM, however, there is only one entry point, but with special symbol/s distinguishing the "re-entered tasks" from the "new tasks" to facilitate the dismissing of unactioned "re-entered tasks" 7 days into the future. Now because of this, "re-entered tasks" due to be deleted 7 days after they were written intermingle with "new tasks" that are due to be deleted 30 days after! This creates task-choosing dynamics that are totally different from those in the original DWM. And Alan, the dynamics that are relevant to what you said comes from the fact that daily recurrent tasks are right beside the "re-entered tasks" even if these recurrent tasks are entered as "new tasks".

    What's the significance? The thing is, when I am processing my DWM, the daily recurrent tasks "stand out" naturally, even though they are not especially marked as "re-entered tasks," because I KNOW they should be done sometime today the moment I read them. There is no need to mark them as "re-entered" except when I have to leave a recurrent task hanging unfinished. If this was in an original DWM scheme and those daily recurrent tasks were written in the 30th day page I most probably would have missed them. But in the new way of DWM, there is no danger.

  • nuntym:

    << However, I want to try the paradigm that it is not /doing/ that's important but /finishing/ >>

    Getting off the subject of DWM, here's a simple engine for getting tasks finished:

    1. Write down three tasks.

    2. Work on the first task for as long as you feel like.

    3. If you finish the task, cross it out and move on to the next task.

    4. If you don't finish the task, cross it out and re-enter it at the end of the list.

    5. When you have only one task remaining on your list, add another two and continue as before.

    Although it sounds simple, this has got some powerful time management principles behind it.

    It's best to use quite broad-brush tasks, e.g. "Project X" rather than "Find Project X File".

    I had been thinking of Cricket's "complicated" question,

    http://www.markforster.net/blog/2010/10/26/my-favourite-time-management-system.html#comment10323303

    and Alan's note that daily recurrent tasks should be rewritten as "re-entered" tasks upon completion. The only thing that I could think of to answer both would be to distinguish two types of "re-entered tasks": Re-Entered Tasks that should be done ASAP, and Re-Entered Tasks that should (probably could only) be done tomorrow. This could be done again using marks on the beginning of each item, and trying to make them as intuitive as possible I thought of this:

    http://i994.photobucket.com/albums/af63/nuntym/DWM/marks.jpg

    So the following would be the procedure for this:

    1) Choose a task and work on it.
    2) If you have to leave it unfinished for now but intend to finish it today, cross-out the item and re-write on the end of the list as a "Re-Entered ASAP Task."
    3) If either you have to leave it unfinished but determined you will resume it tomorrow or in a few days, or you just finished a daily recurrent task, cross it out and re-write on the end of the list as a "Re-Entered For Tomorrow Task." This should signal you to skip the item over when you process your DWM again today.
    4) On the morning of the next day (or on the evening of the current day), after drawing the line denoting the start of a new day for DWM, look through your list for "Re-Entered For Tomorrow Task" items, decide which items you will work on today (or tomorrow), then shade them in to mark them as "Re-Entered ASAP Task" items.
    5) Both "Re-Entered ASAP Tasks" and "Re-Entered For Tomorrow Tasks" that are still not acted upon by the 7th day are to be dismissed.
