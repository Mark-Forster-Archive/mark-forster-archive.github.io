  • Derek,

    I tried the two continuous tasks, but I found that since in our ticket system had all the details It was too easy to cherry picked the easy stuff and avoid other things.

    I'm currently using SFv3,

    My list will have each ticket in it now. I tend to write "Username @ TICKETNUMBER, keyword1 keyword 2" enough info so that I know who it's for, and likely what it's about; and the ticket number helps me use the ticket system search feature to go straight to it when I decide to work on it.

    I have found that this forces me to work on the tickets in a much more timely manner. My usual backlog was around 80-100 before AF1, 60-80 with AF1, and about 30-40 with SFv3.

    This also allows me balance the support tickets equally amongst all the routine sysadmin work and the project and development stuff we do.

    With AF1 and two recurring tasks as proposed in this thread; I'd have about 200 tasks on ten pages and another 80-100 in tickets. With SFv3 and everything in the system as discrete entries I'm well under 60 items and holding. Warning though it was really slow going when I first loaded the backlog it took about 2 weeks for the momentum to get rolling
