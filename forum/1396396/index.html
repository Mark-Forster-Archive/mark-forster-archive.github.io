Of Tasks and Project Management

  • With all the talk of time management versus project management and the new thread on AF advanced, it seems to me if we are running a task management system there should not be any re-writing of tasks. If I am writing tasks they are short actions I should be able to do and not need to re-write. For example, call architect A about sending qualifications. I make the call it is done. If he is not there, I may note I am waiting for a call back, or if I don't like him, I may write a new task like call architect B about his qualifications.

    If I am putting projects on my list, then of course work on project X or build dream house would need to be re-written many times until complete. But, what is the need, most people would know they are building a house, they would not loose track of the project, it would be the tasks they would need to plan and act on. So, then we are back to project management and they should not even be in the TM system. They may need a master list of their projects if they have many but this is also not a TM system.

    I started my TM journey looking for a system to bridge this need and quite frankly and not sure I am all that much closer as I keep experimenting and trying to find the blend of TM and PM resources to make this go smoother. Thanks all for the interesting discussions of late and looking forward to the new release by Mark.

    Gerry

    2 - Taking the above in consideration, I agree that projects should not go in Task Management systems but they DEFINITELY should go in Time Management Systems. Maybe, just maybe (and this is the way I approach it personally) There should be no "project tasks" in a Time Management System...

  • Gerry: I believe Mark would propose all simple tasks go in the task list, and those would include:
    put garbAge out, plan project x, walk 3 miles, read some war and peace, do next thing in project x, learn Stairway on guitar.

    Some of the above are repeated tasks. You do some now, and some later. You can make a system that skips rewriting but Mark and many don't see that as a hassle. You can in fact have a process that does both concurrently, which may or may not be better.
