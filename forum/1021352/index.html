Keeping up with friends' blogs

  • I have quite a number of friends and family who blog and several who blog daily and/or have multiple blogs. I also have a number of these who seem upset with me when I am not up-to-date with them and commenting on them. "Didn't you read my blog?" That kind of thing. I do have my blogs in a reader. But between it and Facebook, I think I am dealing with some major obligation-based procrastination.

    Anyone else deal with this problem and find a solution that worked for you?

  • Here is what Alan Weiss says about blogs and social sites in chapter 5 of his new book "Thrive!".

    Here is how you Thrive despite the chaos around you.
    Alan’s Tips for Making Sense of the Riot Around Us

    "Limit your time on social sites. Treat them as a hobby and, as any hobby, you get to them in your spare time. If you’re posting on Facebook even once an hour, or trying to gain contacts on Linkedin every day, or following two dozen people on Twitter, it’s the equivalent of spending six hours a day collecting stamps, or spending all day tuning the car’s engine, or not coming out of the garden for the entire weekend.
    You know who engages in that kind of behavior? Retired people, people with WAY too much time on their hands, that’s who."

  • My $.02 (and I have a friend that does that to me too Mel) is that it can be a compliment to a friend or family member if you take an afternoon every once in awhile (like once a month or something) to go through their blogs with focused attention and read and comment on what sticks out. If they want ME to know something personally - pick up the phone or shoot me an email, don't expect me to read your blog to find out about your life. I go on googlereader about once a month or so. It just doesn't occur to me I guess.

    I suppose different people have different purposes for their blogs. I write mine for myself, to think things through, have a record of some things, not for other people at all. Some people - like your friends - use it as a means of keeping others posted on what's going on in their lives. But just like you might not talk to those people on the phone every day or email them every day, you can't be expected to read their blogs every day either.

    I go on facebook maybe once every couple of months and my friends have learned (I wish!) that I ignore all the little games and stuff that they send me. I use FB pretty much only for a receptacle for mobile photo uploads. Hmm. Perhaps this is why I only have a few close friends and not 500 acquaintances. Acquaintances are overrated, friends are not.
