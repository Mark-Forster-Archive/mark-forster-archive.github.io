Phrasing items as questions

  • There was a post that suggested phrasing items as a question - "make call re item xyz?"

    As I understood it (and I can't find the post again to make sure) -
    If the answer's yes cross the item off, it's done, and add any necesary follow-up tasks.
    If the answer is no, cross the item off because you answered the question (even though it doesn't get you any closer to a finished phone call) and re-add it to the end of the list.
    I have several items that I'm dreading (not surprisingly they're all the same type of to-do) but they need to be done, so dismissing them is not the 'right' option. But if I rephrase the item as a question, I'm thinking there'll become permanent fixtures on the list as they get crossed off and re-added since the answer is 'no'

    I'm assuming that sooner or later (probably later) these items-as-question items eventually do get done, dismissed, or delegated. Don't they?

  • I don't phrase tasks as a question unless they are waiting for tasks like "Has X called back with Y information?"

    As to dreaded tasks, I am easy on myself with respect to complete before ticking the task off. One of the best ways I have found to get something moving is to focus on the smallest next action possible.

    For example:
    Think about XYZ

    Might become:
    Find XYZ project information folder and put it on desk

    OR

    List two people who might have ideas about XYZ


    For very difficult tasks, even thinking about it enough to figure out that amount of granular meaning moves it forward. So rephrasing it into very small action is enough to let me rewrite it. When I get to that rewritten task, I will usually have less resistence, as it is more bite-sized.
