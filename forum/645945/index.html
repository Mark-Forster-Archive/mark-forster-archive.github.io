  • I'm just getting near to the end of re-reading the Richard Hannay stories by John Buchan. Most people have heard of "The Thirty-Nine Steps" but there are four further novels, each of which is better than the one before (in my opinion). They were written and are set in the period before, during and after the First World War.

    When I first read the novels fifty years ago, there were still plenty of Richard Hannay hero-types around. I knew quite a few of them, though I often only found out how heroic they had been when I read their obituaries.

    Nowadays everything he did, thought and said would be totally out of fashion, and quite a bit of it (like smoking a pipe in a pub, or hunting with dogs) illegal.

    But I do wonder what he would have made of Autofocus!
