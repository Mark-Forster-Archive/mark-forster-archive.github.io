  • Laurence,

    Some people use the "Circa" / "Rolabind" books. Levenger (search the web) offers a starter kit for $40 with a $40 gift certificate so it is essentially free (except for shipping). They make a 3x5 format with "rings" at the top and a pen loop. They also make some pages (lines, squares, TO DO list, Project List) on card stock. However, in order to use your own printed pages and forms, you need to buy their rather expensive punch. (They do not use a ring binder arrangement (with closed holes) but keep the holes open a bit so you can "tear" the pages out and "press" them back in elsewhere. It comes in several other form factors from letter size (8x11) down to an impossibly small 1x2 inch (or something like that) size.

    The upside is that you can manage pages as you like ... keeping your book lean and mean. The downside is that it is more fussing and expense.

    One thing you might consider is simply bagging all the overhead of marking lines and such. Just bite the bullet and realize you are, in essence, starting from scratch and just copy over only KEY items and let the list evolve fresh. It is not as awful as it sounds and it is a way of purging a lot of things quickly and getting you moving quickly again. Just a thought. (Basically, it is what you'd have to do if you lost your book!)

  • BTW, I found it helps to get the measurements of the various sizes and compare them to what you have used in the past. Letter and 3x5 are kind of obvious, but the "Compact" and "Junior" are close to the same size and of slightly different form factors. 3x5 is my style as I work out of my pocket, but the "Compact" is not a bad size either. "Junior" is more for those who like to write on a surface, I guess.

    The thing that Rolabind (the manufacturer) got right is having the rings spaced the same way for all sized. So it is possible to pull out a 3x5 sheet and pop it into your Compact binder. Lots of nice things you can do if you're of a mind to experiment.
