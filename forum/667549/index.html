Happiness is - just one active page

  • Today, I reached a point with AF that I expected never to see. I have just one active page with 19 live items on it.

    Gradually over the weeks since AF began, I have been tending more and more to work closer to the last few pages of my book and last night I fully expected to end the day with only two active pages. You can imagine my satisfaction with getting 20 tasks done today and having just the one page remaining active. Most of the remaining items are recurring, so I do not expect to lower the number any further.

    I cannot imagine how others can work AF effectively with 20 or more active pages. I can only percieve that they must be dealing with heafty backlogs and something will have to give before too long.

    I am starting to believe that Af is gently training me to take on no more than what can be done with just 7 normal days each week. It is a very long time since I have had the feeling I have today that I am "up with things" and that there are few if any important things that have fallen through the cracks.

  • Hi Pascal

    I think you raise a very interesting point when you talk about "one's mind and life circumstances". I think we have a concept of how we would like our lists to be and it is to some extent that concept that causes us some degree of frustration. If we, thinking rationally about our lists, feel we should spend longer, or that we should be moving more quickly, then either "belief" will give us a feeling of unease if our experience varies from that. I believe that our AF will actually settle down according to our preferred way of thinking and doing, and you are right, in either case (such as large project items worked little & often, or small individual tasks) - this is AF.

    What I have found is that AF has enabled me to go through the process of redefining my tasks and as a result expressing them in the way that works best for me. My tasks on early pages are written differently to later pages.

    What I find as a general rule is that, on the first two or three passes of a 38 task page, I will do about half of the items. The remaining items will clear at the rate of 3-7 on each consecutive pass. Why that number? - no idea, but a definite pattern is starting to emerge.

    I "want" to have less pages but am not finding the number of pages to be a real problem for me. As I say definite patterns are starting to emerge and I find I can normally identify reasons for resistance fairly promptly - doesn't mean I necessarily want to deal with those reasons but that's another subject:-)
