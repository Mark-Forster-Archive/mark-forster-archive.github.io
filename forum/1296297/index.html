They're Making Good Paper Products Again. Yay!

  • As a teenager, I used Daytimer's wirebound series. They came with blank pages (and various forms) with cut-outs down the side so you could fit them into the wire binding. It was the best of both worlds. Smaller spine and you could fold them over, and you could also move pages around. (It's much like Levenger's Circa series, but not as bulky.)

    This feature stopped several years ago. Apparently the former DayTimer clients (lawyers and executives according to the advertising images) all moved to PDAs.

    Blueline's new Miraclebind has that feature. So far the sizes are only 8x5in and up. Calendars are daily, weekly and monthly. Yes, the cover can be bent back. No, they're not compatible with their other wirebound books.

    Prices for their book and calendar pages look good. Lined refills are pricey, but a book with lined pages is reasonable -- so buy the book for the pages. Crossing my fingers that they don't plan to raise the kit prices once we're hooked.

    http://bluelineinc.com/vw/fs/p002.htm

    Is it sad that for me, returning to my teen years means having a great organizer in my purse?
