Tagging notes to a task on paper system?

  • I want to start tagging notes to a task in AF - basically have a brief description of the item that will fit on my one line, and then if I need some supplementary description of what to do to include it somewhere - on the left hand side of the page maybe? (I only use the right hand side for writing my tasks).

    This is kind of similar to the old Franklin Planner method I used years ago, but you would put the notes in a separate area of your notebook, if I recall.

    I'm trying to think of the best way to tag these notes. I've thought of putting a (1), (2) etc. beside the item on the left hand side preceding the task - I use the right margin for deadline dates if applicable and write the day I completed the item on the left margin so don't want to use those spaces. Then use the next lines down on my left hand side of the paper to write the notes, preceded by the (1), (2)...

    Any other ideas? Please let me know if I've missed advice on this that's already out there somewhere in the discussion.

  • You could draw lines between notes on the left page and the corresponding items on the right page... arrows... dotted lines... get visual! I think it would be easier for my eye to quickly follow a line, than to have to match up (1) on the left with (1) on the right, (2) with (2), etc. But your eye may be different :-)

    Going a bit further with the concept, it could be a nice split between left-brain/right-brain "creative vs. logical" thinking. The left page is for unstructured notes and doodles and mindmaps, while the right page is an ordered logical list of items. Then connect the two with whatever lines or pointers you want to draw.

  • Hi Jacqueline

    One of the things I have found is that any form of tags in the paper system by means of a margin notation or highlighting, inhibits the flow of the item scanning. What I have found works best is to add a reference at the end of a task after a dash. For example if an email generates a task I put it in my AutoFocus folder in Outlook and the task will have the suffix - AFe. I can easily scan my list for all such tasks but they do not inhibit my scanning in the same way. Additional highlighting was the worst distraction - my highlighting is reserved solely for dismissed items - and only when the entire page is dismissed.

  • I handle this pretty much the way you described in the original post: AF list on the right-hand pages, notes & stuff on the left-hand pages.

    E.g.: Task is "Drop off prescription at pharmacy (see left)". Then I taped the actual prescription to the left-hand page. Drawing an arrow works OK too.

    I've also used notes like this: "Drop off prescription at pharmacy (*1)". Then on the left-hand page, I write (*1) and add the extra info.

    Very simple, doesn't distract at all from scanning the AF list, and keeps everything handy.

  • In the moment, have one somewhat large spiral-bound notebook and I freely mix AF pages with others. This looks like:
    ...
    - active AF list
    - some scribblings, concerning a letter I intend to write
    - a lengthy memo from a phone call
    - some thoughts about a project, plus some phone numbers I have to call
    - the next AF list
    - notes for a travel I have to organize (hotels, train times etc.)
    - conceptual notes for a new novel
    - an idea for a novel or story (probably a bad one, but I write them all down...)
    - another AF list
    ...

    I am accustomed since years to start every subject on a new page, so I can tear them out and file them together (for example, all novel ideas go in one place, all scribblings for one novel in another, etc.). Every page gets the date in the upper left corner and the subject in the upper right, so I can always file them properly, and over twenty years' time, an impressive system of notes has grown this way which is invaluable for me.

    Yes, one has to turn more pages this way. But so what? On the other hand, one is less tempted to jump around in the different AF pages; the basic rule that every page has to be considered as a unity is supported: the others are simply out of sight!

    So, maybe I'll stick with this.

    Do I need tagging? No. Actually, I know whether I have made notes for a project or not. I do, however, add a tag to indicate where I store a document that is concerned (a letter to answer, for example).
