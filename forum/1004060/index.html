My AF4 thoughts so far

  • I've been using AF4 on FocusPad for a few weeks now, and here are my thoughts so far.

    It's a vast improvement and I feel there's a long way to go. I generally find that when I get into the list for the day, after I review the list for a couple of rounds, I generally feel that sense of confidence that it is tracking everything in my life that I need to be tracking. But, when I'm looking at 25 pages of stuff, getting to that peace-of-mind stage takes work - it's an investment.

    Most days I can muster up the will to do so, but there are other days, days with a lot of activity, where I don't, and I end up feeling unsettled, like I am forgetting something.

    I think some of it is the size of the list. One thing that helped me is that I noticed several of my todo items fit a theme. For instance, I have paid client work, but I also have several of my own side ventures, none of which pay me anything but might give me income in the future if I work on them. I almost never work on these items in a day; I keep delaying them but never dismissing them. And they were making my list feel very large. I recognized this, and decided I would offload all of them - I deleted them all from my AF4 list, put them in an OmniFocus bucket (a separate list), and then in AF4 I put "schedule Ventures day". I scheduled a Ventures day, and then completed several of the items off that separate list. The other benefit is that the AF4 list then felt more manageable; small enough that I could grasp the shape/smell/sound/whatever of it when I wasn't staring at it, and didn't feel like I had to review it every time I sat down to use it, and thus didn't feel reluctant to sit down and grapple with the largeness of it.

    But then the list grew again, and now it just feels unwieldy, and to the point that if I start working on it, I worry that I won't start working on the things I actually need to work on because I won't get to them in time for me to make proper headway on them before I have to stop working.

    So in short it seems there is a scaling consideration with AF4. I can't quite get into the no-mind feeling of just trusting everything is there, because (and this is key) - it regulates the speed with which I go through the list. If I'm aware there's an urgent item twelve pages from now, I'll work faster through those twelve pages than I will if I'm not aware of something being urgent.

    As a result, I find that an uncomfortably high level of my mental energy is spent on the meta layer - understanding the makeup of my list - than on the do-ing layer.
