AF4 in Brief

  • For those who didn't get to Page 4 of the Comments from Mark's Fri 04 Sep live-blogging, I'm repeating my summary post here.

    I'd like to offer a simple description of the process. I'll use C ("Current") for open list #2, and B ("Backlog") for closed list #1.

    Brief Autofocus 4 Instructions (assumed start at Current List)

    1. Unicycle C, then multicycle B. Return to C after unproductive B cycle.

    2. On return from C to B, cross out each highlighted task.

    3. On unproductive initial B cycle, highlight all B tasks then close entire list. Continue to multicycle newly expanded B, skipping highlighted tasks.

    I think that covers the entire strategy, other than the details we already know how to do (e.g. choosing what "stands out"; deciding which highlighted items to re-enter and re-word, etc.).

  • Ubi,

    That is indeed a condensed summary. On the off chance that it is TOO condensed for those who were not following the demonstration Mark gave today, let me post a more expanded description.


    =====

    Summary of AF4

    The AF4 list structure -- AF4 consists of two lists (or list parts)

    - The first is a closed list called "Backlog" (for the moment ... it might later be called the "Closed List")
    - Second is an open list
    - The two lists are treated as one with regard to where one is in the overall processing cycle. IOW, start tomorrow where you left off today.


    [NOTE: There is possibly a third list, of a transient nature, which results from marking all tasks on the first list as "on notice for dismissal", moving the "line" to the end of the second list, and then adding new tasks after that line ... thus resulting in the temporary creation of the third list. This third list is quickly deleted when, on the next pass, all "on notice items are deleted. See the processing details for more information.]


    The rules for processing AF4 are as follows:

    1. The first ("Closed") list is cycled through repeatedly and items are actioned (if they stand out), or passed over
    1.1. Actioned items are crossed out
    1.1.1. If there is more work to be done (or the task is repetitive), it is added to the end of the second list.

    1.2. If something was actioned in this pass and nothing further stands out
    1.2.1. Move to the beginning of the second list and begin scanning/processing that list

    1.3. If nothing was actioned in this pass of the first list
    1.3.1. All items are put "on review"
    1.3.2. The "backlog line" is moved to the end of the second list making all of that the new "backlog"
    1.3.3. All new items are added after the new "backlog line"

    1.4. On the next pass of the first list, after returning from working on the second "Open" list
    1.4.1. Dismiss all "on notice" items
    1.4.2. Add back any that seem to want to be done to the end of the second list (possibly rewording them)

    2. The second ("Open") list is processed in only one pass each time it is entered
    2.1. It is processed only when nothing stands out from the first list
    2.2. As with the first list, if an item is actioned it is crossed off and re-added to the end (of the second, open, list) if there is more work to do or it is a repetitive item

    =====

    That is my best understanding so far. I'm sure there are more errors, so ... ;-)

  • Here's my interpretation of the rules, for what it's worth:

    1. Start at the beginning of the Closed List, and look at each item.

    2. If an item is highlighted, you must either take action, or delete the item. ("Take action" can also mean "rewrite the task at the end of the Open List")

    3. If an item is not highlighted, you may take action if you like, or you may skip. After you take action, cross off the item from the Closed List. If work still remains for that item, add it to the end of the Open List.

    4. When you come to the end of the Closed List: if you have taken action on any of the items on the Closed List, then start again at the beginning of the Closed List and go through it again.

    5. But when you get to the end of the Closed List without having taken action on anything:
    (A) If this was your first pass through the Closed List:
    1. Highlight all remaining items on the Closed List.
    2. Move the Line to the end of the Open List (so now, all items are on the new Closed List).
    3. Keep working through the Closed List (which now includes the tasks that used to be on your Open List)

    (B) Otherwise:
    1. Start at the beginning of the Open List, and look at each item.
    2. For each item, you may take action, or you may skip. After you take action, cross off the item from the Open List. If work still remains for that item, add it to the end of the Open List.
    3. When you get to the end of the Open List, go back to Step 1.

    You will have noticed that I moved through the list very fast, especially when the backlog was large. I find this happens naturally. Paradoxically the larger the backlog, the quicker one moves through it.

    In the case of an urgent items, imagine that you are buried in your backlog somewhere. You write the urgent item at the end of the active list. Your subconscious/intuition tells you to get a move on, so you finish working on the backlog item you are currently on, circle round the backlog - nothing. Move through the active list - nothing. Action the urgent task (which is still the last item in the list).

    Where do you go from there? As you've just done the last task on the list, you go to the beginning of the list, i.e. back into the backlog.

  • Mark,

    O.K. So I have already made a tweak! LOL ;-)

    No, this is not a tweak to make THE SYSTEM work better. I made it purely to accommodate my physical setup. I'm posting it here so that others who have similar physical limitations might think about how to get the benefits of AF4 if they are working with a page oriented physical setup.

    My setup:

    - As I have said before, I don't work at a desk so I can't use a computerized implementation ... paper works best for me. But this also means I need a small format book to carry in my pocket.

    - The smaller format (3x5 inches) also means that there are fewer items on a page (20) so I found myself flipping around the book too much. I tried paperclips, rubber bands, dog earing pages, moving Post-its around ... all added a level of fussing I was not comfortable with.

    - I also have several lists I keep with me: shopping, discussions with my wife, etc. I don't want those items distributed through a long list so I keep separate lists.

    - Because of those considerations I switched from my beloved cheap ass spiral books to a Circa/Rollabind book. (Basically movable pages.)

    My modified AF system works well with this setup, but with AF4 I was puzzling about how to manage drawing the line and moving the closed backlog out of my way and making the active section a backlog. Basically, with movable pages I got everything flowing smoothly by removing pages when they were closed. But now that AF4 no longer uses "pages" but is, physically, a single long list ... I was facing having to start flipping pages again. No way, Jose ;-)

    So here is my minor alteration. When it comes time to draw the line at the end of the active list and delete the closed list, I don't draw the line at the end of the last item, but rather draw it at the end of the last full page. IOW, some of the items at the end of my open list remain on the open list. What does this mean for me?

    - I can remove the old closed list pages (adding the kept items to the end of the remaining open list page)
    - I then move all of the FULL pages of the open list to the front of the book (I have a divider tab separating the closed and open lists for ease of access)
    - I then have the last partial page starting the new open list and I continue adding to that.

    The only effect on the system is that I have as many as 19 maximum (10 average, 1 minimum) open list tasks that remain on the open list when I "draw the line". I don't see that as a problem for me. At my current level of task chunking and activity I fill between one and three pages (add 20-60 items) per day, so an average of 10 items that don't become backloged when they "should" is a nit, as I see it.

    Also, since I have the ability to easily remove pages, I remove pages (from both lists) with all items completed as I go. IOW, if I have only two items open in 4 pages on the closed list, I will have pulled the pages with no open items on them as soon as they were completed. This is to keep the book light and thin. (The downside to Circa/Rollabind is that when the book starts to bulge, the pages can pop out.) It also keeps page flipping to a minimum ;-)

    So, no messing with the way the system works, just a nod to notebook limitations. Now to run the system and get a feel for it in my life.
