Question about going to the next page

  • I am confused about when to stop reviewing a page and moving to the next one. Here's my actual situation:
    Assume that I have one and a half pages of tasks. On day one, say, I go through the first page and I follow the procedure of going round the first page several times doing tasks that stand out and repeating this cycle until the end of the day. I then stop on day one because of lack of time. On which page should I start on day two? Would it be from the top of page one or page two?

    An alternative situation for which I would appreciate your comments: Say you go through a particular page, doing tasks that stand out, but you have to stop (end of day) before you've had a chance to reach the bottom of the page. How do you mark the spot you reached on that page, that is the last task that you did? I ask this, because presumably one would start the following day's review from the spot that one finished on the previous day. Therefore, some means of marking the "reached here" point is needed.

  • @Otto: Your description of the dismissal process does not seem correct to me.

    Dismissing happens like this. You just finished working a page, so you go to the next active page. You do Quickstart Step 1 -- quickly read through all the items on the page. You then do Quickstart Step 2 -- Go through each item looking for one to stand out. If NOTHING stands out, and you go through the whole page top to bottom, and take no action on any of them, then you dismiss all the remaining items on the page -- Quickstart Step 7.

    However, if during Quickstart Step 2, you actually do take action on one or more items, then that page is no longer subject to dismissal at this time.

    The last active page is not subject to dismissal.

    Does that clarify it?
    12 March 2009 at 21:43 | Seraphim
  • My understanding is that the dismissal step applies _whenever_ no item stands out during step 2 -- whether it occurs on the very next page or on a return through the entire list. So both Otto (reply 4) and Seraphim (reply 7) are correct. Dismissal is a general consideration which applies to any page, whenever you pass through it -- not strictly between steps 6 and 8.

    If you are new to AF, then every next page is fresh, and "dismissable" items very likely will not be encountered until you recycle through the entire list, as Otto describes. At that point Seraphim's description takes effect.

    I suspect this debate is caused by the intrusive location of step 7 in the Quick Steps. A possible solution is to reverse steps 7 and 8, as proposed in http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/693330.

  • You lot have even got me confused!

    The idea is that any time you visit a page and don't do any items on it, then all the items on it are dismissed. In other words you have to do at least one item every time you visit a page. This applies at any stage - the only exception being the last page while it still has emply lines on it.

    As for coming back to the list after breaking for the night, I strongly recommend starting from where you left off, i.e. not just the page on which you stopped but also the item on which you stopped - just as if the break had never happened.
