  • Here in Ireland they are known as 'Nature Study Notebooks' and are used in schools and colleges where a combination of writing and diagrams are needed.

    They can be hard to find here, but the larger stationary stores stock them from time to time - so I buy a few of them at a time. I have found only one supplier online (see URL below).

    <http://www.schoolbooks.ie/school-books/2456-0-Notebooks---Hardback-School-Books.php>

    While the notebooks have the following brand names and URL printed on them, the websites don't actually exist (weird).

    Supreme Stationary 200 Page Nature Notebook www.supreme-stationery.com (does not exist!)
    Lismore 120 Page Nature Notebook www.kempis.com (empty website!)

    The notebooks have a sewn-in ribbon book-mark which I use to mark my place among the active pages. I staple two wide elasticised markers on the front and back covers. The front one marks the oldest active page and the back one marks the newest page. That way I can flip open the book exactly at the page I need.

    By using un-ruled pages for note-taking I am encouraged to be artistic and graphic and creative. I find this stimulates the right hemisphere and promotes better integration between left- and right- brain thinking.

    Oh, by the way, I have settled on using a propelling pencil for these books. Fountain pens are great - but often dry-up or become patchy at unpredictable moments. Ball-point pens get blotchy when you use them to draw lines (crossing-out entries and drawing mind maps) - I got blobs of ink on the pages, my fingers and clothes. The pencil (I use B or 2B graphite) slows my writing down enough to keep it legible - and has a lovely tactile feel. It also encourages me to shade things and doodle in 3D on the blank pages.

    I have, in the past, used a hardback A4 Sketchbook - with blank pages only. This allowed my right-brain stuff to flourish, but its difficult to keep the lists neat and tidy without the ruled lines.

    I hope this helps.
    Respect
