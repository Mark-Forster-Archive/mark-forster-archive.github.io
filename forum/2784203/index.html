How do you organize yourself during your free time?

  • During regular working hours I've been able to be more or less effective using scheduled work sessions and breaks and a long list, but I haven't been able to figure out how to be consistently "productive" (feel like I'm using my time well) during my free time (evenings and weekends).

    I've tried scheduling "work"/focus sessions (when I'm on my personal list), but it feels too constrained for down time, and I end up with breaks where I don't know what to do. I've tried no schedule but it seems without promised breaks I kinda of avoid lists.

    I know some people do the same processes in their time off as during work, but it seems some also do different processes.

    So I'm curious, how do each of you organize yourselves during your free time? Is it different/the same as during work?

  • I note lots of tasks in my AF notebook, but when I sit down in the evening after my dayjob, or on the weekend when other larger tasks/errands have been looming and need dispatching, I find that Serial No-List is my preferred go-to. Whatever's on the top of my mind is usually worth following; I may also ask the question "what am I putting off NOT doing?" just to refresh the Google search of my mind and see what else emerges. And of course, include distractions and fun stuff/downtime activities on that list also.

    It can be a long list! After the list is made, standing-out makes the choices.
