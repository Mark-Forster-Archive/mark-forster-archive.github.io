Re-adding tasks vs. skipping pages to find something you CAN do

  • As I get down to the last couple of tasks on quite a few pages at the front of my personal/home book, I find quite a few tasks that I can't do because of basically two circumstances:

    1) Very bad weather (we're talking -28C with windchill here), so items like vacuum the car, wash the car, really can't get done and I haven't been able to in the last 3 weeks
    2) They require a longer block of time than I have during the week for a project that can't be chunked down - may involve taking my son somewhere fun that's in the city, so it's a 4-6 hour time commitment, or doing a renovation job that requires rented tools

    I really want to leave them on the list where they are because I don't want to get them buried in the new pages and want them to stand out for me next weekend (+10 temperatures at last) - which they will because they're the last things on the list. I'm afraid that if they're buried in new stuff next weekend when I come to them I won't feel drawn to do them. Note that I have no aversion to doing them at all.

    I was thinking of just having a scheduled weekend for these bigger items so I can knock them off the list at last.
