Why I trust Autofocus to work for me

  • I have been using Autofocus for a week now, completely mesmerized and a little baffled by the way it melts away my resistance to doing work. How is it that this simple system has instantly made me more productive when everything else I have tried (for the last ten years, mind!) has failed?

    I finally figured it out today, and I am kicking myself for not realizing this earlier.

    I have tried so many productivity "systems", over and over again. A to-do list for the day, inevitably too long and never completed. Time blocking/scheduling, which I never could discipline myself to follow. Listing my three "big rocks" for the day, also never completed. Planning out each day in advance, only to completely ignore said plan within 5 minutes of waking up. Each of these quickly degraded into simply doing what was most urgent. What do these systems have in common? They all tell me WHEN to do my tasks: on a particular day, at a particular time, or in a particular order.


    I HATE people telling me when to do things. Always have. (It was a major sticking point with my parents when I was growing up - them saying "Do this right NOW" pretty much guaranteed I'd walk off in a huff :-) ) So why on Earth have I been imposing a schedule on MYSELF for so long?


    There you have it. All these years, I have been searching for a better plan, when really the problem was the plan itself. With AF, I have no plan; I just have a choice. And I TRUST that when I make that choice, it will be the best choice I can make at the time based on my goals, my energy levels, the urgency of the task, etc. Since all of the tasks are in the system, I don't have to worry about forgetting them. Less worrying means I have more energy to DO the tasks.

    I really think "Trust the system" ought to be bolded and coloured red on the front page! It is probably the most important rule in the instructions, and in some ways the essence of the system.


    This week, for the first time since a major burn-out a few years ago, I was not only productive, but I enjoyed doing my work. How I missed that feeling! I am sure it will be a while before I am fully immersed in the system, but for the first time in a LONG time I am confident that I will be productive again. I can't thank Mark enough for this brilliant idea! And don't let anyone ever tell you this is "just a to-do list'. I am living proof it's not :-)
