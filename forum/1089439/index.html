Just open the darned thang!

  • I am back to using AF1. For me it is the best version because it makes sure that I spend most of my time doing the important stuff rather than the urgent stuff.

    However, and this is a problem I have had with all versions, I quite often find it very difficult to just open the AF list at the start of the working day. Consequently, I can "waste" hours before I finally get round to opening the Autofocus book.

    But I have noticed recently that a tweak has occurred that I didn't consciously introduce... Every morning I must use my asthma inhaler. I often forgot to do this, so I added it to my AF list. Now, when I use my asthma inhaler in the morning (which has become a habit thanks to AF), I have to cross it off my list and reenter it for tomorrow. Somehow the two actions have become inextricably linked -- I can't do the one without doing the other.

    When I cross "Inhaler" off the list in the morning, I then work from that point, using AF1 rules, rather than go back to the point where I left off yesterday (I always forgot where I got to, anyway, and posted about that months ago). This ensures that the urgent stuff gets done early, which overcomes any panic I might feel if I am not cycling round the rest of the list fast enough. Occasionally, I get stuck at the most recent end of the list, and if I notice this and feel it is undesirable, I will force myself back to the oldest page of the list.

    So, if you have trouble starting on your list at the beginning of the day, find something you do every morning, add it to the list, and hopefully it will become linked in the same way as for me.

    HTH,
