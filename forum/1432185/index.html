  • I like to keep my day's appointments in front of me as I'm working my SF list, and also, I often like to have few items on a short list of things I hope to get done today. Although I work the SF list strictly according to the rules, it helps me to make sure I don't forget something that is time-sensitive.

    I described my setup in the thread linked above.I have it in an 81/2 x 5 1/2 size looseleaf binder. When I open the notebook, the left-hand page has my schedule for the day (printed in advance from my computer) with room to write notes, or a "today list" or a shopping list, or whatever. On the right-hand page I have my SF list (current page on top; when I "turn to the next page" I put the top page at the back of the section.) This is working beautifully for me.

    I have been using SF for a few weeks now, and have used other systems of Mark's. I am a GTD seminar alumnus, and have quite a large collection of time management books. All of these systems had something to offer, but I have never been quite so productive (and good about not letting things slip through the cracks) as I have been these past few weeks with SF.

    I posted pictures of my notebook on the Flickr site. I enjoy seeing the pictures of people's various setups!
