Considering returning to PDA for AF

  • Since AF4 was revealed, I have been using pen and paper in a small notebook, and this led to much higher productivity - a release from hi-tech! But while I am convinced of the essential logic behind AF4, as the list has grown, I find I am getting diminishing returns as it takes too long to plow through the list again when I return to the closed list, which consists of a great many almost completed pages. I also have to filter manually for the tasks relevant to the place where I am.

    So I am considering reverting to ListPro. This can easily present those tasks which can be done at any one place, and also those which can only be done at a specific place. I am thinking of using a separate dividing line for each place (this is the system I use for inbox zero email, drawing on AF4 methodology).

    But ... I love entering the items by hand, so I shall probably continue doing so, in a single notebook, and crossing them out as they are transferred to ListPro.

    As Roger has said, ListPro is pretty good for AF. Perhaps some judicious use of AutoHotKey may automatically tick and copy to the bottom.

  • I've adapted my hybrid system and it seems to be working nicely with ListPro AND a notebook (Moleskine medium size, close to A5)

    Bearing in mind I have a laptop (Toshiba Portege M700) and a Smartphone/PDA (HTC TouchPro 2), I can use AF4 thus:
    - ListPro contains my Backlog/Closed and Current/Open Tasks ie tasks by Status
    - the notebook's right-hand pages contain only new tasks that spring to mind (some of which I decide to do immediately or get to later in the day), but which are NOT in ListPro; the left-hand pages record notes from phone calls, meetings.

    This means I can cycle through ListPro as per the AF4 rules, with Current/Open consisting of Current/Open in ListPro PLUS any tasks written down in the notebook during the day.

    For repeating tasks, like emails, clear Inbox etc, I can mark them done. and ListPro does a satisfying strikethrough.

    At the end of the day, I do the following:
    - in ListPro: delete non-recurring completed tasks (I may cut and paste them into a Done/Zapped list if I think I may want to do them again some time in the future).
    - in ListPro: change the status of completed recurring tasks in Backlog/Closed to Current/Open and resort the list by Status.
    - in ListPro: tasks for Review (in Backlog/Closed) are cut and pasted into a Review List, and a Review task added in the main AF4 list in ListPro.
    - any tasks written down in the notebook during the day and NOT addressed are entered in ListPro with Current/Open Status.

    Thus at the beginning of the day, i do not have to look in the notebook for any tasks, just bring up ListPro and write down new ones during the day. If I am away from my laptop, I have the synchronised LIstPro lists in my HTC Smartphone/PDA, AND my notebook. I can also print my AF4 list and fold it inside my notebook

    I may not have explained my hybrid system very well, but would welcome constructive comments or questions. I'd be interested in Andreas' super system if it could by synchronised between laptop and Smartphone - maybe it does already and I've missed something!
