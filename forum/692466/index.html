When can I rephrase items?

  • I have been using AF for a month, and found that this system works better than any other time management systems I have ever used. This forum helps me understand AF as well.

    I have a question regarding "rephasing." I am wondering when I can rephrase tasks. In the original instruction, you see the description "re-phrase" just in the section #7 (dismissing items). In some posts, however, people say that you can rephrase an item and cross it off. In other words, "rephasing" can be considered as a sort of "actions."

    Either way might be OK, but if there are any clear ideas or definitions as to when to rephase, it would be helpful to me.
    12 March 2009 at 3:22 | mit
  • Mit, the beauty of AF means you can do it whenever it works for you! There are just no hard and fast rules unlike other systems. I have been using AF since Day One of it's release, and have found through various discussions of other peoples experiences and my own that many things can either trigger a rephrasing of a task or make that an action in itself.

    For example: if I have a stubborn item or two left on an active page as part of my considering dismissing items I may find that rephrasing the entire task (maybe it was to big an item and so break it down) works. If it is just building up resistance - think about why and then maybe cross off and add to current list "Review actions needed/Consider/Discuss XXXX."

    If a variable relating to that task changes then I will cross off the original task and add back in with that change taken into account.

    I would definitely confirm that I see rephrasing as a legitimate "action" having been taken on a task. They system works brilliantly for me, and whilst I have toyed with tweeks I have basically stuck with the basic set of instructions, use two books @Home and @Work and manage time critical items with an action usually in the task list to ensure small steps progress and an appropriate calendar/schedule entry, it has not failed me yet. They key is continually working the pages and trusting the system.

    Good luck!
