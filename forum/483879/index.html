  • This is a silly use for the Noguchi method, but when I hang my newly laundered clothes in the closet, they go on the left side of the rack. When I wear something, I return it at the end of the day to the left side of the rack. So I can always tell when I look at the rack what I've worn recently and, incidentally, what clothes I'm not wearing at all because they're migrating to the right side of the rack.

    Oh well, it works for me and my nerd ways....

    For file folders, I keep a small bankers box on my desk with folders for active projects, and the most recently used ones are at the front. But when those projects are done, I typically recycle the folder and throw out the contents. Long-term storage is in a bigger drawer and uses typical alphabetical organization.
