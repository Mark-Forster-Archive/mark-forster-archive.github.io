Procrastination -- slightly off-topic

  • I wonder if anyone has any basic advice regarding staying off the internet when one is resisting an unpleasant task.
    For example. At this very moment, I have 100 pages of student writing which I must read for a class that I am teaching tonight at 6 p.m. Eastcoast US time (about 8 hours from now).
    So far this morning i have done an hour of my own writing, taken care of e-mails, visited this discussion board, checked my Facebook page, backed up my hard-drive, and done some other work for my teaching job.
    but I can't seem to sit down and read this 100 page document -- and I find myself surfing the net and arriving at places so inane that I can scarcely understand how I got there (for example, an hour ago I stumbled upon the birth chart of the actress Chloe Sevigny. I have no idea how I wound up there, or why, having done so, I spent a good twenty minutes reading it (Chloe is a Scorpio, in case you're interested). As we all know, the internet is a vortex for procrastinators, and it's harder than ever when you do much of your work on a computer with high-speed access.
    Any thoughts on this topic?

  • Beth has a good idea. Come up with a plan that sounds agreeable to you. What if you read 13 pages an hour and however much time is left in that hour is for you to surf the web or do whatever you want? You feel obligated to do this, so now you really don't want to. You can also tell yourself that you really don't HAVE to read them. That's true you know! You can tell the class you'll have them next week. You don't have to read them by tonight. Once you've established that fact, you might determine that you want to. Why do you want to now that you don't have to? Do you want to so that you know how your students are doing? Do you want to so you seem like a caring, competent instructor? Do you want to do it today so you can have the rest of the week free of thinking about their papers?

    If all else fails, try reading one just to see if it's as awful as you fear it will be. It most likely won't be. :-)

  • Steve, are you working through your lists? Did you have writing, emails, this discussion board, Facebook, hard-drive backup, etc., on your list and did you do them only when they stood out? It may be that you are not using the system as per the instructions and not doing tasks "when" they stand out. In my experience, even the most resisted tasks will stand out at some point (strangely) and, as our subconscious knows deadlines as well as we do, if we are operating the system we will be unlikely to miss them.

    However, there are times of backlog and looming deadlines and in such cases Bev's suggestion is an excellent one. Personally I find that approach to work better than the reward approach as it (a) helps make the task itself more enjoyable and (b) ensures that we are not distracted by vitue of the fact that we only having that one thing with us to work on.
