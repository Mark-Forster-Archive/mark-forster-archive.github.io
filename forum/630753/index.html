Implementation tips for paper and digital

  • === A simple paper tip ===

    Although I've chosen to play AF digital, here is a straightforward tip to mark active pages I've used in the past :

    a) In many datebooks you can tear the down right corner to open the book right to the current page : this makes an easy tip to open AF notebook to the last page when you want to jot a new thought.

    b) Here my other tip : there is an other corner you can tear : upper right ! Tear it when the page is "closed", ie. all items are either done, either rejected, either dismissed. So when you've come to last page, you cycle back to the first past page still active : the first with upper corner not teared.

    (thought of that when I got my page 2 closed, whilst page 1 still had a dozen active items)

    === My Digital tips ===

    I've choosen to stay digital because of :
    * Cleanliness and order (I am very bad to keep papers clean and in good order in the long run)
    * copy (eventually import from a mail or doc, export ...)
    * search / filter (answer which tasks have I recorded for that person or that project )
    * Tagging (I manage to put keywords like who for, what for, ... ) in the item label to ease reviewes or searches
    * Notes (eventually pasted clip of a paragraph that was the source of my thought, detail not to forget that came to mind during slow pace reading, stored link to a doc or an email, etc)

    I soon dissmissed desktop, laptop and even my Tablet (1), and settled to my old pda (still a Palm), always at hand wherever I am on which I am already trained to write easily. Which app then ?  I soon rejected outliners, Word, Excel or databases, and simply choosed to work with ... memos.

    --- Memos for AF ---

    I found most important to keep the AF "Page" concept, and to mimic a paper notebook :

    * I do not use a memo as an AF page, but as an AF item, in the category "Autofocus".
    * Pages are screen displays of "Autofocus" list of memos (with my font setting it makes 20 items a screen).

    * I put dividers bottom of each screen (a blue dotted line, ie an empty memo which title is a dotted line, set to display in blue). That's how I new my remaining items on page 2 were to be dismissed.

    * I never delete items (neither reorder) , but use color coding (two taps) to mark rejected (yellow pale), done (light gray), dismissed (bright orange). Outstandings are normal black.

    * When an item stands out, I drag a green dotted line (another divider memo) just above it to bookmark the position. Back to pda, when I press memo button, it opens where I left : time to enter what's left to do (new memos are appended at the end of the list) and resume my slow pace reading down the current "page".

    * If an item becomes an exception (urgency), I set it red  and connect it to datebook (alarm). My current rule of thumb is to never have more than 3 exceptions a day, and not to let them live more than the day : if not done, then it should be either rejected (to late !) either restated as a new item (emergency action ?).

    Keep it simple : most of my memos - currently 61 since I started anew with AF - are empty (but the title !). But for about six of them I'm happy to have a place for notes where to add a detail, store an exact wording, insert a due date, or a not before date, etc).

    For now, I can just say I feel at ease with this organisation and AF process. I feel motivated to proceed as is... 
    ... until I bump in one of my procrastinative blocks ?

  • Wowi--

    I have 3 notebooks. One for stuff I can only do at home, one for stuff I can only do at work, and one for stuff I can do at any computer. the "Any" list is the largest; home and work are more static.

    Because home and work tend to be shorter lists, I put them both on the same page (with the dynamic division between them suggested somewhere in the forum)--home at the top, work at the bottom. The Any list starts at the beginning of the notebook and works ahead; the Work/Home lists start at the back of the notebook and work forward. I mark the last page for each list with "page points" (cute little metal bookmarks from Levenger) to make it easy to find when adding new tasks. I use another one on the page I'm currently working on, with the "point" pointing right at the task I'm on. (Paper clips or sticky notes or whatever would work just as well; I just happened to have some of the points lying around.)

    I have about 10 active pages currently, so it's not all that tough to figure out where I am, even without marking it; it's also usually very clear which list I should be focusing on at any moment. (ie, I'm at work: look at the work list! I'm on break at work: look at the Any list.)
