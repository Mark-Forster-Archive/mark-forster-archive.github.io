AF and DIT: Implementing the Will-Do-List into AF

  • Autofocus works like a blast for me when it comes to 'normal' tasks which could be done today, tomorrow or any other day. But every day I have some tasks that have to be done exactly on that day. Some of these have lingered on my todo-list for days or weeks, feeding on my procrastination, but now they have to be dealt with. ;-)

    Every morning I view all the open tasks on my autofocus-list and transfer the tasks, that have to be done today, on a closed will-do-list for today. This small list contains only the few tasks that really have to happen today.

    When it comes to doing something, I first look on my will-do-list. If I find nothing there that fits my current time window I switch to my normal autofocus-list. This makes sure that I miss no urgent tasks on page 8 or 9 while I work on the tasks on page 2 or 3.

    Combining these two methods makes AF the time management system I ever used to manage my daily workload.
