  • My AF list consists of a stack of index cards, one card per action. The advantages are that the cards can hold additional notes, as things evolve, an I never have to rewrite list entries nor do deleted entries mess up the list. And "remote capturing" is extremely easy because blank cards are dumped everywhere around the house. I just have to grab one, write down my note, and take care that it is somehow carried to the list. No rewriting needed.
    In the beginning, I experimented with a portable system, but I learned that the AF list can stay at the desk without problems. The important point is that capturing must be possible at any time and at any place, but for me, the list does not need to be with me all the time.

  • I do almost exactly like David. I handle the list that ends up in my small notebook like an AF list except that the only action is to add the item to the real AF list (Home or Work). Then it gets crossed off.
