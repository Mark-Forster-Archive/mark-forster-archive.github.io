Revised steps 5-8, + step 9

  • The parts of AF which were initially a bit unclear to me were when to move to a new page and when to dismiss an item. Otto Fox's summary in another thread was very helpful (see the fourth reply on http://www.markforster.net/forum/post/692231 ).

    As a result of my own confusion and certain quesions by others on this Forum, I revised Quick Start steps 5-8 and added step 9 (text between / is to be italic):

    ------- Revised version of steps 5-8 -------
    5. Continue going round the same page in the same way /until no item stands out for you/.

    6. When you complete a pass of the page without any item standing out, move onto the next page and repeat the process.

    7. Once you’ve finished with the final page, re-start at the first page which still has items that are not crossed off (this is your first “active” page).

    8. If you ever go to a page and no item stands out for you on your /first/ pass through it, then all the outstanding items on that page are dismissed without re-entering them. Use a highlighter to mark dismissed items. (N.B. This does not apply to the final page, on which you are still writing items.)

    9. The next day you begin work, start the process again at the top of the last page you were working on.
    -------

    The main differences are these:
    a. Step 5 has been simplified to deal only with repeated passes on the same page.

    b. Step 6 deals with moving to the next page, restating the original prohibition of step 5b in a positive way.

    c. Step 7 is the original step 8. It has been reordered here because it is a special case of step 6, i.e. the end of the sequential progression through all pages. It also defines what an "active" page is -- a term left implicit not only in the original steps but indeed in all of http://www.markforster.net/autofocus-system/ .

    d. Step 8 is the original step 7. It has been reordered here because it can apply to any page, i.e. generally to the list as a whole. As such, it seemed disruptive to the sequential relation between original steps 6 and 8. The general condition is expressed by the addition of "ever". Finally, the N.B. has been moved to the end of the paragraph to make it a bit more prominent.

    e. Italics are used for the two key conditions in steps 5 and (new) 8.

    f. Step 9 has been added.
