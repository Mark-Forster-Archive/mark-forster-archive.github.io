  • Retweeting is when you repeat someone else's tweet for the benefit of your own followers.

    Example:
    1) Mark tweets:
    "My new Autofocus system will boost your fun and your productivity. #Autofocus"
    2) I might retweet:
    "RT @AutofocusTM My new Autofocus system will boost your fun and your productivity. #Autofocus <-- try it, it works!"

    (If there were space, I might also add a link to your AF instructions page.)
    (Mark, I strongly suggest you add that page as your "more info URL" on your Twitter profile.)

    A retweet begins w/ RT, then the twittername of the person you're retweeting, then all or part of their tweet, then if you want, add <-- (or such) and your own comment. All in 140 chars -- hence the value of shorter initial tweets!

    Some of the more popular Twitter applications have one-click retweeting, but the native Twitter page does not. (I use TweetDeck, for many reasons, including this.)

    Hope this helps more than it confuses, Mark. There is a learning curve to Twitter but IMO it's worth it for fun and wonderful connections. One blog I highly recommend for Twitter how-to is http://www.twitip.com .
