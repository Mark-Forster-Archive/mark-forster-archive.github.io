  • Mark,

    >>> One thing that's struck me very forcibly about the comments on the Revised Autofocus System is that no one has yet proposed any tweaks. That must be a good thing! <<<

    You've just not heard yet ;-) Actually, I've done a good bit of tweaking but I don't think it serves anyone well to post a lot of tweaks to something so new.

    But since you asked:

    - I test all newly added items: "Will I REALLY do this in the next week?" (If no, I dismiss it right then. (Adding it first allows me to keep the grass catcher attribute of AF. Dismissing it immediately keeps AF clean of clutter.)

    - I replaced the AF1 dismissal rule into the Forward Mode. (I foresaw the infinite loop problem and this prevents it.)

    - I allow immediate "rescue" of dismissed tasks and add them to the END of the list with a ! (I wish I could remember who gave me that idea.) This solves my problem of being forced to dismiss items I don't want to ... yet it should not allow a cop out. We'll see.

    - I keep a 20 minute timer going in both modes and check where I am when it goes off. (I was originally going to switch based on the timer but experience showed me that I was often in the middle of something and so could not switch anyway).

    - My first scan of the day is to pull off high priority items and put them on my MUST DO TODAY list ... that list gets worked first and I only start AF when I can no longer progress there. When I hit a "switch" condition, I first go back to that MUST DO list before I go to the other AF Mode. So my sequence is:

    .- AF Scan
    .- Must Do List
    .- AF Reverse Mode
    .- Must Do List
    .- AF Forward Mode
    .- etc.

    Formalizing how I embed AF into the other parts of my systems has helped considerably.

    I've made some "mechanical" changes which don't affect the rules of AF. I'm still working from a pocket notebook but I think I'll make the backup recording in Excel to monitor statistics a permanent thing. (If I ever get the spreadsheet working the way I want. I did try to collect the statistics manually but after coming up with different task counts every time I counted, I gave up on that idea ;-)

    But all of these tweaks are to accommodate how I work ... and I do work differently than many others so I can really see RAF needing many fewer tweaks over all.
