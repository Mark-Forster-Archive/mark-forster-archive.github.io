AF4 with dismissal?

  • Here's an approach to the AF4 highlighting process that I am thinking about:

    Highlight the items on the Closed List after nothing stands out on a first pass through the Closed List. Draw a line at the end of your Open List -- that is now your new Closed List. All the highlighted items are now "dismissed". Add a task to Review Dismissed Items.


    Basically, the only difference is that, instead of being forced to decide something on them the first time you come to them when you cycle through the Closed List again, you just "put them on review" like you would in AF1, AF2, or AF3.

    It's very AF1-like, it seems to me.

    I'm guessing, Mark, that you already tried this, and maybe you felt that the "on review" method just wasn't effective enough. The new AF4 method certainly does force you to take action.

    But what I liked about the old method is it allows a task to "ripen" some more, and keeps it around in an easily available location so you can find it later if you want.


    I would be quite willing to "dismiss" my Closed List by highlighting it for future review. But since I know that, following AF4 rules, I'll be forced to either act, delete, copy forward, or copy to my Tickler, on every one of those items, I just keep cycling through. Even just copying them all to my Tickler would be painful and time-consuming. And even if I do copy them to my Tickler, they suddenly become harder to find. I really liked AF1 because I could just leave them all in AF and would know exactly where to find them if I ever wanted to.

    As a result, I keep cycling through, cycling through, whittling down that Closed List. Meanwhile my Open List is 15 pages long and growing unmanageable.

  • Hello Seraphim,
    I can see the appeal of your suggestion, and I don't think it would devastate the psychology of AF4, but I do think it would weaken it.
    Yes, the forced decisions in AF4 are hard, but that is part of its power. WHY are you cycling through, cycling through, and never deciding on these things? I was in a similar situation this morning and finally the frustration of my growing 'open list' forces me to dismiss around 20 items over a similar number of pages. Then, when I got to go back to them, I was forced to examine each one carefully KNOWING that I would not get to look at it again. A few I could dimiss entirely, but for meany I finally realised why my eyes had been gliding past them without even seriously considering doing them. Then I reworded it, or broke it down into a few tasks or changed tack on it entirely. and already I can feel myself drawn to them. eg. For a few tasks, I had to write a protocol for a new procedure. Not much fun. But then I reworded it as "Create simple laminated document for XXX". Now I think: "You mean I get to use my laminator! Let me at that document!" (We're silly creatures)
    Sure, after going through my dismissed tasks my new open list was already over a page long, but I want to do some of them: well worth the concentrated focus it took.
    Your suggestion just weakens the tension a little bit between wanting to close the open list and shrink the old closed list. USE that tension for all it's worth. It's one of the key things that stops AF from being a shopping list of to-dos.
