Do you AF2 when you are away?

  • I can't see this problem answered anywhere else but will someone please tell me if it has been? I was forging ahead very nicely with AF2 but now it's academic conference season and I'm on my 2nd week out of 3 conferencing. I'm away from home and many of my tasks just can't be actioned here. If I carry on AFing, I'll have everything dismissed by the end of the conferences. And just the same would apply if you go on holiday.

    So do you AF2 when you are away? Put the whole thing on hold - but I'm not on holiday and would like to continue to do some items on the list?

    If I keep to the rules and have everything dismissed by the time I'm back to normal, that might give a good opportunity to make the first new item 'read through dismissed items' and reinstate what seems to be still good to do. But that doesn't seem to be exactly what Mark envisaged. I'd be grateful for any thoughts.
    22 July 2009 at 16:03 | Lesley Moss
  • Hi Lesley,

    I started AF2 using my active AF1 list on the day AF2 came out and coincidentally went on holiday that day as well.

    Prior to leaving, I went through my list and marked all of the items I could do while I was away and made sure that I had the proper tools / paper / books / files etc. with me on the trip. I didn't dismiss according to the rules on things I couldn't have actioned where I was, just on things I didn't action because I chose not to. I think I only dismissed two or three items in the 9 days.
