An Extension of AF: Context Cards

  • I've been working, with pretty good success, with AF for a couple of weeks now, and I just had a thought which will I think, be useful to me; maybe others will find it helpful as well.

    One really nice feature of GTD is the notion of "context", which allows the grouping of things like shopping lists, errands, agnedas with specific people, "someday"'s and "maybe"'s< etc. Someone pointed out on the forums someplace that it wasn't too useful to put your shopping list into AF as it occurred to you, since you'd have a hard time finding the items. Instead, the items should be kept on a separate shopping list, and an entry "Do the marketing" should be entered into your AF list.

    It occurs to me that, in this instance, "marketing" is just the context for the shopping list, and this idea could be extended in a couple of directions.

    Suppose that, in addition to the AF notebook, one maintains a series of (say) 3x5 card--convenient if you're using a Moleskine size notebook--of "contexts", like "shopping", "errands", "Agenda: Joe", "Someday/Maybe", etc...? In a Moleskine, cards can be kept in the back pocket, alternatively, they could be used as a bookmark--presumably one won't have an enormous stack of these.

    I feel like something along these lines would shore up a weakness in AF which the idea of "contexts" in GTD helps to address--I make a note to put something in the mail for a friend (which I can only do if I go to the post office); I might also need something at the hardware store (which is near the post office). In GTD, I'd put these all in the context of "Errands", so in the case I'd have a card with "Shopping" on one side and "Errands" on the other stuch in my notebook...

    Thoughts?

  • Encouraged by this, I now have 5 x 3 cards in the back folder of my Moleskine (127mm x 210mm, brown paper cover, ruled). There is one for ERRANDS, but I've also added three cards for routine stuff: Daily, Weekly, Monthly.

    As these are scheduled (unless overtaken bu emergency or other context), I don't have to scan my AF lists (Work and Personal, one book), Outlook will remind me to look at the cards, When not working abroad, I work in a home office, so I have forced myself to show separate routine tasks for Work and Personal, but on the same card.

    Example,: on the AF Weekly Routine Card, I have Filing and Clean Desk/Equipment under Work, but under Personal I also have Filing (often do the two together anyway!) along with Check Medication Stock (don't want to run out abroad).

    Works for me.
