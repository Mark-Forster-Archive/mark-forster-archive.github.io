Unorthodox usage of AF+DIT?

  • After a while experimenting with AutoFocus, I'm arrived to a kind of solution, I'd like to introduce and get some remarks and/or suggestions from you all.

    Firstly: I'm IS Project Director BUT I use paper a lot (I don't know why, but when writing a task on a paper sheet, I feel more 'commited' to do rather tahtn on an Excel or OutLook like solution. Same for MindMapping: I love the concept and I strongly prefer to do mindmap on on brown paper (witk stick'its) or on a whiteboard, rather than within a tool.
    I guess what I need is something like an 'overview of everything', when thinking, and the PC screens are too small for that...
    For the same reasons, I don't like when working on AF list to forget an 'day' event (appointment or just a 'todo this day'). Probably a kind of rest of culture about 'if you say you'll be there or do that for this slot, do it' (I'm quite commit oriented.)

    Now, "my" solution:
    1. I decided to use only one book. I really don't like to separate in different places what I'm supposed to do.
    2. I decided therefore my "A day a page" calendar (13x20cm).
    3. For each day: I divide vertically the page
    3.1 left if for appointments
    3.2 right is for AF (and I can have up to 29 tasks) and tasks to be done on this date only
    4. when déciding what to do next:
    4.1 I look firstly on the agenda part of the day (I might have a meeting or a booked slot for a 'meeting with myself' on something specific for that day)
    4.2 if no 'appointment', then I use the AF section of the calendar, exactly the way described in AutoFocus pilot of our friend Mark.
    4.3 it means I have a mark for the current day (on my calendar, the right bottom corner is easily 'tearable', as there are little holes dedicated for that; I use the right top corner to go quickly to the last page with on open task (I mean not dismissed not done); I use a piece of paper to know where I am on AF list.
    E.g.: today, my "not completed or dimissed' page of AF is on page of the day Tuesday 20th of January

    Working that way, it is very rare I need to go to future dates (excepted for 'on that date' todos).
    The fact I have something 'todo' on past pages (past dates) gives me a feeling of: guy, you need to do something there! (And then, I applied the 'little and often' principles'.

    That's it. I works perfectly so far, it's very easy to handle.
    Your comments and/or suggestions are very welcome!

    Thanks.
    Pascal

  • Hi Pascal,

    My mind works a lot like yours. I like paper because human vision is wider than any display that I can currently afford ;-) I mind map a lot and do it by hand ... I agree with Tony Buzan that the eye hand coordination stimulates thinking and drawing symbols and pictures is a part of the process. There is a place for computer mind maps, and I use those as well, but more often after I have finished the creative part and want to tidy things up to send elsewhere.

    As to your AF system, it sounds well thought out and I can see where it would work. But realize that there is an underlying assumption in that system: that the size of the AF list grows in a way that does not put it too far out of reach of your calendar. IOW, the AF list could grow too large and you might run out of the range of the book after a time. Also, at year end, you might end up hauling around that calendar for a while -- but that is true of any book, I suppose.

    But otherwise, I love the creativity of that integration. Nice.
