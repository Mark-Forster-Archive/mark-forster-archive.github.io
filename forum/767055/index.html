  • Perhaps the questions posted here (in this forum) could drive it?

    - What does it mean to "dismiss" an item?"
    - Do I lose dismissed items forever?
    - What page do I start on tomorrow morning ... page 1 ... the page I was on ... the next page after that? And what line item do I start on?
    - I'm afraid I'll miss something buried on my list. What should I do?
    - How should I handle completed items? Should I cross off, check off, highlight, etc.?
    - What are the pros and cons of paper vs digital?

    I'm with you that it should not be links to discussions. People go to FAQs to see the answers, not chase links and read. Unfortunately it will require someone to read those discussions and answer the question in a fair and balanced way.

    I also believe that a resources section would be helpful. In that I would put links to journals, pens, whatever people have suggested that would be helpful.

    I'm wondering if there should be a separate section on motivation. It seems to be a big topic and yet it is not really part of the system, per se. I'm not even sure what you'd put in that section as it is so much outside of AF. OTOH, much of the discussion on this forum is about motivation, procrastination, etc.

    Yet another outlier is the whole area of DIT, and CI, etc. There is lots of discussion of the role of those ideas to augment AF, or somehow work within it.

    Finally, I don't know if it pays to have a separate "tweaks" section. I suppose many of the tweaks would be naturally covered in the questions about "how do I ...?", but maybe some would not. I guess the problem there is if you outline the official AF rules and include the tweaks it would look something like:

    - Put all of your tasks on the list ... except those which you might put on an index card

    - Start at the first page ... except if you start at the last page

    - Work forward ... unless you work backward or jump all around

    - Keep working items on one page until you are done ... unless you do only one item per page

    - Dismiss items when you can't do at least one ... unless you dismiss them early ... or unless you decide to keep them hanging around

    - An item is done if you work on it ... unless you just think about working on it ;-)

    It might be distracting to dump all of the tweaks on a person just starting out ... OTOH, it might be useful to present them in context with many cautions about trying it as written for at least a month before trying any tweaks.

    Just some random thoughts this morning ;-)
