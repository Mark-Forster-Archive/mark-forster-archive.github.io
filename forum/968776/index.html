  • Hi Eugene,

    There are several alternatives to using two notebooks.

    For example:
    - Use the front of the book for one location. Flip the book over and use the back of the book for the other location. Works well with graph paper notebooks.

    - Use different colored pens for different locations (makes me dizzy personally, but might work for someone)

    - Use left pages for one location, and right pages for another location

    - Use the stalagmite / stalactite method (tasks for one location you write on the page from the top down, tasks for the other location you write on the page from bottom up, and let them meet in the middle). Works great for AF1. For AF4, it can get a little confusing exactly how to "draw the line" separating the Closed and Open Lists. For me, I just started a new page and that solved the problem.

    - If your locations aren't clearly distinct (e.g., sometimes working from home, sometimes in the office, so work and home are not always distinct), you could try mixing everything in one list. This works great for me, but at times it can be distracting.
