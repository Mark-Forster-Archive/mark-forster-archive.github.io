  • Avrum,

    No worries. I am not a published author like Mark or David Allen. I am in the investment management business and have over the years read, tried and used pretty much all of the systems out there. My system is developed based upon what I think works and provides the most return on your investment, which is in my opinion a simple, portable, paper based system. It was written on a variety of plane rides over the last year or so.

    Part of my thinking is that TM requires an investment of time and like any other investment it should produce a return on investment. I don't want to bag on GTD which I used, but for me the return was negative. In other words, more time invested than saved using it. I also am not a fan of the idea that a TM system takes two years to master. So, I took what worked for me and put it into two simple paper based systems, that I have used and have provided me a positive return on the time invested to set up and use them, which is like AF not a big time investment to get going.

    At this point, I give the first ebook away and sell the second for a nominal charge, it is not how I primarily make my living, but hope I am adding something to the productivity discussion. My other pet peeve is systems that are not supported and books that are teasers to buy other products, so I try to be available to answer questions.

    But of course with Mark, you get the best of all worlds, free productivity systems from a known expert and free support via the forum. That's a deal you can't beat.
