How to start with DWM?

  • Okay, I'm willing to start the experiment, but I'm a little confused when it comes to migrating all my tasks to the new system.
    I have here an old "closed" AF4 list that still has tons of stuff in it, plus a more recent DIT backlog. How do I best transfer them to DWM? I have seen some ideas mentioned in several posts, but if I haven't missed something (couldn't read everything that was written since the initial announcement), there is no 'approved' method to switch yet.

    From the instructions, one should probably dump all tasks into the +1 month date (March 4), but this doesn't seem sensible to me. The thought of having a large part of them expire when that day arrives, scares me. OTOH, the time period between today and March 4 would contain all my tasks anyway, and scattering them between those two dates wouldn't change anything. I would still have to do some work on them, or let them expire.

    Or should one start a completely new list, and just abandon all the old stuff?

    And what about the AF advice to keep separate notebooks for work and home? For me, it still seems to make sense, and I'm planning to keep them separate.

    Another approach would be to keep te AF list going, but reenter unfinished tasks on the 7 day list. This should whittle down the AF list quite quickly to the hard core of things which aren't actually getting your attention (i.e. you should go through the open list very quickly, so more of your time is spent in the closed list, so you quickly get to the stage where you have to dismiss the closed and close the open. Then you can't get off the closed list until it is done.)

    This would trap you on the old tasks until they are complete, dismissed or moved to the new list. You might want to set aside time for the AF list every day as a current initiative and increasingly use the DWM list as the main one. It's really just a slightly complicated way of declaring a backlog.

  • There are two "approved" ways of starting the list:

    1. (Recommended). Start from scratch and add tasks as you think of them or as they come up in the normal course of events. That's the way I started off with DWM and it has worked just fine.

    2. Add everything from your old list (weeding as necessary) in at the 1-month point. If that fills the page, then continue adding them to the next page. This can be done in stages if you prefer (as Rainer suggests).

    Note that in both the above approaches, all tasks go in at the 1-month point.
